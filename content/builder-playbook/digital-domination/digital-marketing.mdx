# Digital Domination: Building Your Online Presence

## The Reality of Digital Marketing in Solar

Traditional door-to-door knocking will always be your primary lead source. Nothing beats human-to-human connection.

But digital presence matters because:
1. **Social proof** - People check you out online before meeting you
2. **Lead generation** - Some leads come through digital channels
3. **Team credibility** - Great social media builds team reputation
4. **Local authority** - Digital presence builds neighborhood awareness
5. **Referral amplification** - Happy customers tag you, multiply your reach

You don't need to be an Instagram influencer. But you should be easy to find online and look professional.

---

## The Digital Presence Checklist

### 1. Google Reviews (Most Important)

**Why it matters:**
- First place people look
- Affects Google search rankings
- Builds social proof
- Can kill leads if reviews are bad

**What to do:**
- Ask happy customers to leave Google reviews
- Respond to all reviews (good and bad) professionally
- Aim for 4.5+ stars overall
- Get at least 20+ reviews
- Highlight recent positive reviews on social

**Word track to ask for review:**
> "Hey, I'd love your help. Could you leave a quick Google review about your experience? Just search '[KIN] on Google' and leave a star rating and a quick comment. It really helps."

---

### 2. Before/After Installation Photos

**Why it matters:**
- Visual proof of quality
- Shows progression of work
- Builds confidence for prospects
- Great for social media

**What to collect:**
- Before: Existing roof (clear, good angle)
- During: Installation in progress (interesting angles)
- After: Finished system with smart meter, monitoring app
- System producing (day one production numbers)

**How to use them:**
- Post on social media (Instagram, Facebook)
- Include in email signatures
- Share with prospects in consultations
- Create before/after carousel posts

**Pro tip:** Get customer permission before publishing photos. Most are happy - it's free advertising for them too.

---

### 3. Facebook Business Page

**Why it matters:**
- Most local customers are on Facebook
- Easy to manage
- Can run targeted ads
- Shows company legitimacy

**What to include:**
- Company info (address, phone, website)
- Professional logo
- Before/after photos
- Customer testimonials
- Regular posts (2-3x per week)
- Team photos
- Local event participation
- Community involvement

**Content ideas:**
- "Solar Saturdays" - weekly tip posts
- Customer spotlights - feature happy customers
- Educational content - how solar works
- Team updates - celebrate team wins
- Neighborhood focus - photos from local installs
- Seasonal content - "time to save before rates increase"

---

### 4. Instagram Presence

**Why it matters:**
- Visual platform (perfect for before/afters)
- Reaches younger demographic
- Good for brand building
- Easy to reach local audience with hashtags

**What to post:**
- Before/after installation photos
- Team photos
- System production updates
- Customer testimonials
- Behind-the-scenes content
- Local neighborhood highlights
- Educational graphics
- Celebrations of team wins

**Hashtag strategy:**
- Local hashtags: #CarlsbadSolar #SanDiegoSolar #[YourCity]Solar
- Industry hashtags: #SolarEnergy #GoSolar #RenewableEnergy
- Engagement hashtags: #SolarSavings #CleanEnergy #EnergyIndependence

**Content cadence:** 3-4 posts per week minimum.

---

### 5. Customer Testimonials (Video > Text)

**Why it matters:**
- Video testimonials are 10x more powerful than written
- People trust people, not companies
- Overcoming objections with customer voices
- Share-worthy on social media

**How to collect:**
- After system goes live, ask for testimonial
- Keep it simple: "How has solar changed your life?"
- Phone video is fine (don't need professional production)
- 30-60 seconds is perfect
- Get permission to share

**Content ideas for testimonials:**
- "What's your favorite part?"
- "How much are you saving?"
- "Would you recommend KIN?"
- "What was the experience like?"
- "What surprised you?"

**How to use them:**
- Post on Facebook and Instagram
- Share in email to prospects
- Play during team meetings
- Use in sales presentations
- Highlight on website

---

### 6. Email Newsletter (Optional but Powerful)

**Why it matters:**
- Stays in touch with past contacts
- Educational content builds authority
- Seasonal content builds urgency
- Can drive referrals

**Frequency:** Once per month

**Content ideas:**
- "SDGE rates are up X% - lock in savings now"
- "Winter solar production myths"
- "Customer spotlight" - feature happy customer
- "Team spotlight" - introduce team member
- "Solar education" - how-to content
- "Promotion" - limited-time offers

**Tool:** Use free tier of Mailchimp or similar

## The Digital Funnel: From Awareness to Appointment

### Awareness Stage (Top of Funnel)
- Facebook ads to neighborhood (solar system + savings)
- Instagram posts with local hashtags
- Google reviews and search presence
- Word-of-mouth spreading online (tags, shares)

### Interest Stage (Middle)
- Website or landing page with system details
- FAQ addressing common objections
- Before/after galleries
- Customer testimonial videos

### Decision Stage (Bottom)
- Google reviews for social proof
- Email or messenger to provide quote
- Case study (how much customer is saving)
- Clear CTA to book appointment

---

## Social Media Best Practices

### Do:
- Post consistently (schedule if needed)
- Engage with followers (respond to comments)
- Use local hashtags
- Feature your team (humanize brand)
- Celebrate customer wins
- Share educational content
- Be authentic
- Post quality over quantity

### Don't:
- Post politics or divisive content
- Ignore negative comments (respond professionally)
- Copy competitor content
- Post low-quality photos
- Spam your followers
- Make false claims
- Overpromise results
- Disappear for months

---

## Leveraging Team Members

Every team member should be a brand ambassador:

**Ask your team to:**
- Share company posts on personal Facebook
- Tag KIN in their stories when knocking doors
- Share customer testimonials from their closures
- Post about their wins (appointment set, deal closed)
- Use branded hashtags in personal posts

**This amplifies reach:**
- 10 team members sharing = 10x reach
- Their friends are more likely to engage
- Personal shares get higher engagement than company posts
- Creates culture of team pride

---

## Measuring Success

### Metrics to track:
- Google review count and rating
- Facebook engagement (likes, comments, shares)
- Website traffic (if you have website)
- Lead source (how many from digital vs. door knock)
- Cost per lead (if running ads)

### Monthly review:
- How many Google reviews did we get? (Goal: 2-3/month)
- What post had highest engagement?
- Did we get leads from digital channels?
- What content resonated most?
- What should we adjust?

## Budget for Digital (Optional)

If you want to run paid ads (not required):

**Monthly budget:** $300-500/month for Facebook/Instagram ads targeting local neighborhoods

**Ad strategy:**
- Target homeowners in your service area
- Show before/after photos
- Lead with savings ("Save $1,800/year")
- Clear CTA ("Call for free quote")
- A/B test different messages
- Track ROI carefully

## The Reality Check

Digital marketing is supporting, not primary. You'll still get most of your leads from:
- Door knocking (60-70%)
- Referrals (20-30%)
- Digital (5-10%)

But that 5-10% from digital can be 20-30 extra appointments per month if you do it right.

Digital presence also makes you look credible and professional. When prospects Google you and see great reviews, active social media, and professional presence, they're more likely to say yes.

## The Digital Domination Challenge

**30-day challenge:**

**Week 1:**
- Set up Facebook business page
- Collect 5 customer testimonial videos
- Take 20 before/after photos
- Set up Instagram account
- Get 10 Google reviews

**Week 2:**
- Post twice daily on Facebook
- Post 4x on Instagram
- Feature customer testimonial video on all platforms
- Respond to all comments and reviews
- Invite 20 customers to leave Google reviews

**Week 3:**
- Continue daily posting
- Launch email list (collect 30 emails from past contacts)
- Create one customer case study
- Get team members to start sharing

**Week 4:**
- Maintain momentum
- Measure results
- Double down on what's working
- Plan month 2 content

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Google reviews are critical** - Ask customers for them
2. **Visual content wins** - Before/after photos, testimonials, team photos
3. **Consistency beats perfection** - Regular posting > perfect posting
4. **Engagement matters** - Respond to comments and build community
5. **Testimonials are powerful** - Video testimonials are 10x more powerful
6. **Your team amplifies** - Get team members sharing
7. **Digital is supporting** - Door knocking is still primary
8. **Measure and adjust** - Track what works, do more of it

You don't need to be a digital marketing expert. Just be consistent, authentic, and focused on showing great work and happy customers.

That's digital domination in solar sales.
