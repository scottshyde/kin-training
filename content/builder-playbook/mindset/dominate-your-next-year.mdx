# Dominate Your Next Year

Once a year, carve out two to eight hours — alone, no phone, somewhere you can think. This is your annual review. It's the highest-leverage planning session of the year and most people never do it.

<Accordion title="Part 1: The Annual Evaluation">
Before you can plan what's next, you have to be honest about what was. These prompts are not rhetorical. Write actual answers.

**What were your biggest achievements this year?**
Be specific. Not "I did well" but what, exactly, happened that you're proud of.

**What were the highlights — moments you want to remember?**
These anchor the year to something real. The business result and the life moment are both worth naming.

**What did you learn?**
About yourself, about the industry, about how to sell, how to lead, how to live.

**Who did you help, and how?**
Sales is service. Who's better off because you were in the field this year?

**What failure taught you the most?**
This is often the richest question. The failure that stung — what did it actually teach you if you let it?

**Where did you waste time?**
Honest answer. Not where you felt busy — where did time go that didn't produce anything real?
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Part 2: Setting the Next Year">
**Income range goal:**
Set a range, not just a floor. "I want to make $180,000-$220,000" is better than "at least $150,000." The range gives you a stretch and a floor.

**Closed deal goal:**
Derive this from your income goal using the reverse-engineer formula. Make it specific.

**Professional goals:**
What skill, credential, or capability do you want to develop? What would make you more effective in the field or in a leadership role?

**Personal goals:**
Health, fitness, learning, hobbies. These aren't separate from your professional performance — they're the foundation of it.

**Family goals:**
What do you want your relationships to look like this year? What does the person you love most need from you that you haven't been giving?

**Recruiting goals:**
How many A-players do you want to bring into the organization?

**Travel:**
Where? When? Make it real enough to save for.

**Skills to develop:**
One or two non-sales skills that make you more capable as a whole person.

**Habits to build or break:**
Be specific. "Be healthier" isn't a habit. "Lift four days a week" is.

**Lifetime goal check:**
Pull out your DMP. Are your one-year goals connected to your lifetime vision? If not, recalibrate.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Purpose of the Exercise">
Most people plan in 30-second bursts — a thought in the shower, a note on their phone. This process is different. It's deliberate, extended, and written.

The act of writing forces clarity. The act of reviewing forces honesty. The combination produces direction — which is the thing most people lack, not talent or ambition.

> Do this once a year. Review it quarterly. Touch it monthly. Live it daily.
</Accordion>
