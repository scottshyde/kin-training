# Dominate Your Next Year

<Lead>Once a year, carve out two to eight hours — alone, no phone, somewhere you can think. This is your annual review. It's the highest-leverage planning session of the year and most people never do it.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Part 1: The Annual Evaluation">

<Callout type="important" title="BE HONEST">
Before you can plan what's next, you have to be honest about what was. These prompts are not rhetorical. **Write actual answers.**
</Callout>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Biggest Achievements">
Be specific. Not "I did well" but what, exactly, happened that you're proud of.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Highlights — Moments to Remember">
The business result and the life moment are both worth naming.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="What Did You Learn?">
About yourself, the industry, how to sell, how to lead, how to live.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Who Did You Help?">
Sales is service. Who's better off because you were in the field this year?
</Step>
<Step number={5} title="What Failure Taught You the Most?">
The failure that stung — what did it actually teach you if you let it?
</Step>
<Step number={6} title="Where Did You Waste Time?">
Not where you felt busy — where did time go that didn't produce anything real?
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Part 2: Setting the Next Year">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Income Range Goal">
Set a range, not just a floor. "$180,000-$220,000" is better than "at least $150,000." The range gives you a stretch and a floor.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Closed Deal Goal">
Derive from your income goal using the reverse-engineer formula. Make it specific.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Professional Goals">
What skill, credential, or capability do you want to develop?
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Personal Goals">
Health, fitness, learning, hobbies — these aren't separate from professional performance, they're the foundation.
</Step>
<Step number={5} title="Family Goals">
What does the person you love most need from you that you haven't been giving?
</Step>
<Step number={6} title="Recruiting Goals">
How many A-players do you want to bring into the organization?
</Step>
<Step number={7} title="Habits to Build or Break">
Be specific. "Be healthier" isn't a habit. **"Lift four days a week"** is.
</Step>
<Step number={8} title="Lifetime Goal Check">
Pull out your DMP. Are your one-year goals connected to your lifetime vision? If not, recalibrate.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Purpose of the Exercise">

<Callout type="insight">
Most people plan in 30-second bursts — a thought in the shower, a note on their phone. This process is different. It's deliberate, extended, and written.
</Callout>

<KeyTerm term="Why It Works">The act of writing forces clarity. The act of reviewing forces honesty. The combination produces direction — which is the thing most people lack, not talent or ambition.</KeyTerm>

<PullQuote>"Do this once a year. Review it quarterly. Touch it monthly. Live it daily."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>
