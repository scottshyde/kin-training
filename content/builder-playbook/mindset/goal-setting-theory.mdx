# Goal Setting Theory

<Lead>"Master prospector beats master closer." The most sophisticated close in solar sales is useless if there's no one to close. Volume is the foundation. And volume starts with a number — your income goal — reverse-engineered all the way down to how many doors you knock today. This isn't motivational. It's math.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Reverse-Engineer Formula — Closers">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "$4,000", label: "Average Per Deal" },
  { value: "50", label: "Deals for $200K" },
  { value: "60", label: "Doors Per Day" }

| Step | Formula | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Annual income goal | (your number) | $200,000 |
| Deals needed | Income ÷ $4,000 | 50 deals |
| Sits needed | Deals × 3 | 150 sits |
| BAs needed | Sits ÷ 0.6 | 250 BAs |
| DMs needed | BAs × 10 | 2,500 DMs |
| Doors needed | DMs × 6 | 15,000 doors |
| Daily doors (250 working days) | 15,000 ÷ 250 | **60 doors/day** |

<KeyTerm term="For $300K/Year">Roughly 90 doors/day.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What the Numbers Tell You">

<Callout type="insight" title="LIBERATING, NOT DISCOURAGING">
The formula doesn't lie. If you're hitting 30 doors a day and wondering why your pipeline feels thin, the numbers are telling you exactly what's happening.
</Callout>

<KeyTerm term="The Principle">Doors are entirely within your control. You can't control who answers. You can't control the weather or the territory. But you can control how many doors you knock. And the formula tells you exactly how many that needs to be.</KeyTerm>

<PullQuote>"Control your input. Let the math handle your output."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Russell Ballard Principle">

<PullQuote>"It's a marvelous work and a wonder, but it will not move forward of itself."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Applied to Sales">The system works. KIN's process works. Griffin Hill works. But none of it moves without you initiating. Doors don't knock themselves. The rep who understands this stops waiting for a good territory, a good lead, a good day. They knock.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Using the Formula">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Sunday Planning">
Set the week's door target based on your income goal.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Daily Schedule">
Build your day backward from the door number.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Weekly Evaluation">
How many doors? How many DMs? How many BAs? Are your ratios where they should be?
</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="insight">
The formula makes underperformance **visible and specific** — which means it makes improvement visible and specific too.
</Callout>

</Accordion>
