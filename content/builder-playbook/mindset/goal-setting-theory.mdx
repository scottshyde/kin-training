# Goal Setting Theory

> *"Master prospector beats master closer."*

The most sophisticated close in solar sales is useless if there's no one to close. Volume is the foundation. And volume starts with a number — your income goal — reverse-engineered all the way down to how many doors you knock today.

This isn't motivational. It's math.

---

## The Reverse-Engineer Formula

Start with what you want to make. Work backward to what you have to do.

### For Closers ($4,000 per deal average)

| Step | Formula | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Annual income goal | (your number) | $200,000 |
| Deals needed | Income ÷ $4,000 | 50 deals |
| Sits needed | Deals × 3 | 150 sits |
| BAs needed | Sits ÷ 0.6 | 250 BAs |
| DMs needed | BAs × 10 | 2,500 DMs |
| Doors needed | DMs × 6 | 15,000 doors |
| Daily doors (250 working days) | 15,000 ÷ 250 | **60 doors/day** |

For $300,000/year: roughly **90 doors/day**.

### For Setters ($2,000 per deal average)

At a $2k/deal average, the same formula requires roughly double the door count to hit comparable income:

| Annual Income | Daily Doors Needed |
|--------------|-------------------|
| $100,000 | ~60 doors/day |
| $150,000 | ~90 doors/day |

---

## What the Numbers Tell You

The formula doesn't lie. If you're hitting 30 doors a day and wondering why your pipeline feels thin, the numbers are telling you exactly what's happening.

This is liberating, not discouraging — because doors are entirely within your control. You can't control who answers. You can't control the weather or the territory. But you can control how many doors you knock. And the formula tells you exactly how many that needs to be.

> **Control your input. Let the math handle your output.**

---

## The Russell Ballard Principle

> *"It's a marvelous work and a wonder, but it will not move forward of itself."*

Applied to sales: the system works. KIN's process works. Griffin Hill works. But none of it moves without you initiating. Doors don't knock themselves.

The rep who understands this stops waiting for a good territory, a good lead, a good day. They knock.

---

## Using the Formula

Sit down each Sunday and set the week's door target based on your income goal. Build your daily schedule backward from that number.

If your goal is $150,000 this year and you have 250 working days, you need 60 productive doors every workday. Plan your route, your times, and your energy around hitting that number.

Weekly evaluation: how many doors did you knock? How many DMs did you get? How many BAs? Are your ratios where they should be?

The formula makes underperformance visible and specific — which means it makes improvement visible and specific too.
