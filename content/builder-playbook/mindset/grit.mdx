<Lead>Dr. Craig Manning spent years studying what actually predicts long-term success — not in a lab, but with elite performers across sports, sales, and high-performance fields. His findings are counterintuitive and worth understanding deeply.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="What Determination Actually Is">

<Callout type="insight">
Determination isn't willpower in the moment. It's the **accumulated habit** of doing hard things consistently, even when motivation is absent.
</Callout>

The rep who knocks when it's hot, books when they're tired, and shows up on Saturday when every instinct says don't — that rep is building determination the same way a weightlifter builds muscle. **Through resistance.**

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Six Steps to Strengthen Willpower">

<Callout type="important" title="DISCIPLINES, NOT SUGGESTIONS">
These directly affect your mental capacity to persist.
</Callout>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Get Healthy">
Your body is the hardware your mindset runs on. Chronic fatigue, poor nutrition, and physical inactivity all reduce mental durability. You cannot have elite willpower in a depleted body.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Sleep 7.5-9 Hours">
Sleep debt directly impairs decision-making, emotional regulation, and persistence. The rep who runs on 5 hours is playing with a handicap they can't see.
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Eat 5 Fruits and Vegetables Daily">
Blood sugar stability affects mood and decision quality throughout the day. The afternoon energy crash is often a nutrition issue, not a motivation issue.
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Move">
Physical movement — especially in the morning — primes the brain for focus and stress tolerance. A workout before a door shift changes what you're capable of at 6pm.
</Step>

<Step number={5} title="Practice Gratitude">
Gratitude actively interrupts the negativity bias that makes doors feel heavier than they are. Morning gratitude practice shifts your baseline emotional state.
</Step>

<Step number={6} title="Think Intentionally (DMP Work)">
Intentional thought about who you're becoming and what you're building creates internal alignment that sustains effort when external motivation fails.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Identify and Leverage Your Strengths">

<KeyTerm term="Deploy What Works">You have things you're already good at. Know what they are, name them explicitly, and find ways to deploy them more often. Performance in your zones of strength builds confidence that carries into harder terrain.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Identify and Neutralize Your Weaknesses">

<Callout type="warning">
Weaknesses don't disappear by ignoring them — they **ambush** you. Name yours. Build a system that minimizes their impact.
</Callout>

If you're bad at mornings, structure accountability. If you're inconsistent with follow-up, build a forcing function.

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Surround Yourself with the Right People">

<KeyTerm term="The Multiplier">This is the one step that multiplies all the others. Your environment shapes your standard. The people you spend the most time with set the floor for your effort, your attitude, and your expectation of what's possible.</KeyTerm>

Choose accordingly.

</Accordion>
