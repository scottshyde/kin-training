# Getting the SDGE Bill: The Essential First Step

The SDGE bill is the single most important document in a solar appointment. A proposal without the bill is theoretical. A proposal with the bill is personal and powerful.

<Accordion title="Why the Bill Matters More Than You Think">
The SDGE bill is the key to:

1. **Proving the need** - Shows their current energy cost
2. **Building credibility** - You can show exact numbers, not estimates
3. **Creating urgency** - Seeing $150/month in black and white hits different
4. **Calculating accurate quotes** - Can't design a system without knowing usage
5. **Showing savings** - Can demonstrate exact ROI based on their bill
6. **Building confidence** - Concrete numbers = more confidence in solar
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Challenge: Getting the Bill">
Here's the reality: Most customers don't have their bill handy. Some don't even know where it is. Your job is to make it easy to get it without friction.

**Possible locations:**
- Physical copy in a drawer (rare - maybe 20% of people)
- Email inbox (more common - maybe 40% of people)
- SDGE online account (most reliable - maybe 80% of people have access)
- Completely lost (surprisingly common - maybe 10% of people)

Your approach needs to handle all these scenarios smoothly.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Pre-Appointment Request">
This starts BEFORE the appointment. When you're setting up the appointment (or knocking on the door setting up the appointment), mention the bill:

**Word Track:**
> "Hey, one thing that'll make our appointment way more efficient - can you pull your most recent SDGE bill? I'm going to review it beforehand so when I sit down with you, I can show you exactly how much you're spending and how solar changes that number.
>
> Do you have it handy, or will you need to go dig it out?"

**Why this approach:**
- Frames it as "making things efficient" (they want that)
- Explains why you need it (they understand the value)
- Gives them a choice (dig it out now or find it before appointment)
- Normalizes it (sounds routine)

**Their likely responses:**
- "Yeah, I have it" - Perfect, take a photo
- "I'll find it before you come" - Great, they'll look for it
- "I don't have a copy" - You'll handle this at appointment
- "It's in my email somewhere" - They'll search or you can help at appointment
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Getting the Bill During Appointment">
If they don't have it ready when you arrive, here's your systematic approach:

### Approach 1: The SDGE App (Best Option)

**Word Track:**
> "No problem at all - it's super easy. We can just pull it up on your phone. Do you have the SDGE app?"

**If they say yes:**
> "Perfect. Just open it up and show me your last month's bill. [Look at it together]. OK, so you're using about [usage] kWh per month and paying about $[amount]. Got it."

**If they're not sure:**
> "It's the app on your phone - if you search 'SDGE' you should find it. Or we can open a web browser and log into their website. Let me help you."

**Advantages:**
- Digital = always available if they have account access
- Easy to take screenshot
- Shows current and historical usage
- Most reliable

### Approach 2: Email (Second Best)

**Word Track:**
> "Do you get email bills from SDGE? Let me help you search for it. Open your email and search for 'sdge' or 'bill'. It should come up."

[Help them search if needed]

> "Great - here it is. [Look together]. Can you screenshot this or forward it to me?"

**Advantages:**
- Bill is right there in their email
- Easy to screenshot or forward
- Quick process

### Approach 3: Physical Mail (Works but Slower)

**Word Track:**
> "Your bill usually comes in the mail. Do you get mail today, or do you have the last one lying around somewhere? Let me help you look."

[Help them search - usually kitchen drawers, desk, etc.]

> "Here it is. [Look at it together]. Let me just take a picture of this so I have it for reference."

[Take photo with your phone or theirs]

**Advantages:**
- Physical copy is concrete
- They feel good having it
- You have the exact bill
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Most Difficult Scenario: No Bill Available">
Sometimes you'll encounter a customer who genuinely doesn't have a bill available:

**Reasons:**
- Just moved in
- Bills go to spouse/business address
- Online account not set up
- Email deleted
- Never paid attention to billing

**Your Response - Part 1: Don't Panic**

You can actually work without the bill, but it's suboptimal. Here's how to handle it:

**Word Track:**
> "That's OK - it happens. Here's what we can do: Let me ask you a couple questions about your bill, and we can estimate your usage. It'll be close, and then once you find the actual bill, we'll refine the numbers.
>
> About how much do you pay SDGE each month on average?"

**Then ask:**
- Summer bill (highest)
- Winter bill (lowest)
- Average
- KWh usage (if they know it)
- Time living in home (affects estimates)

**Your Response - Part 2: Get It Before the Next Meeting**

> "Here's what we'll do: I'm going to send you a link to SDGE's website so you can create an online account [or access if it exists]. Once you pull your bill, text it to me [give phone number] and I'll refine all these numbers.
>
> By the time I come back for the design review, we'll have exact numbers and I'll show you exactly how solar works for your situation. Sound good?"

**This approach:**
- Doesn't block the appointment
- Gets them to pull their bill before next visit
- Shows you're organized and thorough
- Gives them an action item (increases commitment)
- Refines numbers before contract
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What to Look For on the Bill">
When you have the bill, here's what you're extracting:

### The Critical Numbers

**1. Monthly Usage (kWh)**
- Location on bill: Usually middle or bottom right
- What it means: How much electricity they use per month
- Why it matters: Determines system size
- Look for: kWh usage, not dollars

**2. Monthly Cost ($)**
- Location on bill: The big number they see first
- What it means: What they're paying SDGE
- Why it matters: Baseline for savings calculation
- Look for: Total amount due (often at top of bill)

**3. Rate per kWh**
- Location on bill: Usually in the middle section, sometimes multiple rates
- What it means: Price per unit of electricity
- Why it matters: Changes with time of use, season, consumption
- Look for: Sometimes listed as $/kWh or different rates (peak, off-peak, super-peak)

**4. Usage Pattern (Seasonal)**
- Location on bill: 12-month history (often on back or detailed view)
- What it matters: Summer = high, Winter = variable (SD mild)
- Why it matters: System needs to cover peak months
- Look for: Which month was highest usage?

### Secondary Information

**5. Bill date and period**
- Why: Confirm this is recent (not years old)
- Look for: "Bill period" usually at top

**6. Customer name and address**
- Why: Confirm it matches who you're talking to
- Look for: Should match home address

**7. Account number**
- Why: Used for SDGE coordination later
- Look for: Usually near customer name
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What NOT to Do">
### Mistake 1: Pressure Them for It
**Wrong:** "I need the bill to move forward. Can't do anything without it."

**Right:** "The bill helps me be more accurate. But if you don't have it, we can estimate and refine later."

### Mistake 2: Act Annoyed They Don't Have It
**Wrong:** [Sigh] "OK, well, this is going to be harder..."

**Right:** [Smile] "No worries - happens all the time. Let's just estimate."

### Mistake 3: Take It and Not Return It
**Wrong:** Borrow the bill and forget to give it back

**Right:** Take a photo/screenshot and give the original back immediately

### Mistake 4: Take Multiple Photos
**Wrong:** [Takes 5 photos of the same bill from different angles]

**Right:** Take ONE clear photo showing the key information

### Mistake 5: Get Frustrated with Their Ignorance
**Wrong:** Act annoyed that they don't know their bill amount

**Right:** "Most people don't track this closely - that's why we help!"
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Script for Smooth Bill Extraction">
Here's the complete word track from asking to analyzing:

> **YOU:** "One thing that'll really help me - do you have your SDGE bill handy?"
>
> **THEM:** [Varies]
>
> **YOU:** [If they have it] "Perfect, let me take a look at that for a second. [Study it] OK, so you're using about [X] kWh per month and paying about $[Y] per month. Is that about right for a typical month?"
>
> **THEM:** [Confirms]
>
> **YOU:** "And is summer higher than winter, or pretty consistent?"
>
> **THEM:** [Answers]
>
> **YOU:** [If they don't have it] "No worries - happens all the time. Let me just ask: about how much do you pay SDGE per month? And do you know if it's higher in summer?"
>
> **THEM:** [Answers]
>
> **YOU:** "Got it. [Write down numbers] What I'll do is use these as estimates, and once you find your actual bill, text me a photo and we'll refine everything. Does that work?"
>
> **THEM:** [Agrees]
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Using the Bill in Your Presentation">
Now that you have the bill, here's how you use it in your presentation:

**Opening with the bill:**
> "So looking at your bill, you're paying SDGE about $150 per month, which is about $1,800 a year. That's pretty typical for a home your size in San Diego. Here's what we can do with solar..."

**Showing savings:**
> "A system for your home would offset about 80% of that usage. So instead of $150/month going to SDGE, you'd pay us about $95/month with our financing. That's $55 in month-one savings, and the savings grow every year."

**Creating urgency:**
> "What most people don't realize is SDGE's rates are going up 18% this year. You're going to see that on your next bill. But with solar, your rate is locked in, so while SDGE goes up, your costs actually stay stable."

**Demonstrating expertise:**
> "Looking at your usage pattern, your peak months are [season]. That means your system needs to be sized to handle those peak months. We'll design a system that covers about 80% of your usage, which gives you maximum ROI."
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Bill as Social Proof">
The SDGE bill isn't just a calculation tool - it's emotional proof:

> "When customers see their bill for the first time in this context, they realize 'Oh wow, I'm actually spending $1,800 a year on this?' It becomes real. And that's when they realize solar isn't an option - it's a necessity."

The bill makes it personal. It's not theoretical. It's their money, in black and white.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Key Takeaways">
1. **Get the bill before the appointment** - Make it part of the booking process
2. **Have a backup plan** - SDGE app first, email second, physical third
3. **Don't be pushy** - Smooth process, not aggressive
4. **Take one clear photo** - Professional, not chaotic
5. **Extract the key numbers** - Usage (kWh) and cost ($)
6. **Use it in your presentation** - Show exact savings based on their bill
7. **Create urgency** - Rate increases + locked-in rates = motivation

The bill is your most powerful tool for showing the real, concrete value of solar. Treat it like the treasure it is.
</Accordion>
