# Getting the SDGE Bill: The Essential First Step

<Lead>The SDGE bill is the single most important document in a solar appointment. A proposal without the bill is theoretical. A proposal with the bill is personal and powerful.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Why the Bill Matters More Than You Think">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "PROOF", label: "Shows Their Current Energy Cost" },
  { value: "CREDIBILITY", label: "Exact Numbers, Not Estimates" },
  { value: "URGENCY", label: "$150/Month in Black and White Hits Different" },
  { value: "ACCURACY", label: "Can't Design Without Knowing Usage" }
]} />

<KeyTerm term="The Cascade">Every advantage compounds — proving the need leads to credibility, which leads to urgency, which leads to accurate quotes, which leads to confidence. The bill unlocks all of it.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Pre-Appointment Request">

<Callout type="important" title="THIS STARTS BEFORE THE APPOINTMENT">
When you're setting up the appointment — or knocking on the door to set it up — mention the bill.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Hey, one thing that'll make our appointment way more efficient — can you pull your most recent SDGE bill? I'm going to review it beforehand so when I sit down with you, I can show you exactly how much you're spending and how solar changes that number. Do you have it handy, or will you need to go dig it out?"</PullQuote>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Frames It as Efficiency">They want that. Nobody wants a longer appointment.</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Explains Why You Need It">They understand the value — it's for *their* benefit.</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Gives Them a Choice">Dig it out now or find it before appointment. Either works.</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Normalizes It">Sounds routine because it is routine.</Step>
</Steps>

<KeyTerm term="Their Likely Responses">"Yeah, I have it" → take a photo. "I'll find it before you come" → they'll look. "I don't have a copy" → handle at appointment. "It's in my email somewhere" → help them search at appointment.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Getting the Bill During the Appointment">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Approach 1: The SDGE App (Best Option)">

<PullQuote>"No problem at all — it's super easy. We can just pull it up on your phone. Do you have the SDGE app?"</PullQuote>

**If yes:** *"Perfect. Just open it up and show me your last month's bill. OK, so you're using about [usage] kWh per month and paying about $[amount]. Got it."*

**If not sure:** *"Search 'SDGE' on your phone — you should find it. Or we can open a web browser and log into their website. Let me help you."*

<KeyTerm term="Why This Is Best">Digital is always available. Easy to screenshot. Shows current and historical usage. Most reliable.</KeyTerm>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Approach 2: Email (Second Best)">

<PullQuote>"Do you get email bills from SDGE? Let me help you search for it. Open your email and search for 'sdge' or 'bill'. It should come up."</PullQuote>

Help them search if needed. *"Great — here it is. Can you screenshot this or forward it to me?"*
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Approach 3: Physical Mail (Works but Slower)">

<PullQuote>"Your bill usually comes in the mail. Do you have the last one lying around somewhere? Let me help you look."</PullQuote>

Help them search — usually kitchen drawers, desk, filing cabinet. *"Here it is. Let me just take a picture of this so I have it for reference."*
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Most Difficult Scenario: No Bill Available">

<Callout type="warning" title="DON'T PANIC">
You can work without the bill. It's suboptimal, but it doesn't kill the appointment.
</Callout>

**Common reasons:** Just moved in. Bills go to spouse. Online account not set up. Email deleted. Never paid attention.

<PullQuote>"That's OK — it happens. Here's what we can do: Let me ask you a couple questions about your bill, and we can estimate your usage. It'll be close, and then once you find the actual bill, we'll refine the numbers. About how much do you pay SDGE each month on average?"</PullQuote>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Estimate In-Person">
Ask: summer bill (highest), winter bill (lowest), average, kWh usage if they know it, time living in home.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Get It Before the Next Meeting">

<PullQuote>"Here's what we'll do: I'm going to send you a link to SDGE's website so you can create an online account. Once you pull your bill, text it to me and I'll refine all these numbers. By the time I come back for the design review, we'll have exact numbers."</PullQuote>

</Step>
</Steps>

<KeyTerm term="Why This Works">Doesn't block the appointment. Gets them to pull the bill before next visit. Gives them an action item which increases commitment.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="What to Look For on the Bill">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "kWh", label: "Monthly Usage — Determines System Size" },
  { value: "$", label: "Monthly Cost — Baseline for Savings" },
  { value: "$/kWh", label: "Rate — Changes with TOU, Season, Consumption" },
  { value: "12-MO", label: "Usage Pattern — System Covers Peak Months" }
]} />

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Monthly Usage (kWh)">
Usually middle or bottom right. How much electricity they use. **Look for kWh usage, not dollars.**
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Monthly Cost ($)">
The big number at the top. What they're paying SDGE. Total amount due.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Rate per kWh">
Middle section. Sometimes multiple rates — peak, off-peak, super-peak. Price per unit of electricity.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Seasonal Usage Pattern">
12-month history (often on back or detailed view). Which month was highest? System needs to cover peak months.
</Step>
</Steps>

**Secondary:** Bill date (confirm it's recent), customer name and address (confirm it matches), account number (used for SDGE coordination later).

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="What NOT to Do">

| Mistake | Wrong | Right |
|---------|-------|-------|
| **Pressure them** | "I need the bill to move forward. Can't do anything without it." | "The bill helps me be more accurate. If you don't have it, we can estimate and refine later." |
| **Act annoyed** | *[Sigh]* "OK, well, this is going to be harder..." | *[Smile]* "No worries — happens all the time. Let's just estimate." |
| **Take it and not return it** | Borrow the bill and forget to give it back | Take a photo and give the original back immediately |
| **Multiple photos** | 5 photos from different angles | ONE clear photo showing the key information |
| **Get frustrated** | Act annoyed they don't know their bill amount | "Most people don't track this closely — that's why we help!" |

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Complete Bill Extraction Script">

<Callout type="tip" title="MEMORIZE THIS FLOW">
From asking to analyzing — the complete word track.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"One thing that'll really help me — do you have your SDGE bill handy?"</PullQuote>

**If they have it:**

<PullQuote>"Perfect, let me take a look at that for a second. OK, so you're using about [X] kWh per month and paying about $[Y] per month. Is that about right for a typical month? And is summer higher than winter, or pretty consistent?"</PullQuote>

**If they don't have it:**

<PullQuote>"No worries — happens all the time. Let me just ask: about how much do you pay SDGE per month? And do you know if it's higher in summer?"</PullQuote>

Then: *"Got it. What I'll do is use these as estimates, and once you find your actual bill, text me a photo and we'll refine everything. Does that work?"*

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Using the Bill in Your Presentation">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Open With the Bill">

<PullQuote>"So looking at your bill, you're paying SDGE about $150 per month, which is about $1,800 a year. That's pretty typical for a home your size in San Diego. Here's what we can do with solar..."</PullQuote>

</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Show Savings">

<PullQuote>"A system for your home would offset about 80% of that usage. So instead of $150/month going to SDGE, you'd pay us about $95/month with our financing. That's $55 in month-one savings, and the savings grow every year."</PullQuote>

</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Create Urgency">

<PullQuote>"What most people don't realize is SDGE's rates are going up 18% this year. You're going to see that on your next bill. But with solar, your rate is locked in."</PullQuote>

</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Demonstrate Expertise">

<PullQuote>"Looking at your usage pattern, your peak months are [season]. That means your system needs to be sized to handle those peak months. We'll design a system that covers about 80% of your usage, which gives you maximum ROI."</PullQuote>

</Step>
</Steps>

<KeyTerm term="The Bill as Emotional Proof">The bill makes it personal. It's not theoretical. It's their money, in black and white. When customers see their bill in this context, they realize solar isn't an option — it's a necessity.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>
