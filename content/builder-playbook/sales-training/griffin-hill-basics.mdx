# Griffin Hill: The Integrity Sales System for Builders

<Lead>As a builder or team leader, your job is not to run every door or close every deal. Your job is to build a team that executes the Griffin Hill system consistently. This page covers the full six-step process, the coaching tools you need, and how to train and measure your reps.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Complete 6-Step Process">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "6", label: "Routines in the System" },
  { value: "CO → NA", label: "Setter Owns" },
  { value: "SP → CI", label: "Closer Owns" },
  { value: "SNE", label: "Ends Every Routine" }
]} />

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Case Open (CO) — Setter">

**Goal:** Turn a suspect into a prospect.

Five plays in sequence: **Rapport** → **Positioning** → **Benefit (5 Ps)** → **Proof** → **Schedule Next Event**

<Callout type="tip">
**Coaching focus:** Most weak setters skip Proof or rush SNE. Drill those two plays specifically.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Needs Audit (NA) — Setter starts, Closer finishes">

**Goal:** Get enough information to build a compelling proposal.

Plays: Reframe → Query Plays (Opportunity, Problem, Effects, Consequences) with Drill Down → SNE

<Callout type="warning">
The #1 closer mistake is presenting too early. A quality NA should take **15-25 minutes**. If your closer presents after 5 minutes, they're skipping this routine.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Solution Presentation (SP) — Closer">

**Goal:** Propose, persuade, convince.

Plays: Reframe → Transition via Benefit → Proposal (FABP method) → Service Levels & Pricing → Call to Action Close

Additional plays: Insurance, Validation, Ignore, Overcoming Objections (Identify/Isolate/Evaluate)

<KeyTerm term="Coaching Focus">Is the proposal personalized to what the homeowner said during NA, or is it a generic pitch? The Reframe is what makes it feel custom.</KeyTerm>
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Adapted Solution (AS) — Closer">

**Goal:** Re-engage when the first solution doesn't close.

Reframe CO + NA + SP, adjust the proposal, issue a new Call to Action Close. This is not "more pressure" — it's a better-fit solution based on what you learned from their objection.

<Callout type="tip">
Track how many deals close on adapted solution vs first attempt.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={5} title="Closing Interactions (CI) — Closer">

**Goal:** Keep the prospect alive during the dangerous delay period.

**Give** something of value → **Get** a mini-commitment → **Schedule Next Event**

<Callout type="warning">
Are your closers following up, or hoping the homeowner calls back? Track CI activity: follow-up calls, next events scheduled, pipeline aging.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={6} title="Fulfillment & Follow Up (FF) — Closer + Ops">

**Goal:** Deliver on promises, collect proof, harvest referrals.

Fulfill promises → Collect Proofs (reviews, testimonials, screenshots) → Harvest Referrals → Check-ins at 30/60/90 days

<KeyTerm term="Coaching Focus">Is your team collecting proofs and referrals, or moving on? The best teams build a referral engine that reduces dependence on cold doors.</KeyTerm>
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

**Plays:** Role-play specific plays in morning meetings. Focus on one play per week for mastery.

**Metrics:** Review daily, not weekly. Use metrics to diagnose, not punish. Trace breakdowns to specific plays.

**Coaching:** Ride-alongs at least twice per week per rep. Ask the rep what went wrong before you tell them.

<Accordion title="Productivity, Proficiency, and Performance">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Stage 1: Productivity">

**Definition:** Doing the activity. Getting reps in motion.

Quality will be low — that's expected. Volume builds confidence and reveals patterns.

<KeyTerm term="Coach">Are they hitting activity minimums? Showing up on time? Following the process at all?</KeyTerm>

<Callout type="warning">
**Red flag:** A rep who will not do the activity. Address effort before skill.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Stage 2: Proficiency">

**Definition:** Doing the activity well. Executing each play with skill.

Now they're active but inconsistent. This is where you coach specific plays. Role-play. Review recordings.

<KeyTerm term="Coach">Which play breaks down most? Are they drilling down or staying surface-level? Can they adapt off-script?</KeyTerm>

<Callout type="warning">
**Red flag:** A productive rep who plateaus. They need play-level coaching, not more volume.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Stage 3: Performance">

**Definition:** Doing the activity well consistently, producing results.

Coaching shifts to optimization: sharpening word tracks, improving close rate by 5%, handling edge cases.

<Callout type="warning">
**Red flag:** A high performer who gets sloppy. Performance without process won't last. Hold the standard.
</Callout>
</Step>
</Steps>

<Accordion title="Metrics — What to Measure">

**Setter Metrics:**

| Metric | Daily Target | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Doors Knocked | 60-80 | Productivity |
| Conversations | 15-25 | Rapport play effectiveness |
| Appointments Set | 3-5 | Full CO execution |
| Sit Rate | 70%+ | SNE + confirmation quality |
| Both Decision Makers | 80%+ | SNE question discipline |
| Bill Collected | 60%+ | CO completeness |

<KeyTerm term="Diagnostic Flow">Low appointments? Check conversations. Low conversations? Check doors. Low sit rate? Check SNE execution.</KeyTerm>

**Closer Metrics:**

| Metric | Weekly Target | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Appointments Sat | 8-12 | Pipeline activity |
| Close Rate | 30-40% | SP execution quality |
| Adapted Solution Closes | Track % | AS routine skill |
| Follow-Up Calls | 15-20/week | CI discipline |
| Referrals Collected | 2-3/week | FF execution |
| Google Reviews | 2-3/week | FF execution |

<Accordion title="Six Steps to a Successful Sales Call">

<Callout type="insight" title="TEACH THIS TO EVERY REP">
Applies to every door knock, every appointment, and every follow-up.
</Callout>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Define the Objective">
Before the interaction, know exactly what outcome you want. Not "have a good conversation" — a specific, measurable outcome.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Identify the Plays">
Which plays from the routine are most important for this specific interaction?
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Select the Plays">
Choose which plays you'll use. Not all are needed every time.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Practice">
Role-play the interaction before it happens. Takes 60 seconds. A closer in the car before an appointment should run their Reframe out loud.
</Step>
<Step number={5} title="Execute">
Run the interaction. Follow the process. Trust the plays. Adapt within the system.
</Step>
<Step number={6} title="Evaluate">
What worked? What play broke down? What will you do differently?
</Step>
</Steps>

<KeyTerm term="The Edge">Most reps only do Step 5. The ones who do all six outperform everyone.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Running Griffin Hill in Your Morning Meeting">

**10-Minute Format:**

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Metrics Review (3 min)">
Yesterday's numbers. Who hit targets? Where are the gaps?
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Play of the Day (4 min)">
Pick one play. Teach it or role-play it. Keep it focused.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Objective Setting (2 min)">
Every rep states their specific objective for the day.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Energy & Send-Off (1 min)">
Get them out the door with purpose.
</Step>
</Steps>

**Weekly Deep Dive Schedule:**

| Day | Focus |
|-----|-------|
| Monday | CO role-plays (setters) / NA role-plays (closers) |
| Tuesday | Review recorded doors or sits |
| Wednesday | Overcoming Objections practice |
| Thursday | Pipeline review — every pending deal gets a next event |
| Friday | Proof/referral push + wins celebration |

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Building a Griffin Hill Culture">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Use the Vocabulary">
Say "Case Open" not "door approach." Say "Schedule Next Event" not "book an appointment." When everyone uses the same words, coaching is faster.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Celebrate Process, Not Just Outcomes">
When a setter nails all five CO plays but the homeowner says no, celebrate the execution. Process produces results over time.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Coach Daily">
Not weekly. Not when there's a problem. A 2-minute coaching moment after a ride-along is worth more than a 30-minute lecture.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Track and Display Metrics">
Put the numbers where everyone can see them. Visibility creates accountability.
</Step>
<Step number={5} title="Hold the Standard">

<PullQuote>"You skipped the Proof play. Let's run it again."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="The Rule">The standard you walk past is the standard you accept.</KeyTerm>
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>
