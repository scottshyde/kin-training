# Griffin Hill Sales System Basics

Griffin Hill is the proven sales methodology that drives success at KIN Home. Understanding these core principles will transform your sales effectiveness. Every interaction should focus on understanding customer needs and providing genuine solutions.

<Accordion title="The Griffin Hill Philosophy">
Griffin Hill emphasizes that **sales is about serving customers, not selling products.** Every interaction should focus on understanding customer needs and providing genuine solutions.

### Core Principle: The Needs Audit

The Needs Audit is the foundation of every sale:

1. **Ask powerful questions** - Understand the customer's current situation, frustrations, and desires
2. **Listen actively** - Let the customer talk 70%, you talk 30%
3. **Identify the real need** - Look beyond stated problems to underlying concerns
4. **Position your solution** - Show how your product/service solves their real need
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Six Core Activities">
Every salesperson must master six daily activities:

### 1. Prospecting
- **Goal:** Find qualified leads consistently
- **Daily target:** 8-10 quality conversations
- **Methods:** Door-to-door, phone calls, referrals, digital leads
- **Key skill:** Building initial rapport and identifying opportunities

### 2. Qualifying
- **Goal:** Ensure the prospect is a good fit
- **Key questions:**
  - Do they own their home?
  - Are they dissatisfied with current situation?
  - Do they have decision-making authority?
  - Can they afford the solution?

### 3. Needs Analysis
- **Goal:** Understand the customer's real problem
- **Techniques:**
  - Ask open-ended questions
  - Use the Needs Audit framework
  - Identify emotional drivers (not just logical)
  - Confirm understanding by summarizing

### 4. Presentation
- **Goal:** Show how your solution meets their needs
- **Key elements:**
  - Personalized proposal
  - Financial benefits highlighted
  - Risk mitigation addressed
  - Clear next steps

### 5. Overcoming Objections
- **Goal:** Address concerns without being defensive
- **Method:**
  - Listen fully to the objection
  - Acknowledge their concern
  - Ask clarifying questions
  - Provide evidence-based response
  - Confirm they're satisfied

### 6. Closing
- **Goal:** Gain commitment
- **Techniques:**
  - Trial close: "If we could solve this, would you be interested?"
  - Assumptive close: "Let's schedule the installation"
  - Takeaway close: "I understand you're not ready now..."
  - Direct close: "Are you ready to move forward?"
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Customer Journey">
```
Prospect -> Qualify -> Needs Audit -> Present -> Handle Objections -> Close -> Install
```

At each stage:
- **Build trust** through consistency and honesty
- **Demonstrate understanding** of their situation
- **Provide solutions** that genuinely help
- **Remove barriers** to moving forward
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Key Mindset Principles">
### 1. Solution Focus
Don't focus on features. Focus on benefits and outcomes the customer cares about.

### 2. Long-term Relationship
Your goal isn't just one sale — it's building a customer for life.

### 3. Professionalism
Your appearance, demeanor, and communication reflect KIN Home's values.

### 4. Persistence
Objections aren't rejections. They're opportunities to provide more value.

### 5. Continuous Learning
The best salespeople never stop improving their craft.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Daily Discipline">
Success comes from consistent action:

- **Daily prospecting** - Quality conversations every day
- **Daily learning** - Study this playbook, watch training videos, practice scripts
- **Daily reflection** - What worked? What didn't? How can you improve?
- **Daily practice** - Role-play objection handling, rehearse presentations
- **Daily accountability** - Track activities, review metrics with your team
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Common Mistakes to Avoid">
1. **Talking too much** - Let customers speak 70% of the time
2. **Presenting too early** - Complete the Needs Audit first
3. **Assuming you know their need** - Always confirm with questions
4. **Using high-pressure tactics** - Trust-based selling works better
5. **Not following up** - Persistence is crucial
6. **Ignoring objections** - They're valuable feedback
7. **Not closing** - Confidence in asking for the sale is essential
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Your Success Metrics">
Track these daily:

- **Prospecting contacts:** 8-10 per day
- **Qualified leads:** 3-5 per day
- **Presentations:** 2-3 per week
- **Closes:** 1-2 per week (30-40% close rate is strong)
- **Customer satisfaction:** 4.8+ stars (target)
</Accordion>
