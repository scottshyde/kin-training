# Griffin Hill: The Integrity Sales System for Builders

As a builder or team leader, your job is not to run every door or close every deal. Your job is to build a team that executes the Griffin Hill system consistently. This page covers the full six-step process, the coaching tools you need, and how to train and measure your reps.

<Accordion title="The Complete 6-Step Process (All Routines)">
Griffin Hill organizes the sales cycle into six routines. Each one has specific plays, and each play has a purpose. Your team needs to know what to do, when to do it, and why it matters.

### Routine 1: Case Open (CO)
**Owner:** Setter | **Goal:** Turn a suspect into a prospect

The CO has five plays executed in sequence:
1. **Rapport** — Who I am. Build human connection in the first five seconds
2. **Positioning** — What I do. Frame KIN Home as a local energy company that lowers bills
3. **Benefit** — Why they should care. Use the 5 Ps: Profit, Prestige, Pleasure, Preservation, Pain Relief
4. **Proof** — Evidence that the benefit is real. Neighbor references, screenshots, reviews
5. **Schedule Next Event (SNE)** — Book the appointment with both decision makers and the utility bill

**Coaching focus:** Are your setters executing all five plays in order? Most weak setters skip Proof or rush SNE. Drill those two plays specifically.

### Routine 2: Needs Audit (NA)
**Owner:** Setter starts, Closer finishes | **Goal:** Get enough information to build a compelling proposal

Plays:
1. **Reframe** — Restate what was established in the Case Open to transition into discovery
2. **Query Plays with Drill Down:**
   - **Opportunity Queries** — Status quo, vision, 3 likes, 3 frustrations, decision process, financial situation
   - **Problem Queries** — 5 industry challenges, 3 barriers, 1 breakthrough question
   - **Effects** — What is the impact of the problem?
   - **Consequences** — What happens if they do nothing?
3. **Schedule Next Event** — Transition to the Solution Presentation

**Coaching focus:** Are your closers asking enough questions before presenting? The #1 closer mistake is presenting too early. A quality Needs Audit should take 15-25 minutes. If your closer is presenting after 5 minutes, they are skipping this routine.

### Routine 3: Solution Presentation (SP)
**Owner:** Closer | **Goal:** Propose, persuade, convince

Plays:
1. **Reframe** — Summarize CO + NA findings. "So based on everything you've told me..."
2. **Transition via Benefit** — Bridge from their pain to your solution
3. **Proposal** — Method (FABP: Feature, Advantage, Benefit, Proof), Statement of Work, Mutual Expectations
4. **Service Levels & Pricing** — Present options clearly
5. **Call to Action Close** — Ask for the commitment

Additional plays available:
- **Insurance Play** — Reduce perceived risk
- **Validation Play** — Affirm their concerns are normal
- **Ignore Play** — Some objections dissolve when you do not engage with them
- **Overcoming Objections** — Identify, Isolate, Evaluate (simple objections: Feel/Felt/Found; complex: deeper discovery)

**Coaching focus:** Is the closer reframing before presenting? Is the proposal personalized to what the homeowner said during NA, or is it a generic pitch? The Reframe is what makes the proposal feel custom.

### Routine 4: Adapted Solution (AS)
**Owner:** Closer | **Goal:** Re-engage when the first solution does not close

When a homeowner does not close on the initial SP, the closer reframes CO + NA + SP, then adjusts the proposal and issues a new Call to Action Close. This is not "more pressure" — it is a better-fit solution based on what you learned from their objection.

**Coaching focus:** Are your closers giving up after one "no," or are they adapting? Track how many deals close on the adapted solution vs. first attempt.

### Routine 5: Closing Interactions (CI)
**Owner:** Closer | **Goal:** Keep the prospect alive during the dangerous delay period

Between the presentation and a final decision, deals die. CI is about staying engaged:
- **Give something** — A new piece of value (updated savings, a neighbor testimonial, a rate change alert)
- **Get a mini-commitment** — Something that moves them forward (pull up their last bill, talk to their spouse, check their roof from the app)
- **Schedule Next Event** — Never leave a follow-up without a specific next time

**Coaching focus:** Are your closers following up, or are they hoping the homeowner calls back? Track CI activity: follow-up calls made, next events scheduled, and the pipeline aging report.

### Routine 6: Fulfillment & Follow Up (FF)
**Owner:** Closer + Ops | **Goal:** Deliver on promises, collect proof, harvest referrals

1. **Fulfill promises** — Everything you said during the sale must happen. Install dates, savings projections, communication cadence
2. **Collect Proofs** — Get Google reviews, video testimonials, before/after bill screenshots. These feed back into your setters' Proof plays
3. **Harvest Referrals** — Ask every installed customer: "Who else do you know in the neighborhood who's tired of overpaying SDG&E?"
4. **Schedule Next Event** — Check-in calls at 30, 60, 90 days post-install

**Coaching focus:** Is your team collecting proofs and referrals, or are they moving on to the next deal? The best teams build a referral engine that reduces dependence on cold doors.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The 4 Coaching Technology Tools">
Griffin Hill gives you four tools to develop your team. Use all four — most struggling teams only use one or two.

### 1. Process
The six-step system itself is the process. Your job is to make sure every rep knows the steps, the plays within each step, and the sequence. If a rep does not know the process, they cannot execute it.

**How to coach it:**
- Whiteboard the six routines and quiz your reps
- Have reps explain each play in their own words
- Walk new reps through the process before they ever knock a door or sit an appointment

### 2. Plays
Plays are the specific actions within each routine. Rapport is a play. Drill Down is a play. The Insurance Play is a play. Your reps need to know which plays exist and when to use them.

**How to coach it:**
- Role-play specific plays in morning meetings
- Focus on one play per week for mastery
- Record and review plays from real door or sit interactions

### 3. Metrics
You cannot coach what you cannot measure. Metrics tell you where the breakdown is happening.

**How to coach it:**
- Review metrics daily, not weekly
- Use metrics to diagnose, not to punish
- When a number is off, trace it back to the specific play that is failing

### 4. Coaching
This is the meta-tool — your ability to observe, diagnose, and develop your reps. Coaching is not telling someone what to do. It is watching them do it, identifying the gap, and helping them close it.

**How to coach it:**
- Ride-alongs and listen-ins at least twice per week per rep
- Use the "observe, diagnose, prescribe" model
- Ask the rep what they think went wrong before you tell them
- Celebrate wins. Correct process, not just outcomes
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Productivity, Proficiency, and Performance">
Griffin Hill teaches that improvement happens in three stages. You must coach each stage differently.

### Stage 1: Productivity
**Definition:** Doing the activity. Getting reps in motion.

At this stage, the rep just needs to do the work. Knock doors. Make calls. Book appointments. Run sits. Quality will be low and that is expected. Volume builds confidence and reveals patterns.

**Your coaching focus:**
- Are they hitting activity minimums? (Doors knocked, conversations, appointments set)
- Are they showing up on time and putting in full effort?
- Are they following the process at all, even roughly?

**Red flag:** A rep who will not do the activity. Address effort before skill.

### Stage 2: Proficiency
**Definition:** Doing the activity well. Executing each play with skill.

Now the rep is active but inconsistent. Some doors go great, others fall flat. This is where you coach specific plays and techniques. Role-play. Review recordings. Focus on the weakest link in their chain.

**Your coaching focus:**
- Which play is breaking down most often?
- Are they drilling down on Needs Audit queries or staying surface-level?
- Can they adapt when a door or sit goes off-script?

**Red flag:** A rep who is productive but plateaued. They need play-level coaching, not more volume.

### Stage 3: Performance
**Definition:** Doing the activity well consistently, and producing results.

The rep now executes the system reliably. They close deals, earn referrals, and perform under pressure. Coaching shifts to optimization: sharpening word tracks, improving close rate by 5%, handling edge-case objections.

**Your coaching focus:**
- Where can they gain an extra 5-10% conversion?
- Are they mentoring newer reps?
- Are they collecting proofs and referrals consistently?

**Red flag:** A high performer who gets sloppy. Performance without process will not last. Hold the standard.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How to Coach Reps Through Each Routine">
Use this as a quick reference for your one-on-ones and ride-alongs.

### Coaching Case Open (Setters)
- Listen to their door approach. Do they hit all five plays?
- Time their Rapport — it should feel natural, not rehearsed, and take under 10 seconds
- Check their Benefit play — are they using the right P for the situation?
- Is their Proof play specific? (Name, street, dollar amount — not "lots of people are saving money")
- Is their SNE locked? (Specific day, time, both decision makers confirmed, bill requested)

**Quick drill:** Have them run their Case Open on you in 90 seconds. If they cannot do it in practice, they cannot do it at the door.

### Coaching Needs Audit (Closers)
- Sit in on an appointment. How many questions do they ask before presenting?
- Are they using Opportunity and Problem queries, or just one type?
- Are they drilling down or accepting the first answer?
- Do they reframe before transitioning to the presentation?

**Quick drill:** Give them a homeowner scenario. They must ask at least 8 questions before you allow them to present. If they struggle, the NA muscle is weak.

### Coaching Solution Presentation (Closers)
- Does the presentation reference what the homeowner said during NA?
- Are they using FABP (Feature, Advantage, Benefit, Proof) or just listing features?
- Do they present pricing confidently or apologetically?
- Is the Call to Action Close clear and direct?

**Quick drill:** Role-play a presentation where the homeowner said their summer bill is $350 and they are frustrated about rates increasing. The closer must tie every part of the proposal back to those two points.

### Coaching Adapted Solution (Closers)
- When a deal does not close, does the closer ask why or just accept the "no"?
- Can they reframe the entire conversation and pivot to a different solution?
- Are they tracking which adapted solutions convert?

### Coaching Closing Interactions (Closers)
- How many pending deals are in the pipeline?
- When was the last time each pending deal was contacted?
- Does each pending deal have a scheduled next event?
- Are closers giving value on follow-ups or just "checking in"?

**Quick drill:** Pull up the pipeline. Any deal older than 7 days without a scheduled next event is at risk. Have the closer call them right now.

### Coaching Fulfillment & Follow Up
- Are installed customers being contacted at 30/60/90 days?
- How many Google reviews has the team collected this month?
- How many referrals came from installed customers?
- Are proof assets (screenshots, testimonials) being shared with setters?
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Metrics — What to Measure">
Track these numbers daily for every rep. Review them in your morning meeting and one-on-ones.

### Setter Metrics
| Metric | Daily Target | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Doors Knocked | 60-80 | Productivity — are they working? |
| Conversations | 15-25 | Rapport play effectiveness |
| Appointments Set | 3-5 | Full CO execution |
| Sit Rate | 70%+ | SNE quality and confirmation quality |
| Both Decision Makers Present | 80%+ | SNE question discipline |
| Bill Collected at Door | 60%+ | Closing the CO completely |

**Diagnostic flow:** Low appointments? Check conversations. Low conversations? Check doors. Low sit rate? Check SNE execution and confirmation calls.

### Closer Metrics
| Metric | Weekly Target | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Appointments Sat | 8-12 | Pipeline activity |
| Close Rate | 30-40% | SP execution quality |
| Average Deal Size | Varies by market | Pricing confidence and upsell skill |
| Adapted Solution Closes | Track % | AS routine skill |
| Follow-Up Calls Made | 15-20/week | CI routine discipline |
| Referrals Collected | 2-3/week | FF routine execution |
| Google Reviews Collected | 2-3/week | FF routine execution |

**Diagnostic flow:** Low close rate? Sit in on an appointment — is the NA thorough? Is the SP personalized? Low referrals? Check if they are asking at all.

### Team-Level Metrics
| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------------|
| Set-to-Sit Ratio | Appointment quality and confirmation process |
| Sit-to-Close Ratio | Closer effectiveness |
| Average Days to Close | Pipeline health |
| Cancellation Rate | Post-close fulfillment quality |
| Referral Rate | Customer satisfaction and FF execution |
| Revenue per Door Knocked | Overall system efficiency |

**The metric that matters most:** Revenue per door knocked. This single number captures the entire system's effectiveness from first knock to signed contract.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Six Steps to a Successful Sales Call">
Teach this framework to every rep. It applies to every door knock, every appointment, and every follow-up.

### Step 1: Define the Objective
Before the interaction, the rep must know exactly what outcome they want. Not "have a good conversation" — a specific, measurable outcome.

- Setter: "I will book an appointment for tomorrow at 5:30 with both decision makers and the utility bill"
- Closer: "I will close this deal tonight or identify the exact objection preventing it"
- Follow-up: "I will get a mini-commitment — they will pull their latest bill and confirm a re-sit for Thursday"

### Step 2: Identify the Plays
Which plays from the routine are most important for this specific interaction? A setter approaching a house with solar panels already on it needs different plays than a setter approaching a first-time prospect.

### Step 3: Select the Plays
Choose the specific plays you will use. Not all plays are needed every time. A follow-up call does not need a full Case Open — it needs a Reframe and a CI play.

### Step 4: Practice
Role-play the interaction before it happens. This takes 60 seconds. A closer sitting in the car before an appointment should run through their Reframe and opening NA queries out loud.

### Step 5: Execute
Run the interaction. Follow the process. Trust the plays. Adapt when needed but stay within the system.

### Step 6: Evaluate
After the interaction, review what happened. What worked? What play broke down? What will you do differently next time? This is where coaching and self-coaching intersect.

**As a builder:** Teach your team to do Steps 1-4 before every interaction and Step 6 after every interaction. Most reps only do Step 5. The ones who do all six will outperform everyone.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Running Griffin Hill in Your Morning Meeting">
Your morning meeting is where the system comes to life. Here is a structure that works.

### 10-Minute Morning Meeting Format
1. **Metrics Review (3 min)** — Yesterday's numbers. Who hit targets? Where are the gaps?
2. **Play of the Day (4 min)** — Pick one play. Teach it or role-play it. Keep it focused
3. **Objective Setting (2 min)** — Every rep states their specific objective for the day using Step 1 of the Six Steps
4. **Energy & Send-Off (1 min)** — Hype the team. Get them out the door with purpose

### Weekly Deep Dive (Pick One Per Week)
- Monday: Case Open role-plays (setters) / Needs Audit role-plays (closers)
- Tuesday: Review recorded doors or sits — coach specific plays
- Wednesday: Overcoming Objections practice
- Thursday: Pipeline review — every pending deal gets a next event
- Friday: Proof and referral collection push + wins celebration
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Building a Griffin Hill Culture on Your Team">
The system works when it becomes the language of your team. Here is how to make that happen.

- **Use the vocabulary.** Say "Case Open" not "door approach." Say "Schedule Next Event" not "book an appointment." Say "Needs Audit" not "asking questions." When everyone uses the same words, coaching is faster
- **Celebrate process, not just outcomes.** When a setter nails all five CO plays but the homeowner still says no, celebrate the execution. Process produces results over time
- **Coach daily.** Not weekly. Not when there is a problem. Every single day, coach someone on something specific. A 2-minute coaching moment after a ride-along is worth more than a 30-minute lecture
- **Track and display metrics.** Put the numbers where everyone can see them. Visibility creates accountability
- **Hold the standard.** When a rep skips a step, call it out. Not harshly — but clearly. "You skipped the Proof play. Let's run it again." The standard you walk past is the standard you accept
</Accordion>
