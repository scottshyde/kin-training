# Knocking an Install: Referrals from Existing Customers

## The Foundation: Are You Ready?

This is a great flow to use if you know how to do a Needs Audit properly already. **If you do not have all the needs audit word tracks and solidify the appointment word tracks memorized yet, GO NO FURTHER.** Return once those have been nailed down.

This play-set assumes you've mastered:
- Relationship building and rapport
- Comprehensive needs audit questions
- Overcoming objections
- Closing appointments

If you haven't mastered those, you'll struggle with this. Come back when you're ready.

---

## What is "Knocking an Install"?

**Knocking an install** means visiting the homes of customers who already have solar systems with KOTA (or KOTA's predecessors like LGCY), engaging with them about their experience, and harvesting referrals from them.

This is one of the most powerful lead generation strategies because:
1. **Proven product:** They already love solar - you're not selling them on the concept
2. **Social proof:** They're happy enough to have already installed
3. **Neighbor authority:** They'll refer you to neighbors they trust
4. **High intent:** Referrals from neighbors are pre-qualified leads
5. **Conversion rate:** Referred leads convert 2-3x higher than cold leads

## The Philosophy: Gratitude, Not Pressure

Your goal is NOT to pressure existing customers for referrals. Your goal is to:
1. Express genuine gratitude for their business
2. Learn about their favorite parts of the experience
3. Understand what's working in their lives because of solar
4. Ask naturally about who they know who might benefit
5. Get permission to mention their name

**Tone:** Grateful, curious, generous. NOT salesy, pushy, or commission-hungry.

## The Complete Knock-an-Install Process

### Step 1: Door Knock & Rapport (2-3 minutes)

**Word Track:**
> "Hey, what's up? How's your day going?
>
> [Listen for genuine response]
>
> Amazing. I'm Cody with Kinhome. I'm following up on all things solar in the neighborhood. Because you are a solar client, I just had a couple questions for you. Is that okay?"

**Key Points:**
- Casual, friendly opening
- Identify yourself and company
- Explain why you're there (not mysterious)
- Ask permission before diving in
- Make it clear this is a quick check-in, not a sales pitch

### Step 2: The Gratitude & Favorite Parts (5-7 minutes)

**Word Track:**
> "First of all, I just want to say thank you for going solar. That was a great decision, and I'm glad you're part of the KOTA family.
>
> I'm curious - what are 2-3 of your favorite parts of your experience working with Kin? Just walk me through what's been great."

[Listen carefully. Let them talk. Take notes.]

**Why this works:**
- Starts on a positive note
- Gets them talking (builds rapport)
- Helps you understand what messaging resonates
- Gives you testimonial content
- Builds authentic conversation

**Listen for:**
- Savings amounts
- Reliability of system
- Quality of installation
- Service experience
- Impact on their lives
- Emotional benefits (energy independence, environmental pride, etc.)

### Step 3: Deepening the Conversation (3-5 minutes)

**Follow-up Word Tracks:**

> "Tell me more about that."

[Let them elaborate on what they said]

> "What do you love most about your system?"

[Listen and take mental note]

> "When did you get it installed?"

[Understanding their timeline]

> "And who was your rep again?"

[Confirm they remember their closer]

> "Are you still in contact with them?"

[Gauge satisfaction]

**What You're Gathering:**
- How satisfied are they (trust level)
- Timeline since installation (how much benefit they've seen)
- Their closer's name (so you're not confused about which rep serviced them)
- Ongoing relationship (do they still talk to KOTA)
- Level of enthusiasm (high energy = great referral source)

### Step 4: The Referral Harvest (5-10 minutes)

This is the critical part - but do it gently, naturally, with genuine curiosity.

**Word Track 1 - The Neighbor Question:**
> "Now it's time for my real question - since you've had it, who has asked about the project?
>
> Like, who's come up to you and said 'Hey, I noticed your solar panels, tell me about them'?"

[Listen. Let them think. They might list 2-3 neighbors]

> "And do you know what's held them back from doing it as well?"

[Understand the objections so you can address them when you knock]

---

**Word Track 2 - The Direct Question:**
> "Which of your neighbors have you told about your experience going solar?"

[Listen for names and enthusiasm]

> "Who's your best friend in the neighborhood?"

[Get specific names and relationships]

> "Can you point them out to me on this map? See if I've already talked with them?"

[Practical: Know who's already been knocked on]

---

**Word Track 3 - The Permission Close:**
> "Do you mind if I go knock on their door and tell them you had a fantastic experience? I'd love to tell them you recommended us."

[Critical moment - get permission to use their name]

---

**Word Track 4 - Getting the Names:**
> "What's their name again? And where do they live exactly?"

[Exact address, spelling of name]

> "What do you think would be their biggest concern about going solar?"

[Prepare your objection handling]

> "And do you think they'd be open to talking about it?"

[Gauge likelihood of receptiveness]

---

### Step 5: The Commitment & Thank You (2 minutes)

**Word Track:**
> "I really appreciate you taking the time. You're awesome. I'm going to go knock on [Neighbor's Name]'s door and tell them you recommended us.
>
> If you think of anyone else, feel free to reach out to me directly. Here's my card. [Hand over card]
>
> And hey - if there's ever anything you need with your system, you know who to call."

[Firm handshake, genuine smile]

## The Complete Workflow: From Knock to Conversion

**Example Complete Conversation:**

YOU: "Hey, what's up? How's your day going?"

HOMEOWNER: "Good, good, just finished some yard work."

YOU: "Nice! I'm Cody with Kinhome. I'm following up on solar with everyone in the neighborhood. Since you've got a solar system, I had a quick question - mind if I ask?"

HOMEOWNER: "Sure, yeah."

YOU: "First, thank you for going solar - that was a great call. I'm curious - what are 2-3 of your favorite parts of your experience with us?"

HOMEOWNER: "Well, the savings have been amazing. We went from $180 a month to like $50. And the guys were really professional - they didn't make a mess. Plus knowing we're doing something good for the environment."

YOU: "That's awesome - $180 to $50 is huge. Tell me more about the savings part - like, how quickly did you notice that?"

HOMEOWNER: "First bill after they activated it. Like, it was immediate."

YOU: "That must have felt good. And what about the installation - when did you get it done?"

HOMEOWNER: "About three years ago."

YOU: "Three years - so you've been enjoying these savings for a while now. Who was your rep?"

HOMEOWNER: "Uh, I think his name was Mike? Or Mark?"

YOU: "Got it. Cool. So here's my real question - since you've had it, who's asked you about the panels?"

HOMEOWNER: "Oh man, definitely my neighbor Tom. He asked me like six months ago. And then the Johnsons across the street asked too."

YOU: "Nice. And what do you think is holding them back?"

HOMEOWNER: "Tom's worried about the cost. The Johnsons are just thinking about it."

YOU: "That makes sense. Do you mind if I go knock on Tom's door and tell him you said it's been awesome and to talk to me?"

HOMEOWNER: "Yeah, for sure. Go talk to him."

YOU: "Perfect. What's Tom's last name, and what's his address?"

HOMEOWNER: "Tom Richardson, it's the blue house right there [points]"

YOU: "Got it. And what do you think his biggest thing is - the cost concern?"

HOMEOWNER: "Yeah, I think he's worried he can't afford it."

YOU: "I can help with that. Thanks so much for your time - you're awesome. Here's my card in case you think of anyone else. And if you ever need anything with your system, just call me."

[Handshake and leave]

## The "Golden Questions" Framework

These are the questions that unlock the best referrals:

1. **"Who has asked you about your panels?"** - Direct, specific, gets real names
2. **"What's held them back?"** - Understand their concerns so you can address them
3. **"Who's your best friend in the neighborhood?"** - Gets the closest relationships
4. **"Can I use your name when I talk to them?"** - Social proof permission
5. **"What do you think their biggest concern is?"** - Preframing for your pitch

Master these five questions and you'll harvest high-quality referrals consistently.

## The Follow-Up: From Referral to Appointment

Once you've gotten a referral, your first knock on **that** neighbor's door changes:

**Word Track:**
> "Hey, how's your day going? I'm Cody with Kinhome. I actually just talked to [Previous Customer] right there [point], and they told me you might be interested in solar?
>
> Do you have two minutes for a quick question?"

**Notice what changed:**
- You NAME the referral source immediately
- You REFERENCE where they are (social proof)
- You assume interest gently ("might be interested")
- You ask for time, not permission (more confident)

This completely changes the dynamic. Instead of cold knocking, you're a trusted friend of their neighbor.

## Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Being Too Salesy
**Wrong:** "Hey! You need solar! It's amazing! Can I schedule you for an appointment?"

**Right:** "Hey - I just talked to Tom. He loves his system. I'm just curious what you think about solar?"

### Mistake 2: Pressuring for Referrals
**Wrong:** "Who should I talk to? I need three names."

**Right:** "Who's asked you about the panels? No pressure if no one has."

### Mistake 3: Not Using Their Name
**Wrong:** "I talked to your neighbor and he said to talk to you."

**Right:** "I just talked to Tom Richardson and he said you'd asked about solar. He told me to come see you."

### Mistake 4: Forgetting to Thank Them
**Wrong:** Get referral and leave

**Right:** Thank them for their time, their business, their help, and their referral

### Mistake 5: Rushing the Conversation
**Wrong:** "Tell me your favorite parts quickly."

**Right:** Listen fully, take notes, let them elaborate, show genuine interest

## Mindset for Knock-an-Install Success

### You're Not Selling - You're Grateful

You already made the sale with this customer. You're not here to convince them of anything. You're here to say thank you and learn from their experience.

### Your Best Testimonial is Them

Every satisfied customer is your best marketing. They've already done all the convincing - they have solar, they love it, they save money. When you knock with their name attached, you're not selling. You're introducing.

### Referrals are Golden

A referred lead is 2-3x more likely to close than a cold lead. They already know what solar is, they know it works, and they've heard from someone they trust that KOTA is legit. Your job is just to educate and schedule.

### Every Customer is a Sales Team Member

You're not asking for a favor. You're giving them the opportunity to help their neighbor make a great decision. They WANT to help. They LOVE their system. Let them.

## The Numbers: Why This Works

**Average Results from Knock-an-Install:**
- 8-10 homes knocked per day
- 4-5 homeowners available
- 2-3 give referrals
- Average of 2-3 names per referral
- = 4-9 qualified leads per day
- From just knocking on 8 homes

**Conversion Rate:**
- Cold knock: 10-15% appointment rate
- Referred knock: 40-50% appointment rate
- That's 3x better

**Why:**
- Customer has already solved their own objection (they have solar)
- They're saying "this is good"
- Neighbor knows it worked for them
- You're not cold - you're warm

## The Challenge: 30 Days of Consistent Knocking

Your growth comes from consistency. Try this:

**Week 1-2:** Knock on 10 installs (find them via maps, records, or your manager)
- Get used to the rhythm
- Collect feedback on messaging
- Harvest your first referrals

**Week 3-4:** Follow up on referrals AND knock 10 more installs
- See how many referrals convert to appointments
- Refine your approach based on feedback
- Build your reputation as the "thank you guy"

**Week 5+:** Systematic rolling 10 installs per week + referral follow-ups
- You'll have a constant stream of warm leads
- Your referral network grows exponentially
- Your conversion rate improves as your skills improve

## Key Takeaways

1. **Master needs audit first** - Don't attempt this until you're excellent at discovery
2. **Be genuinely grateful** - This is about appreciation, not commission
3. **Ask the golden questions** - Who asked? What held them back? Can I use your name?
4. **Use their name** - "Tom said to talk to you" is 10x better than "your neighbor said..."
5. **Follow up on referrals** - A referral unharvested is a lead wasted
6. **Be consistent** - 10 installs per week becomes your referral engine
7. **Listen more than you talk** - Your favorite part question should get them talking for 3 minutes

This is one of the highest-ROI activities you can do. Existing customers who are happy are your best sales team. Treat them that way.
