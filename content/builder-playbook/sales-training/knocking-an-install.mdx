# Knocking an Install: Referrals from Existing Customers

<Lead>Knocking an install means visiting existing solar customers, engaging with their experience, and harvesting referrals. Referred leads convert 2-3x higher than cold leads. This is one of the highest-ROI activities you can do.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Before You Start">

<Callout type="warning" title="PREREQUISITES — DO NOT SKIP">
This play-set assumes you've mastered: relationship building and rapport, comprehensive needs audit questions, overcoming objections, and closing appointments. **If you do not have the needs audit and solidify word tracks memorized, GO NO FURTHER.** Return once those are nailed down.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Philosophy">

<KeyTerm term="Gratitude, Not Pressure">Your goal is NOT to pressure existing customers for referrals. Express genuine gratitude, learn about their favorite parts, understand what solar has changed, ask naturally about who might benefit, and get permission to mention their name.</KeyTerm>

<Callout type="insight">
**Tone:** Grateful, curious, generous. NOT salesy, pushy, or commission-hungry. You already made the sale with this customer. You're not here to convince — you're here to thank and learn.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Complete Process">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Door Knock & Rapport (2-3 min)">

<PullQuote>"Hey, what's up? How's your day going? Amazing. I'm [Name] with KIN Home. I'm following up on all things solar in the neighborhood. Because you are a solar client, I just had a couple questions for you. Is that okay?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Key Points">Casual, friendly. Identify yourself and company. Explain why you're there. Ask permission before diving in. Make it clear this is a quick check-in, not a sales pitch.</KeyTerm>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Gratitude & Favorite Parts (5-7 min)">

<PullQuote>"First of all, I just want to say thank you for going solar. That was a great decision, and I'm glad you're part of the KIN family. I'm curious — what are 2-3 of your favorite parts of your experience working with KIN? Just walk me through what's been great."</PullQuote>

**Listen for:** savings amounts, reliability, installation quality, service experience, emotional benefits (energy independence, environmental pride).

<Callout type="tip">
Let them talk. Take notes. This builds rapport, gives you testimonial content, and sets a positive tone before the referral ask.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Deepening the Conversation (3-5 min)">

<PullQuote>"Tell me more about that."</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"What do you love most about your system?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"When did you get it installed? And who was your rep again? Are you still in contact with them?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="What You're Gathering">Satisfaction level (trust), timeline since installation (benefit realized), their closer's name, ongoing relationship with KIN, level of enthusiasm (high energy = great referral source).</KeyTerm>
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="The Referral Harvest (5-10 min)">

<Callout type="important" title="THE CRITICAL MOMENT">
Do this gently, naturally, with genuine curiosity. Not a sales close — a conversation.
</Callout>

**The Neighbor Question:**

<PullQuote>"Now it's time for my real question — since you've had it, who has asked about the project? Like, who's come up to you and said 'Hey, I noticed your solar panels, tell me about them'?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"And do you know what's held them back from doing it as well?"</PullQuote>

**The Direct Question:**

<PullQuote>"Which of your neighbors have you told about your experience going solar? Who's your best friend in the neighborhood? Can you point them out to me on this map?"</PullQuote>

**The Permission Close:**

<PullQuote>"Do you mind if I go knock on their door and tell them you had a fantastic experience? I'd love to tell them you recommended us."</PullQuote>

**Getting the Details:**

<PullQuote>"What's their name again? And where do they live exactly? What do you think would be their biggest concern about going solar? And do you think they'd be open to talking about it?"</PullQuote>

</Step>

<Step number={5} title="Commitment & Thank You (2 min)">

<PullQuote>"I really appreciate you taking the time. You're awesome. I'm going to go knock on [Neighbor's Name]'s door and tell them you recommended us. If you think of anyone else, feel free to reach out to me directly. Here's my card. And hey — if there's ever anything you need with your system, you know who to call."</PullQuote>

</Step>
</Steps>

<Accordion title="Complete Example Conversation">

<Callout type="insight" title="STUDY THIS FLOW">
Notice the pacing — gratitude first, deepening second, referral harvest third.
</Callout>

**YOU:** "Hey, what's up? How's your day going?"

**HOMEOWNER:** "Good, good, just finished some yard work."

**YOU:** "Nice! I'm [Name] with KIN Home. I'm following up on solar with everyone in the neighborhood. Since you've got a system, I had a quick question — mind if I ask?"

**HOMEOWNER:** "Sure, yeah."

**YOU:** "First, thank you for going solar — that was a great call. I'm curious — what are 2-3 of your favorite parts of your experience with us?"

**HOMEOWNER:** "Well, the savings have been amazing. We went from $180 a month to like $50. And the guys were really professional — they didn't make a mess. Plus knowing we're doing something good for the environment."

**YOU:** "That's awesome — $180 to $50 is huge. Tell me more about the savings — how quickly did you notice that?"

**HOMEOWNER:** "First bill after they activated it. Like, it was immediate."

**YOU:** "That must have felt good. When did you get it done?"

**HOMEOWNER:** "About three years ago."

**YOU:** "Three years — so you've been enjoying these savings for a while. So here's my real question — since you've had it, who's asked you about the panels?"

**HOMEOWNER:** "Oh man, definitely my neighbor Tom. He asked me like six months ago. And then the Johnsons across the street asked too."

**YOU:** "Nice. And what do you think is holding them back?"

**HOMEOWNER:** "Tom's worried about the cost. The Johnsons are just thinking about it."

**YOU:** "That makes sense. Do you mind if I go knock on Tom's door and tell him you said it's been awesome?"

**HOMEOWNER:** "Yeah, for sure. Go talk to him."

**YOU:** "Perfect. What's Tom's last name, and what's his address?"

**HOMEOWNER:** "Tom Richardson, it's the blue house right there."

**YOU:** "Got it. Thanks so much for your time — you're awesome. Here's my card in case you think of anyone else."

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Referred Door Knock">

<Callout type="important" title="THIS CHANGES EVERYTHING">
Once you have a referral, your first knock on that neighbor's door is completely different.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Hey, how's your day going? I'm [Name] with KIN Home. I actually just talked to [Previous Customer] right there — and they told me you might be interested in solar? Do you have two minutes for a quick question?"</PullQuote>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Name the Referral Source Immediately">Social proof from sentence one.</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Reference Where They Are">Point at the house — makes it real.</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Assume Interest Gently">"Might be interested" — not pushy.</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Ask for Time, Not Permission">More confident frame.</Step>
</Steps>

<KeyTerm term="The Shift">You're not cold knocking anymore. You're a trusted friend of their neighbor.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Mistakes to Avoid">

| Mistake | Wrong | Right |
|---------|-------|-------|
| **Too salesy** | "Hey! You need solar! Can I schedule you?" | "I just talked to Tom. He loves his system. What do you think about solar?" |
| **Pressuring** | "Who should I talk to? I need three names." | "Who's asked you about the panels? No pressure if no one has." |
| **Not using their name** | "I talked to your neighbor and he said to talk to you." | "I just talked to Tom Richardson and he said you'd asked about solar." |
| **Forgetting to thank** | Get referral and leave | Thank them for their time, their business, and their referral |
| **Rushing** | "Tell me your favorite parts quickly." | Listen fully, take notes, let them elaborate |

</Accordion>
