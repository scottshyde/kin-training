<Accordion title="How Solar Actually Works (Simple Version)">
### The Basic Concept

Solar power is simple: Sunlight hits panels, electricity comes out. That electricity goes to your home or back to the grid.

### The Complete Cycle

1. **Panels capture sunlight** - Photons hit silicon, electrons get excited
2. **DC power is produced** - Panels produce direct current (DC) electricity
3. **Inverter converts to AC** - Converts DC to alternating current (AC) for the home
4. **Power goes to your home first** - Appliances use solar power immediately
5. **Excess goes to the grid** - Extra power flows back to SDGE (they credit you)
6. **At night, you pull from grid** - When sun goes down, you use grid power
7. **Net metering credits you** - SDGE tallies consumption vs. production

### What the Customer Experiences

- Day: Their home runs on solar power, SDGE bill is reduced
- Night: Their home draws from grid, uses credits earned during day
- Month: SDGE bill shows net consumption (usage minus solar production)
- Year: Seasonal variations (more production in summer, less in winter)
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Key Solar Concepts Every Leader Should Know">
### Concept 1: kWh (Kilowatt-Hour)

**What it is:**
- Unit of electrical energy
- 1 kW used for 1 hour = 1 kWh
- How your electricity is measured

**Why it matters:**
- SDGE charges by kWh ($0.15 per kWh, for example)
- Solar is measured by kWh production (how much it makes)
- System size is measured in kW (8kW system = can produce 8 kilowatts at peak)
- Usage + solar offset = customer savings

**In sales:**
> "Your bill shows you use 1,200 kWh per month. Our system will produce about 1,000 kWh per month. That's 80% offset, which means 80% savings."

---

### Concept 2: Net Metering

**What it is:**
- System where excess solar goes back to the grid
- SDGE credits you for that excess power
- You get a "bank" of credits you can use later

**Why it matters:**
- Customer can produce more than they use (in summer)
- Use those credits later (in winter)
- Enables 100% offset systems
- Different from other states (some don't have net metering)

**In sales:**
> "In summer, you produce more than you use. That excess goes back to SDGE, and they credit you for it. In winter, when you use more, you use those credits. That's how you get year-round benefit from solar."

---

### Concept 3: PPA vs. Loan vs. Cash

**PPA (Power Purchase Agreement):**
- You don't own the system, you buy the power it makes
- KIN owns it, you pay per kWh
- $0 down, 25 years, includes maintenance

**Loan:**
- You own the system from day one
- You make monthly payments for 7-15 years
- Get 30% federal tax credit
- Build equity in an asset

**Cash:**
- Pay full price upfront
- Own immediately
- Get federal tax credit
- Maximum long-term savings

**Why it matters:**
- Different customers need different options
- Your job is matching customer to best option
- Each has different financing, tax implications, and long-term value

---

### Concept 4: System Size vs. Consumption

**System Size:**
- Measured in kW (kilowatts)
- 5kW, 8kW, 10kW systems are common
- Bigger = more production, more cost, more savings

**Consumption:**
- Measured in kWh per month
- Customer's bill shows this
- System should be sized to offset 75-100% of consumption

**The Match:**
> "Your house uses 1,500 kWh per month. An 8kW system will produce about 1,200 kWh per month (in San Diego). That's 80% offset - perfect for you."

---

### Concept 5: SDGE Rate Increases

**Historical increases:**
- SDGE rates have increased 5-8% annually
- Not slowing down (energy demand is rising)
- Climate and grid updates driving cost up

**Why it matters:**
- Creates urgency for solar
- Locks in savings
- Solar rate doesn't increase (in PPA) as much
- ROI improves as utility rates rise

**In sales:**
> "SDGE is increasing rates 18% this year. Over 25 years, your rates will probably triple. With our PPA, your rate is locked at 3.5% increase annually. That difference is your long-term savings."

---

### Concept 6: Battery Storage (Optional)

**What it is:**
- Stores excess solar production
- Allows you to use solar power at night
- Provides backup power during outages

**Why it matters:**
- Increases self-consumption (less grid dependence)
- Increases savings (use cheap solar instead of expensive night rates)
- Adds resilience (backup during outages)
- Expensive upfront ($8,000-15,000)

**In sales:**
> "Most customers start with solar only. Some add batteries for maximum savings and backup power. It depends on their priorities and budget."
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="SDGE-Specific Knowledge">
### How SDGE Interconnection Works

**Timeline:**
1. System installed and inspected by city
2. KIN submits interconnection request to SDGE
3. SDGE reviews (typically 2-6 weeks)
4. SDGE approves and installs new meter (if needed)
5. System goes "live" and starts offsetting bill

**What happens at go-live:**
- System is officially connected to grid
- Your production starts counting
- Bills show solar offset immediately
- Customer sees savings on next bill

**Your role:**
- Explain this timeline to customers
- Manage expectations (SDGE timelines vary)
- Reassure them during the waiting period

---

### NEM 3.0 (Net Energy Metering 3.0)

**What changed:**
- California modified net metering rules (effective 2023)
- Reduces credit for excess solar sent to grid
- Makes solar still valuable but changes ROI slightly
- Affects system design (shouldn't go over 100% offset)

**Why it matters:**
- Affects customer savings math
- Shapes system sizing recommendations
- Still makes solar economically viable in California
- Important to understand for positioning

**In sales:**
> "California changed net metering rules recently. Solar is still an amazing investment, but it changed how we calculate the credit for excess power you send back to the grid. Let me show you how this impacts your specific numbers."

---

### Time of Use (TOU) Rates

**What they are:**
- SDGE charges different rates at different times
- Peak hours (4-9 PM): Highest rates
- Off-peak hours: Lower rates
- Super-peak (summer afternoons): Sometimes highest

**Why it matters:**
- Solar production peaks in mid-day (when rates are lower)
- Evening consumption (when rates are peak) isn't covered by solar
- Affects ROI calculations
- Important for battery value (stores peak-production power for peak-rate evening)

**In sales:**
> "Your bill has time-of-use rates. Solar production peaks at midday when rates are lower. If you want to offset your peak evening rates, batteries become valuable."
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Equipment Knowledge for Leaders">
### Panel Brands and Quality Tiers

**Premium (Recommend for most):**
- LG, Sunpower, Panasonic
- 20-22% efficiency
- Better warranties
- Better customer satisfaction

**Mid-tier (Good value):**
- Canadian Solar, JinkoSolar, JA Solar
- 18-20% efficiency
- Solid warranty
- Good for price-conscious customers

**Budget (Avoid):**
- Unknown brands, cheap panels
- 15-17% efficiency
- Poor warranty support
- Bad for KIN reputation

**Your role:** Know what KIN uses and why it's better than cheap alternatives.

---

### Inverter Types

**String Inverters (Most common):**
- One central inverter
- Lower cost
- Simpler installation
- Adequate for most homes

**Micro-inverters (Premium):**
- Each panel has its own inverter
- Better performance in shade
- More expensive
- Best monitoring

**Hybrid Inverters (With batteries):**
- Handles solar + battery system
- More complex
- Required if battery is added

**Your role:** Understand the tradeoffs and why KIN chooses what it chooses.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Business Case for Solar">
### The ROI Model

**Simple breakdown:**
- System cost: $20,000
- Federal tax credit: -$6,000 (30%)
- Net cost: $14,000
- Annual savings: $1,800 (varies by system and location)
- Payback period: 7-8 years
- Additional savings for next 17 years: $30,600+
- 25-year return: 219% (or 12-15% annual return)

**Why it's compelling:**
- Better than most investments (stock market averages 10%)
- Guaranteed by electrical needs (people always need power)
- Protected by 25-year warranty
- Increases home value

<Accordion title="Common Technical Questions You'll Face">
**Q: "Do panels work on cloudy days?"**

A: "Yes, absolutely. They produce about 25-50% of rated output on cloudy days. San Diego has so many sunny days that annual production is still excellent."

---

**Q: "What about when it's super hot?"**

A: "Panels are slightly less efficient in heat - they produce about 10% less on 110 degree days. But our panels have good temperature ratings, so the impact is minimized."

---

**Q: "Can I expand the system later?"**

A: "Yes, though it's easier to design the right size upfront. Expansions are possible but require new permits."

---

**Q: "What if my roof needs replacing?"**

A: "That's why we assess roof condition before installing. If it's old, we recommend replacing the roof first. Then solar goes on the new roof."
</Accordion>
