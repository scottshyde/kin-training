# Daily Routine

> *"Show me your calendar and bank statement and I'll show you what you really value."*  
> — Peter Drucker

Your daily routine is your real performance system. Not the pitch. Not the technique. What you do between 7am and 9pm — and in what order — determines what you're capable of at the door.

This is the recommended structure. Adapt it to your life, but don't lose the non-negotiables.

---

## Monday–Friday

### 7:00 AM — Wake

No snooze. The day you establish that the alarm is a suggestion is the day your discipline starts slipping.

### 7:00–8:00 AM — Power Hour

This is the foundational hour. Do all of these:

- **Affirmations** — read or say your DMP out loud. Keep them present.
- **Oil swish** (oil pulling — oral health and morning focus)
- **Cold shower** — activates the nervous system and builds mental toughness
- **Reading** — 15-20 minutes of anything that builds your mindset or skill set
- **Water** — 16+ oz before anything else
- **Pack the car** — materials, iPad, charger, whatever you need for the day

The Power Hour sets your state. Reps who skip it show up to their first door at a deficit.

### 8:00–9:00 AM — Workout

Physical training is non-negotiable. See GRIT for why. Anything counts — gym, run, bike. The point is movement before the day starts.

### 9:00–10:00 AM — Meal / Shower

Eat real food. Shower. Get ready like the day matters, because it does.

### 10:00 AM–12:00 PM — Power Hours / Pipeline

- Review your weekly plan
- Respond to emails and texts
- Follow up with pipeline (leads, confirms, booked appointments)
- Any pre-work for the day's knocking

### 12:00–1:00 PM — Team Meeting (Monday/Friday)

Attendance required. See Standards for acceptable absences.

### 2:00–4:00 PM — Signs of Life

This is warm-up knocking. Not prime time, but not wasted time either. Work the easier part of your territory. Get your reps in. Shake off any stiffness.

### 4:00–4:30 PM — Reset

Mental and physical reset before prime time. Brief break, food, water. Don't let yourself drag into the best hours of the day.

### 4:30–8:00 PM — Prime Time

This is the most valuable window of the day. Homeowners are home from work. Decisions get made in the evening. Protect this time at all costs.

No social media. No long texts. No errands. You are at doors during this window.

### 8:00–8:30 PM — After Dark / "Come Back Soon"

As it gets dark, some doors close. Use this window to leave notes or cards for homes where no one answered during prime time. "Sorry I missed you — I'll swing back by" cards convert surprisingly well.

---

## Saturday — The Best Day

Saturday is the highest-performing day of the week. Homeowners are home, relaxed, and have time. Protect your Saturday.

- **9:00 AM** — Brick Day (team meeting / accountability check-in)
- **9:45 AM–2:00 PM** — Prime Time

Saturday afternoon has the best conversion rates of the week. Show up.

---

## Sunday — Recharge and Evaluate

Sunday is not a day off from your goals — it's a day to reset and plan.

- Recharge physically and mentally
- Evaluate the week: what did you hit, what did you miss
- Set goals for the coming week
- Plan your territory
- Weekly planning worksheet (see Weekly Planning article)

---

## Non-Negotiables

Regardless of how you customize your routine:

1. **Power Hour happens before doors**
2. **Prime time (4:30–8pm) is protected**
3. **Saturday is not optional**
4. **Sunday review happens**

The reps who follow this structure consistently outperform those who don't — not because the schedule is magic, but because consistency over time compounds.
