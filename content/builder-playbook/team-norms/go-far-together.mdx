# GO FAR, TOGETHER

The phrase isn't decorative. It's a statement of operating philosophy: individual excellence alone is insufficient. The team goes farther together than any one person goes alone — but only if every person shows up to the team the right way.

These are the standards for how we operate together.

<Accordion title="Meeting Standards">
Meetings are the connective tissue of the team. They're where accountability is maintained, skills are sharpened, and culture is reinforced.

**Nothing scheduled during meetings.**
If you've booked something during meeting time, you've prioritized something else over your team. Plan around meetings — not the other way around.

**No phones or screens during meetings.**
Presence is the minimum contribution. Half-presence — body in the room, mind on a screen — is worse than absence because it poisons the attention of everyone around you.

**Attendance means showing up to tend to something.**
The Latin root of "attend" is *attendere* — to stretch toward, to pay attention. If you're there but not attending, you're not really there.

**On time, focused, and prepared.**
On time means before it starts. Prepared means you've reviewed any pre-work. Focused means you're ready to be in the room.

**Handwritten notes.**
Studies consistently show that handwriting activates deeper encoding than typing. Retention is approximately 40% higher. Bring a notebook.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="No Call / No Show Policy">
Two no-call/no-shows to meetings = you're off the team.

This isn't punitive. It's a signal. When someone doesn't show up and doesn't communicate, they've communicated something — that the team doesn't hold the weight it should in their world. Two times makes the pattern clear.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Language in Meetings">
**Minimize cursing.**
Not because anyone is offended, but because it's a proxy for precision. People who curse frequently in professional settings are often using strong language to cover weak thinking. Say the exact thing you mean.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Culture Underneath the Standards">
Standards exist to protect something. The thing they're protecting here is: a room full of people who are trying to build something real and don't have time to carry people who aren't.

A team where everyone meets the standard is a team where the standard itself becomes invisible — because it's just how everyone operates. That team compounds. Results compound. Culture compounds.

GO FAR, TOGETHER means you don't have to do this alone. It also means you can't do it while leaving your team behind.
</Accordion>
