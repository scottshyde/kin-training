# GO FAR, TOGETHER

<Lead>The phrase isn't decorative. It's a statement of operating philosophy: individual excellence alone is insufficient. The team goes farther together than any one person goes alone — but only if every person shows up to the team the right way.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Meeting Standards">

<Callout type="important" title="THE CONNECTIVE TISSUE OF THE TEAM">
Meetings are where accountability is maintained, skills are sharpened, and culture is reinforced.
</Callout>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Nothing Scheduled During Meetings">
If you've booked something during meeting time, you've prioritized something else over your team.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="No Phones or Screens">
Half-presence — body in the room, mind on a screen — is worse than absence because it poisons the attention of everyone around you.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Attendance Means Tending To Something">
The Latin root of "attend" is *attendere* — to stretch toward, to pay attention. If you're there but not attending, you're not really there.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="On Time, Focused, Prepared">
On time means before it starts. Prepared means you've reviewed pre-work. Focused means you're ready to be in the room.
</Step>
<Step number={5} title="Handwritten Notes">
Retention is approximately **40% higher** than typing. Bring a notebook.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="No Call / No Show Policy">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "2", label: "No-Call/No-Shows = Off the Team" }
]} />

<KeyTerm term="The Signal">When someone doesn't show up and doesn't communicate, they've communicated something — that the team doesn't hold the weight it should in their world. Two times makes the pattern clear.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Language in Meetings">

<Callout type="tip" title="MINIMIZE CURSING">
Not because anyone is offended, but because it's a proxy for precision. People who curse frequently in professional settings are often using strong language to cover weak thinking. **Say the exact thing you mean.**
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Culture Underneath the Standards">

<KeyTerm term="What Standards Protect">Standards exist to protect something. The thing they're protecting here is: a room full of people who are trying to build something real and don't have time to carry people who aren't.</KeyTerm>

<Callout type="insight">
A team where everyone meets the standard is a team where the standard itself becomes invisible — because it's just how everyone operates. That team compounds. Results compound. Culture compounds.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"GO FAR, TOGETHER means you don't have to do this alone. It also means you can't do it while leaving your team behind."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>
