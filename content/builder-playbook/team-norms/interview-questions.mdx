# Interview Questions & Recruiting Scorecard

Recruiting is one of the highest-leverage activities a team leader can do. One A-player who runs for three years generates more revenue and less headache than five mediocre hires. The interview is where you find them.

---

## Target Profile

Look for athletes. Specifically: wrestlers, rowers, crew members, competitive swimmers, distance runners. People who have been trained by a coach to do something hard, in the presence of discomfort, with a team depending on them.

Sports build the three traits you need in a solar rep before they ever touch a door.

---

## The Three Traits

### 1. Grit
Will they persist when it's hard? When doors slam? When a week goes sideways? Grit is the foundation. Without it, no amount of skill matters.

### 2. Personable
Can they connect with strangers quickly and genuinely? Not performed warmth — real warmth. Homeowners know the difference.

### 3. Activator
Do they initiate? Do they act without being told? Activators don't wait for permission or perfect conditions. They go.

---

## Grit Questions

> *"What does your daily routine look like right now?"*

*(Looking for: structure, intentionality, early rising, physical training.)*

> *"What's an area of your life where you've been particularly disciplined?"*

> *"Tell me about the most challenging goal you've pursued. How long did it take? What got in the way?"*

> *"Tell me about a time you wanted to quit something but didn't. What kept you going?"*

> *"What do you think separates you from other people your age?"*

---

## Personable Questions

> *"How do you adjust your communication style for different types of people?"*

> *"If someone came to the door and said 'not interested' — walk me through how you'd respond."*

> *"How do you make someone feel comfortable when you've just met them?"*

> *"If I called your last employer right now, what would they say about how you treat people?"*

---

## Activator Questions

> *"Tell me about the biggest personal goal you've ever set for yourself. What did you sacrifice to pursue it?"*

> *"On a scale of 1-10, how lucky are you in life? Explain your answer."*

*(High performers almost always answer 8-10 and credit their own choices. Low performers answer 4-6 and attribute outcomes to circumstance.)*

> *"Give me an example of a time you did something completely outside the box to solve a problem."*

> *"Tell me about a time you took initiative without being asked."*

> *"If I asked you to rank family, finances, health, relationships, and personal development in order of how much you actually invest in them right now — not how you wish you did — what would that order be?"*

*(This question reveals self-awareness. Honest, reflective answers are the goal.)*

---

## Red Flags

End the interview early if you observe:

- No transportation
- Many jobs held for short periods
- Poor hygiene or disheveled appearance
- Timid energy — soft handshake, avoiding eye contact
- Short, one-word answers
- No competitive history (sports, debate, student government — anything)
- Zero eye contact throughout
- Unexplained gaps in employment
- Entitlement language: "I expect," "I deserve," "I was owed"

These aren't character judgments — they're hiring filters. You're looking for a specific profile.

---

## The Recruiting Scorecard

Score each trait 0–3 on three sub-dimensions:

| Trait | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
|-------|---|---|---|---|
| **Grit** | No evidence | One example | Consistent pattern | Exceptional, defined by it |
| **Personable** | Awkward or cold | Adequate | Naturally warm | Magnetic |
| **Activator** | Waits to be told | Follows willingly | Often initiates | Relentlessly self-directed |

**Total Scoring:**
- 8–9 points: **A-Player** — prioritize immediately
- 6–7 points: **B-Player** — strong hire with coaching
- 4–5 points: **C-Player** — borderline, consider carefully
- 0–3 points: **P-Player** — pass

---

## Part-Time Exceptions

For part-time candidates, the bar doesn't lower — the schedule accommodates. Part-time reps must:

- Attend all weekly meetings
- Work one full weekend day
- Hit 4–6 productive hours per week minimum
- Score 7–9 on the recruiting scorecard

Partial commitment with a low score is a recipe for a rep who consumes attention without producing results.
