# Standards

<Lead>Standards aren't restrictions — they're the conditions under which serious people can do serious work together. Knowing the standard protects everyone: the team, the rep, and the homeowners we're serving.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Two-Strike Policy">

<Callout type="warning" title="CONDUCT VIOLATIONS">
KIN Home operates on a two-strike manner for conduct violations. The first serious lapse is addressed directly. The second ends the relationship.
</Callout>

<KeyTerm term="Not Punitive">This is a signal that we take both our team culture and our reputation in the field seriously.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Meeting Standards">

<Callout type="important" title="ATTENDANCE IS REQUIRED">
"Attendance" comes from the Latin *attendere* — to tend to. You can't tend to something you're not present for.
</Callout>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Nothing Scheduled During Meetings">
Block the time. Protect it.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="No Phones or Screens">
Full attention.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="New Reps: Every Meeting for 10 Weeks">
No exceptions.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="On Time, Focused, Prepared">
Ready to contribute.
</Step>
<Step number={5} title="Handwritten Notes">
Retention is **40% higher** than typed.
</Step>
</Steps>

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "2", label: "No-Call/No-Shows = Off the Team" }
]} />

**Acceptable absences** (must be communicated in advance): Funeral or immediate family death, giving birth, documented family emergency, pre-arranged vacation (2 weeks notice).

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Minimum Closer Standards">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "≥40%", label: "Closing % Across 10+ Deals" },
  { value: ">80%", label: "Throughput" }
]} />

<Callout type="warning">
A rep receiving leads and not meeting these standards is a drag on both the setter's pipeline and the team's economics. These are **minimums, not targets**.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="People of Character (PPW)">

<KeyTerm term="The Standard">PPW — People of Character. This is the filter for who gets to be on this team and represent KIN in the field.</KeyTerm>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Do What You Say">Follow through on commitments.</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Tell the Truth">Including about your numbers.</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Treat Homeowners with Genuine Respect">Every door.</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Show Up Prepared">Materials, script, energy.</Step>
<Step number={5} title="Own Mistakes">Without blame-shifting.</Step>
<Step number={6} title="Compete Hard">Without tearing others down.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="insight">
This isn't idealism — it's a practical filter. Reps without character create problems that cost the team more than their deals are worth. We'd rather have **fewer people who are trustworthy** than more people who aren't.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Standards and Self-Respect">

<PullQuote>"Every standard on this list is also an act of self-respect. The rep who shows up on time, does the Power Hour, hits their door count, and tells the truth is building something — not just a paycheck, but a professional identity."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="When No One Is Watching">The reps who've built real careers in this industry did it by holding themselves to a standard when no one was watching. That's the version of standards that matters.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>
