# Team Norms: The Standards That Build Great Teams

## What Are Team Norms?

Team norms are the unwritten rules and standards of excellence that define how your team operates. They're not rules you're forced to follow - they're agreements you make with your teammates about what kind of team you want to be.

At KOTA, our team norms define:
- How we treat each other
- How we approach our work
- How we measure success
- How we support one another
- How we hold each other accountable

---

## Core Team Norms at KOTA

### Norm 1: Accountability Always

**What it means:**
- We own our results
- We don't make excuses
- We acknowledge mistakes quickly
- We commit publicly to goals
- We follow through on what we say

**In practice:**
- Miss a door goal? Own it, analyze why, adjust tomorrow
- Close a deal? Share the win and how you did it
- Didn't follow the word track? Acknowledge it and recommit
- Miss a team meeting? Let everyone know why and when you'll be back
- Something's not working? Raise your hand, don't hide it

**Why it matters:**
- Accountability is how we improve
- Teams that own results outperform teams that make excuses
- Your accountability makes everyone around you better

---

### Norm 2: Daily Door Discipline

**What it means:**
- We show up every day ready to knock
- We don't skip appointments
- We don't sleep in on blitz days
- We're at the first door before 10:30 AM
- We're not on our phone when we should be knocking

**In practice:**
- Monday-Saturday: You're at the first door on time, every time
- You come prepared: bill, materials, confidence
- You dress professionally (KOTA branded gear or professional dress)
- You show up even when you're tired
- You don't call in sick to dodge a difficult week

**Why it matters:**
- Consistency beats talent
- Your neighbors see the same rep every week = trust
- Door discipline is the foundation of every success
- People who skip doors never build momentum

---

### Norm 3: System Execution Over Improvisation

**What it means:**
- We follow the proven playbooks
- We memorize word tracks instead of improvising
- We don't skip steps in the process
- We use the same approach that's been proven
- We trust the system until we prove something better

**In practice:**
- You memorize needs audit questions - don't freestyle them
- You use the door approach script - don't make up your own version
- You follow the solidification process - don't skip steps
- You use the overcoming objections playsets - don't wing it
- If you think you have a better way, test it with your manager first

**Why it matters:**
- The system is proven (other reps use it successfully)
- Consistency compounds: same message, same approach, better results
- When we all follow the same system, we can coach and help each other
- Innovation happens in coaching sessions, not at the door

---

### Norm 4: Coachability

**What it means:**
- We receive feedback without defensiveness
- We ask for help when we're stuck
- We implement coaching quickly
- We celebrate corrections (they mean growth)
- We don't know it all - there's always room to improve

**In practice:**
- Your manager gives you feedback? You listen, you implement, you report back
- Peer offers a suggestion? You try it and report results
- You miss 5 doors? You ask for a coaching session on the process
- Close rate drops? You raise your hand and ask what's wrong
- Someone better than you? You study them and learn from them

**Why it matters:**
- Your best growth comes from feedback
- Defensive people don't improve
- Coachable people become top performers
- Teams that coach each other outperform teams that don't

---

### Norm 5: Celebrating Wins (Yours and Others')

**What it means:**
- We acknowledge great work in front of the team
- We celebrate appointments set, deals closed, and goals hit
- We cheer for our teammates' success
- We share techniques that work
- We don't diminish others' success with sarcasm

**In practice:**
- You set 12 appointments in a day? The team celebrates it
- Teammate closes a deal? You genuinely congratulate them
- Someone has a great week? It gets mentioned at the meeting
- You discover a great word track? You share it with the team
- You hit a monthly goal? The team acknowledges it

**Why it matters:**
- Celebration builds momentum
- Your wins are team wins
- High-performing teams celebrate progress
- Acknowledgment builds culture
- Celebrating others costs nothing and gains everything

---

### Norm 6: Zero Gossip Culture

**What it means:**
- We don't talk about teammates behind their backs
- We handle conflicts directly and respectfully
- We assume good intent
- We don't spread rumors or negativity
- We bring concerns to leadership, not the team

**In practice:**
- If you have an issue with someone, you talk to them or your manager
- You don't complain about a teammate to another teammate
- You don't assume bad intent - you ask questions
- You don't participate in negative conversations
- You redirect gossip: "I don't think that's fair, talk to them directly"

**Why it matters:**
- Gossip destroys team culture
- Trust is built on directness and honesty
- Negative culture spreads faster than positive culture
- Great teams have low drama
- You can't build trust while talking behind people's backs

---

### Norm 7: Lead By Example

**What it means:**
- You don't ask others to do what you won't do
- You dress professional, you expect professionals
- You follow the system, others follow it too
- You show vulnerability, others do too
- You're coachable, others become coachable

**In practice:**
- You're not just asking others to hit door goals - you're hitting them first
- Your scripts are tight because you practiced them
- You handle rejection well, so your team handles rejection well
- You're at meetings early, so everyone is
- You share your failures, so others share theirs

**Why it matters:**
- Leading by example is the only leadership that works
- People do what you do, not what you say
- Culture flows from the top
- Your credibility comes from walking the talk

---

### Norm 8: Radical Honesty

**What it means:**
- We tell the truth, even when it's uncomfortable
- We don't sugarcoat results
- We're transparent about challenges
- We admit mistakes immediately
- We don't pretend to understand when we don't

**In practice:**
- Your manager asks how it went? You tell the truth about what happened
- You don't understand something? You say "I don't understand, can you explain?"
- You made a mistake on a pitch? You admit it and identify what to fix
- Deal fell through? You're honest about why
- You're struggling? You say so instead of pretending you're fine

**Why it matters:**
- Honesty builds trust faster than anything else
- Problems hidden become bigger problems
- Radical honesty allows rapid problem-solving
- You can't improve what you won't acknowledge
- Truth allows the team to help you

---

### Norm 9: Never Settle for Mediocrity

**What it means:**
- Good is not enough
- "We'll try our best" is not our standard
- We aim for excellence in everything
- We don't make excuses for mediocre work
- We raise the bar constantly

**In practice:**
- Your word track is sloppy? You practice until it's tight
- Your appointment show rate is 70%? That's not acceptable (aim for 90%+)
- Your close rate is average? Figure out why and improve it
- Your follow-up is inconsistent? Standardize it
- Your systems are outdated? Update them

**Why it matters:**
- Mediocre teams accept mediocre results
- Excellence is a habit, not a moment
- Your standard sets the team's standard
- Customers feel the difference between good and excellent
- Excellence compounds - excellent this week builds excellent next week

---

### Norm 10: Support Each Other

**What it means:**
- We're not competing against each other - we're competing for the customer
- We share leads if it helps the team
- We cover for each other when someone's struggling
- We mentor newer reps
- We're genuinely happy about each other's success

**In practice:**
- Colleague has a bad week? You check in and encourage them
- New rep is struggling with closes? You practice with them
- You found a great neighborhood for doors? You share the location
- Team member needs help at a job? You're willing to assist
- Colleague gets an amazing appointment? You're genuinely excited for them

**Why it matters:**
- Divided teams underperform
- A rising tide lifts all boats
- Supporting others creates a culture where you're also supported
- The best teams are characterized by genuine camaraderie
- Competition between teammates destroys culture

---

## Living the Norms: Enforcement & Accountability

Team norms only matter if they're actually enforced. Here's how:

### The Leadership Role
- Managers model the norms first
- Managers acknowledge when someone violates a norm
- Managers correct privately and specifically
- Managers celebrate when norms are upheld
- Managers are willing to have hard conversations

### The Peer Role
- You hold teammates accountable respectfully
- You call out norm violations directly
- You celebrate norm adherence
- You model the norms yourself
- You're willing to say "That's not how we do things"

### The Individual Role
- You commit to the norms publicly
- You acknowledge when you fall short
- You course-correct immediately
- You don't make excuses
- You hold yourself to a higher standard than anyone else

- You'll be honest and direct
- You'll support your teammates

## The Payoff: Why Great Teams Outperform

When a team lives these norms:
- Close rates increase 20-30%
- Culture becomes legendary
- People want to join the team
- Retention improves dramatically
- Success compounds year-over-year
- Everyone wins more

A great team doesn't happen by accident. It's built on norms that everyone commits to and everyone upholds.

Your job? Be someone who lives these norms and brings others up to that standard.
