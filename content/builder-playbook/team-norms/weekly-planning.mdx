# Weekly Planning

<Lead>The rep who doesn't plan their week lives inside someone else's priorities. Weekly planning is how you take ownership of your time before the week takes ownership of you. This takes 20-30 minutes. Do it every Sunday.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Page 1: Weekly Evaluation">

<Callout type="important" title="BEFORE YOU PLAN FORWARD, EVALUATE BACKWARD">
Be honest. Vague self-critique doesn't produce specific improvement.
</Callout>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Three Wins from Last Week">
Be specific. Not "I knocked a lot" but "I booked four appointments on Wednesday and all four confirmed."
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Two or Three Improvements">
Where did you lose time, miss appointments, or underperform?
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Top 10 Goals / DMP Check">
Read your top 10. Are you moving toward them? Are they still the right goals?
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="90-Second Affirmations">
Read your DMP affirmations out loud. This isn't woo — it's rehearsal. You're priming the mindset.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Page 2: Weekly Plan">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "$3,200", label: "Example — Specific Income Goal" },
  { value: "REWARD", label: "Name It If You Hit It" }
]} />

<KeyTerm term="Not a Range">Not "between $2,000 and $4,000." A single number. The reward doesn't have to be expensive — it has to be real and specific.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Time Management Matrix (Covey Quadrants)">

| | **Urgent** | **Not Urgent** |
|---|------------|----------------|
| **Important** | Q1: Crisis, deadlines, fires | Q2: Planning, relationships, development |
| **Not Important** | Q3: Interruptions, some meetings | Q4: Scrolling, busy work, time wasters |

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Q1 (Important + Urgent)">
Handle these — they can't wait. But minimize them through Q2 work.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Q2 (Important + Not Urgent)">

<Callout type="insight">
**This is where performance is built.** Pipeline review, skill practice, DMP work, health. Neglecting Q2 is what creates Q1 crises.
</Callout>
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Q3 (Not Important + Urgent)">
Minimize. Delegate. These feel important because they're urgent, but they rarely move the needle.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Q4 (Not Important + Not Urgent)">
**Eliminate.** Social media during prime hours, long unproductive conversations, distraction. This is where hours disappear.
</Step>
</Steps>

<KeyTerm term="Your Goal">Maximize Q2 and protect prime time from Q3 and Q4.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Daily Assignments">

For each day, identify: **Q1 items** (must happen), **Q2 items** (proactive investment), **Q3 items** (batch or minimize), and your door target.

<Callout type="warning">
A plan that lives in your head is not a plan — it's a hope. **Write this out.**
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Planning Discipline">

<PullQuote>"The rep who does this consistently for 90 days will look back and not recognize their former scattered self. The discipline of evaluation and intentional planning compounds the same way interest compounds — slowly at first, then dramatically."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="The Math">Twenty minutes on Sunday buys you twenty hours of clarity during the week.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>
