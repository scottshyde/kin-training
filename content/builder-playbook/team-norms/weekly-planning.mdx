# Weekly Planning

The rep who doesn't plan their week lives inside someone else's priorities. Weekly planning is how you take ownership of your time before the week takes ownership of you.

This takes 20–30 minutes. Do it every Sunday.

---

## Page 1: Weekly Evaluation

Before you plan forward, evaluate backward. Be honest.

**Three wins from last week:**
What actually went well? Be specific. Not "I knocked a lot" but "I booked four appointments on Wednesday and all four confirmed."

**Two or three improvements:**
What didn't work? Where did you lose time, miss appointments, or underperform? Be specific here too. Vague self-critique doesn't produce specific improvement.

**Top 10 goals / DMP check:**
Pull out your Definite Major Purpose list. Read the top 10. Are you moving toward them? Are they still the right goals? This keeps your week connected to your longer vision.

**90-second affirmations:**
Read your DMP affirmations out loud. This isn't woo — it's rehearsal. You're priming the mindset for the week.

---

## Page 2: Weekly Plan

**Specific income goal for the week:**
Not a range — a number. "I want to make between $2,000 and $4,000 this week" is not a goal. "$3,200 this week" is a goal.

**Reward if you hit it:**
Name something specific. The reward doesn't have to be expensive — it has to be real. If you hit $3,200, you get Friday dinner at the restaurant you've been wanting to try. Specificity makes it motivating.

## Time Management Matrix (Covey Quadrants)

Organize your week's activities into four categories:

| | **Urgent** | **Not Urgent** |
|---|------------|----------------|
| **Important** | Q1: Crisis, deadlines, fires | Q2: Planning, relationships, development |
| **Not Important** | Q3: Interruptions, some meetings | Q4: Scrolling, busy work, time wasters |

**Q1 (Important + Urgent):** Handle these — they can't wait. But minimize them through Q2 work.

**Q2 (Important + Not Urgent):** This is where performance is built. Pipeline review, skill practice, DMP work, health. Neglecting Q2 is what creates Q1 crises.

**Q3 (Not Important + Urgent):** Minimize. Delegate where possible. These feel important because they're urgent, but they rarely move the needle.

**Q4 (Not Important + Not Urgent):** Eliminate. Social media scrolling during prime hours, long unproductive conversations, distraction. These are where hours disappear.

Assign your daily activities for the week into their quadrants before the week starts. Your goal is to maximize Q2 and protect prime time from Q3 and Q4.

---

## Daily Assignments

For each day of the week, identify:
- **Q1 items** — what has to happen today regardless
- **Q2 items** — what you're investing in proactively
- **Q3 items** — what you'll batch or minimize
- Your door target for the day

Write this out. A plan that lives in your head is not a plan — it's a hope.

---

## The Planning Discipline

The rep who does this consistently for 90 days will look back and not recognize their former scattered self. The discipline of evaluation and intentional planning compounds the same way interest compounds — slowly at first, then dramatically.

Twenty minutes on Sunday buys you twenty hours of clarity during the week.
