# Distributed Energy — Programs & Resources

<Lead>The solar industry has shifted from a simple panel-and-savings pitch to a full energy ecosystem story. Batteries, virtual power plants, and demand-side programs give you a deeper conversation with homeowners and a stronger value proposition.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Key Programs">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="SGIP — Self-Generation Incentive Program">

California's SGIP program provides rebates for behind-the-meter battery storage systems — particularly for customers in **high fire threat districts** or with **medical baseline rates**.

**Resource:** [cpuc.ca.gov](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov) — search SGIP

<Callout type="warning">
SGIP availability varies by utility and funding tier. **Check current status** before promising availability to a homeowner — it goes in and out of funding.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Demand Side Grid Program">

California's demand side grid program incentivizes homeowners who can curtail energy use during peak grid stress periods. Battery-equipped homes are well-positioned to participate.

**Resource:** [energy.ca.gov](https://www.energy.ca.gov) — search Demand Side Grid
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Virtual Power Plant (VPP) — SDGE + Tesla">

Tesla and SDGE operate a VPP program where enrolled Tesla Powerwall customers can have their batteries dispatched during peak grid events **in exchange for compensation**.

<KeyTerm term="The Value Prop">The homeowner gets paid for having a battery that also protects them during outages. One of the cleanest value propositions in the current market.</KeyTerm>

**Resource:** [tesla.com/support/energy/virtual-power-plant/sdge](https://www.tesla.com/support/energy/virtual-power-plant/sdge)
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="How to Use This in the Field">

**At the door:**

<PullQuote>"One thing that's changed recently is that the battery systems we install can actually earn money for the homeowner by participating in the grid. It's a program where SDGE pays you to use your battery during peak hours. Most people don't know that's a thing yet."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
At the sit, the closer walks through SGIP eligibility, VPP enrollment, and full energy economics. Your job as a setter is to **plant the seed** at the door.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Why This Matters Now">

<Callout type="important" title="NEM 3.0 CHANGED EVERYTHING">
Export compensation dropped significantly under NEM 3.0. But **solar + battery** under NEM 3.0 often performs better than solar alone under NEM 2.0 — because the value shifted from export to storage and grid participation.
</Callout>

<KeyTerm term="The Difference">The rep who understands this tells a different story than the one still pitching 2019 solar economics.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>
