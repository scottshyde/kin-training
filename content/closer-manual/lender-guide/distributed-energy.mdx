# Distributed Energy — Programs & Resources

The solar industry has shifted from a simple panel-and-savings pitch to a full energy ecosystem story. Batteries, virtual power plants, and demand-side programs give you a deeper conversation with homeowners and a stronger value proposition.

<Accordion title="Key Programs">
### Self-Generation Incentive Program (SGIP)
California's SGIP program provides rebates for behind-the-meter battery storage systems -- particularly for customers in high fire threat districts or with medical baseline rates.

**Resource:** [cpuc.ca.gov](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov) -- search SGIP

SGIP availability varies by utility and funding tier. Check current status before promising availability to a homeowner -- it goes in and out of funding.

### Demand Side Grid Program
California's demand side grid program incentivizes homeowners who can curtail energy use during peak grid stress periods. Battery-equipped homes are well-positioned to participate.

**Resource:** [energy.ca.gov](https://www.energy.ca.gov) -- search Demand Side Grid

### Virtual Power Plant (VPP) -- SDGE + Tesla
Tesla and SDGE operate a Virtual Power Plant program in which enrolled Tesla Powerwall customers can have their batteries dispatched during peak grid events in exchange for compensation.

This is one of the cleanest value propositions in the current market: the homeowner gets paid for having a battery that also protects them during outages.

**Resource:** [tesla.com/support/energy/virtual-power-plant/sdge](https://www.tesla.com/support/energy/virtual-power-plant/sdge)
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How to Use This in the Field">
You don't need to be an expert on every program -- you need to know they exist and know how to position them.

**At the door:**
> *"One thing that's changed recently is that the battery systems we install can actually earn money for the homeowner by participating in the grid. It's a program where SDGE pays you to use your battery during peak hours. Most people don't know that's a thing yet."*

**At the sit:**
The closer walks through SGIP eligibility, VPP enrollment, and the full energy economics. Your job as a setter is to plant the seed at the door.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Why This Matters Now">
NEM 3.0 changed the economics of solar-only systems. Export compensation dropped significantly. But solar + battery under NEM 3.0 often performs better than solar alone under NEM 2.0 -- because the value has shifted from export to storage and grid participation.

The rep who understands this tells a different story than the one still pitching 2019 solar economics.
</Accordion>
