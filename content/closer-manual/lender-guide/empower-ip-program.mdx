# Empower IP Program

The Empower IP (Installer Program) is one of the financing/partnership structures KIN operates under. Understanding its mechanics helps set accurate expectations on deal economics and payout timing.

> *"We are in the minor leagues."* -- Use this as a mindset frame. The program has guardrails. Learn them. Work within them. Build toward more leverage over time.

<Accordion title="Holdbacks">
Empower retains holdbacks on deals -- amounts kept in reserve until conditions are met. These are not forfeited; they're deferred. Know the holdback structure before committing to rep payouts on deals going through this program.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Platform Fee">
A **$0.20/watt platform fee** applies under the Empower program. This fee is currently **waived** -- but should be treated as a potential cost in deal modeling if that changes.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Equipment Sourcing">
**SNRGY supply** is used for domestic content qualification under the Sunrun side of the program.

**Everbright / Greentech:** Quote using the **Safe Harbor AVL (Approved Vendor List)**. Don't quote off-list equipment for Empower deals -- it creates compliance issues downstream.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Milestone Payment Schedule">
Empower operates on a **pay-when-paid** model -- KIN receives payment when Empower receives payment from its capital source.

| Milestone | Payment |
|-----------|---------|
| Installation complete | **80%** |
| PTO (Permission to Operate) | **20%** |

**Expect a 1-week delay** after each milestone is approved before funds arrive. Plan cash flow accordingly.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Credit Requirements">
**Minimum FICO: 650**

Deals with homeowners below 650 will not be funded through Empower. Qualify credit before running a full proposal to avoid wasted effort and homeowner disappointment.

> Soft credit pulls are available and don't impact the homeowner's score. Always run the soft pull before design.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Positioning This to Homeowners">
Homeowners don't need the details of the installer program. What they need to know:
- The financing is underwritten by an established company
- Their credit won't be impacted by the initial check
- Approval is straightforward for qualifying homeowners
- There's no obligation until they review and sign final documents

Keep the homeowner conversation focused on their outcome, not your back-end mechanics.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Operational Notes">
- Flag Empower deals to operations early -- equipment sourcing and AVL compliance need lead time
- PTO delays from the utility are outside KIN's control -- set homeowner expectations at signing
- Holdback disputes are handled through Empower's dealer relations team -- document everything
</Accordion>
