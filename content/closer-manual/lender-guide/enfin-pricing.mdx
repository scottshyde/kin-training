# EnFin — Pricing & Strategy

EnFin (formerly known under previous branding) repriced in January and became more aggressive in the California market. Understanding their current structure helps you position deals correctly and set accurate expectations on payout timing.

---

## Repricing and Strategy (Current)

EnFin's current model **incentivizes larger systems** — specifically those with batteries and higher sun-hour locations. The economics favor bundled solar + storage deals over panels-only installs.

**Key levers:**
- Battery additions meaningfully improve deal economics
- Higher sun hours (SoCal, inland, desert-adjacent) score better in their model
- System size and storage capacity are rewarded in pricing tier

When structuring a deal, push toward the natural right-size rather than artificially constraining. EnFin's pricing rewards doing the job right.

---

## Energy Communities Adder

Homes located in designated **Energy Communities** (often census tracts meeting income or fossil fuel employment criteria) receive a **10% additional tax credit** on top of the standard ITC.

This is a significant deal-sweetener. Check eligibility at the IRS/Department of Energy tools — Energy Community status can shift a borderline deal into a clear yes.

---

## Domestic Content

EnFin incentivizes the use of domestically manufactured equipment. DC (Domestic Content) payouts are tied to the escalator structure:
- Higher escalator deals have higher DC payout potential
- Payouts occur after the **M3 milestone**

When discussing domestic content with a homeowner, frame it as: *"Part of how we keep the pricing sharp is using American-made equipment — and the financing company actually incentivizes that."*

---

## Milestone Payment Schedule

EnFin operates on a milestone payment structure. Payouts are triggered by project completion stages:

### With Direct Pay:
| Milestone | Percentage |
|-----------|-----------|
| M1 (Contract signed) | 30% |
| M2 (Install complete) | 50% |
| M3 (PTO / Permission to Operate) | 20% |

### Without Direct Pay:
| Milestone | Percentage |
|-----------|-----------|
| Install complete | 80% |
| PTO | 20% |

**Approval to payment:** Approximately **2 business days** after each milestone is approved.

---

## Important Timing Notes

- PTO (Permission to Operate) is issued by the utility — it's not within KIN's control once the system is complete
- Build PTO timelines into homeowner expectations at the sit — some utilities are 4–8 weeks out
- DC payouts (post-M3) come separately from the main milestone schedule

---

## Positioning to Homeowners

Homeowners don't need to know the milestone structure in detail. What they need to know:
- The financing is through a credible, established company
- Approval is quick once they sign
- There's no obligation to proceed until they review and sign the final documents

Keep the homeowner conversation focused on value, not logistics. The milestone structure is for your operational understanding.
