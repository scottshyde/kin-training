# EnFin — Pricing & Strategy

<Lead>EnFin repriced in January and became more aggressive in the California market. Understanding their current structure helps you position deals correctly and set accurate expectations on payout timing.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Repricing and Strategy (Current)">

<Callout type="insight" title="INCENTIVIZES LARGER SYSTEMS">
EnFin's current model rewards battery additions, higher sun-hour locations, and bundled solar + storage deals over panels-only installs.
</Callout>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Battery Additions">
Meaningfully improve deal economics.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Higher Sun Hours">
SoCal, inland, desert-adjacent locations score better.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="System Size + Storage">
Rewarded in pricing tier.
</Step>
</Steps>

<KeyTerm term="The Principle">When structuring a deal, push toward the natural right-size rather than artificially constraining. EnFin's pricing rewards doing the job right.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Energy Communities Adder">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "+10%", label: "Additional Tax Credit" }
]} />

<KeyTerm term="Energy Communities">Homes in designated Energy Communities (census tracts meeting income or fossil fuel employment criteria) receive an additional 10% tax credit on top of the standard ITC.</KeyTerm>

<Callout type="tip">
This is a significant deal-sweetener. Check eligibility at the IRS/DOE tools — Energy Community status can shift a borderline deal into a clear yes.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Domestic Content">

<KeyTerm term="DC Payouts">EnFin incentivizes domestically manufactured equipment. DC payouts are tied to the escalator structure — higher escalator deals have higher DC payout potential. Payouts occur after the M3 milestone.</KeyTerm>

<PullQuote>"Part of how we keep the pricing sharp is using American-made equipment — and the financing company actually incentivizes that."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Milestone Payment Schedule">

**With Direct Pay:**

| Milestone | Percentage |
|-----------|-----------|
| M1 (Contract signed) | **30%** |
| M2 (Install complete) | **50%** |
| M3 (PTO) | **20%** |

**Without Direct Pay:**

| Milestone | Percentage |
|-----------|-----------|
| Install complete | **80%** |
| PTO | **20%** |

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "~2 DAYS", label: "Approval to Payment" }
]} />

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Important Timing Notes">

<Callout type="warning" title="SET EXPECTATIONS">
PTO (Permission to Operate) is issued by the utility — it's not within KIN's control once the system is complete. Some utilities are **4-8 weeks out**. Build this into homeowner expectations at the sit.
</Callout>

<KeyTerm term="DC Payouts">Come separately from the main milestone schedule, post-M3.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>
