# Financing Options: PPA, Loan, Lease, and Cash

<Lead>Every solar customer falls into one of four financing categories. Your job as a closer is to understand each option deeply, guide the customer to the best choice, and present it with confidence.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Option 1: PPA (Power Purchase Agreement)">

<KeyTerm term="What Is a PPA">KIN finances and owns the system. Customer pays per kWh used — like a utility bill. $0 down, 25-year agreement, KIN maintains everything.</KeyTerm>

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "$0", label: "Down Payment" },
  { value: "~$115/mo", label: "Solar Payment" },
  { value: "$35/mo", label: "Day 1 Savings vs SDGE" },
  { value: "3.5%", label: "Annual Escalator" }
]} />

**Rate Escalator Context:**

| Year | PPA Rate | SDGE Increase |
|------|----------|---------------|
| Year 1 | $0.12/kWh | 5-8%/yr |
| Year 5 | $0.14/kWh | Compounding |
| Year 10 | $0.17/kWh | Still climbing |

<KeyTerm term="Why the Escalator Is Your Friend">3.5% PPA escalator vs 5-8% SDGE increases = savings that grow every single year.</KeyTerm>

**Word Track:**

<PullQuote>"Your SDGE bill is $150/month. With our PPA, your solar payment will be about $115/month. That's $35/month savings immediately, and the savings grow every year as SDGE's rates increase faster than our 3.5% escalator. Plus, there's zero risk. If anything breaks, we fix it."</PullQuote>

**Best For:**

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Credit-Constrained">Can't get loan approval.</Step>
<Step number={2} title="No Cash Available">Don't have savings for down payment.</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Want Simplicity">Prefer paying a bill vs managing investments.</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Maintenance-Averse">Don't want to think about system upkeep.</Step>
</Steps>

**Handling PPA Objections:**

*"I don't like being tied to 25 years"*

<PullQuote>"You're already tied to SDGE. The only difference is now you're paying us a lower rate. Most customers don't think of it as being tied down — they think of it as locking in low rates for 25 years."</PullQuote>

*"The rate going up 3.5% bugs me"*

<PullQuote>"SDGE increases 5-8% every year. You're locking in 3.5%. Year 1 you save $35/month. By year 5, $55/month. By year 10, $90/month. The escalator is actually your friend."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Option 2: Loan">

<KeyTerm term="What Is a Loan">Customer finances the purchase and owns the system immediately. Gets the 30% federal ITC. Monthly payment for 7-15 years.</KeyTerm>

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "$20K", label: "Typical System Cost" },
  { value: "-$6K", label: "30% Federal Tax Credit" },
  { value: "$14K", label: "Net Cost" },
  { value: "~$160/mo", label: "10-Year Payment" }
]} />

**Payment Comparison by Term:**

| Term | Monthly Payment |
|------|----------------|
| 7 years @ 7% | ~$283/mo |
| 10 years @ 7% | ~$235/mo |
| 15 years @ 7% | ~$199/mo |

**Word Track:**

<PullQuote>"Your system costs $20,000. The government gives you a 30% tax credit — that's $6,000 back. A 10-year loan on $14,000 is about $160/month. You're paying $10 more per month to own a system that produces $150+ in power. Year 1 break-even, then 15+ years of free power."</PullQuote>

**Best For:**

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Good Credit (700+)">Strong history, best rates.</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Long-Term Homeowners">Planning to stay 7+ years.</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Tax Benefit Seekers">Want the 30% federal credit.</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Ownership-Focused">Want to own the asset.</Step>
</Steps>

**Handling Loan Objections:**

*"I don't want another monthly payment"*

<PullQuote>"This payment goes toward something you own. Every payment builds equity. Your SDGE bill goes down by the same amount — it's a wash. And after 10 years, the payment disappears and you've got free solar power for 15+ years."</PullQuote>

*"Will this affect my ability to get other loans?"*

<PullQuote>"Lenders see solar loans as very low-risk because the system generates income that covers the payment. In most cases, the solar loan actually helps your credit profile."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Option 3: Cash Purchase">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "54%", label: "Year 1 ROI (with tax credit)" },
  { value: "12-15%", label: "Annual Return After Breakeven" },
  { value: "YR 7-8", label: "Breakeven Point" },
  { value: "$45K+", label: "25-Year Savings" }
]} />

<KeyTerm term="The Math">$20,000 system - $6,000 tax credit = $14,000 net. $1,800/year in SDGE offset. Year 7 payback. Then 18 years of free power. 320% total return — conservative, before rate increases.</KeyTerm>

**Word Track:**

<PullQuote>"Show me another investment that guarantees 12-15% annual returns with zero risk. Your system costs $20,000. You pay that in cash. $6,000 tax credit. Year 7, your investment is fully paid back. Then for the next 18 years, you're getting $1,800/year in free power. Over 25 years, you're saving $45,000+."</PullQuote>

**Best For:**

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Significant Liquid Savings">Can pay $15-25K without impacting emergency fund.</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Long-Term Homeowners">Planning to stay 7+ years, ideally 10+.</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Debt-Averse">Don't like monthly payments.</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Wealth-Builders">Looking for smart investment returns.</Step>
</Steps>

**Handling Cash Objections:**

*"That's a lot of money tied up"*

<PullQuote>"Instead of that money sitting in a bank earning 0.5% interest, it's in solar earning 12-15% annually. It's a fixed investment that generates returns guaranteed by your electricity savings."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Option 4: Lease">

<KeyTerm term="What Is a Lease">Similar to PPA but with a fixed monthly payment (no escalator). Lessor owns the system. Maintenance included. Often shorter commitment with buy-out option.</KeyTerm>

<PullQuote>"Option 4 is a lease. Very similar to the PPA, but with a fixed monthly payment that never increases. You pay a flat $85/month, we maintain the system, and you get clean energy. Lease is great for the absolute simplest option. The downside is you don't get the tax credit and you don't build ownership equity."</PullQuote>

**Best For:** Simplicity seekers, budget-limited, short-term homeowners, hands-off customers.

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="How to Guide Customers to Their Best Option">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="How Long Are You Staying?">
Less than 5 years → Lease/PPA. 5-10 → PPA or Loan. 10+ → Loan or Cash.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Do You Have Cash Available?">
No → PPA or Loan. Yes ($20K+) → Cash if committed long-term.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Lowest Payment or Lowest Total Cost?">
Lowest payment → PPA or Lease. Lowest total cost → Loan or Cash.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="How's Your Credit?">
Below 650 → PPA only. 650-700 → PPA or Loan. 700+ → Any option.
</Step>
<Step number={5} title="Do You Want to Own the System?">
No → PPA or Lease. Yes → Loan or Cash.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="insight" title="RECOMMENDATION ORDER">
**First:** Loan (best balance of ownership and affordability). **Second:** Cash (maximum wealth building). **Third:** PPA (no money down, simple). **Fourth:** Lease (simplest, least long-term value).
</Callout>

</Accordion>
