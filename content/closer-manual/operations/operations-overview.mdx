# Operations Overview

<Lead>Understanding the end-to-end operational workflow helps you set expectations, build confidence, and own the customer relationship from signature to turn-on and beyond.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Complete Solar Project Timeline">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Week 1-2: Design Phase">
Customer signs → design team receives contract → engineer reviews roof, electrical panel, SDGE account → 3D panel placement modeling → customer receives design plan.

<PullQuote>"After you sign today, our design team takes over. Within a week, they'll have created a detailed design showing exactly where your panels will go. You'll get a copy so you know exactly what we're installing."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Week 3: Permit Submission">
Complete design finalized → KIN submits permit package to the city → city begins review (typically 4-6 weeks).

<PullQuote>"This is totally out of your hands — we handle all the city paperwork and communication. You just watch the calendar."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Week 7-10: Installation">
City approves permit → KIN schedules installation → crew arrives with all equipment → panels mounted, wiring completed, inverter installed → system tested → monitoring app activated.

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "1-2 DAYS", label: "Typical installation" },
  { value: "2-3 DAYS", label: "Complex installations" },
  { value: "SAME DAY", label: "System operational" }
]} />
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Post-Install: City Inspection (1-2 weeks)">
Electrical inspector visits → verifies code compliance → provides approval.

<PullQuote>"The city sends an electrical inspector. They're just making sure everything is up to code. If there are any issues, we handle them immediately. But this rarely happens because our installation is meticulous."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={5} title="Post-Install: SDGE Approval (2-6 weeks)">
KIN submits interconnection request → SDGE reviews → smart meter installed → system goes live.

<PullQuote>"Your system is already producing power during this wait. You can see it on the monitoring app. Once SDGE approves, that power gets credited to your bill."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={6} title="System Turn-On">
SDGE sends final approval → system fully interconnected → savings appear on next bill.

<PullQuote>"That moment when you get your first SDGE bill with massive savings? That's the payoff. That's when it all becomes real."</PullQuote>
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Handling Delays">

### City Permit Delays

<PullQuote>"The city is reviewing your permit. This is normal — they're just being thorough. We're following up with them twice a week to keep things moving."</PullQuote>

### SDGE Approval Delays

<PullQuote>"SDGE has a backlog on interconnections right now. Don't worry — your system is already installed and producing. You can see it on the app. Once they approve, that power gets credited to your bill."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Never blame. Never say "It's the city's fault." The customer doesn't care **whose** fault it is — they care that you're handling it.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Post-Turn-On: Your Role Continues">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="30 Days">
Check in on experience. Answer monitoring app questions. Celebrate their savings.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="90 Days">
First bill analysis — actual vs. projected savings. Gather testimonial or referral.
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Year 1 Anniversary">
Share production and savings numbers. Thank them. Ask for referrals.
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Ongoing">
Quarterly check-ins. Rate change updates. Referral relationship building.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="insight">
When you can walk a customer through this entire process with confidence, they feel safe signing with you. They know what to expect, they understand they're not alone, and they trust KIN to handle everything. **That's the power of operational knowledge.**
</Callout>

</Accordion>
