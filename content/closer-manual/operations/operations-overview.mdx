# Operations Overview: From Signed Deal to System Turn-On

## Why Operations Matters to the Closer

Understanding the end-to-end operational workflow serves multiple purposes:

1. **Set Expectations:** You know what happens next and can tell the customer exactly what to expect
2. **Build Confidence:** Customers are more comfortable signing when they understand the journey
3. **Manage Concerns:** When you can explain the full process, concerns disappear
4. **Own the Sale:** From signature to turn-on, the customer knows you've got their back
5. **Reduce Re-Work:** Clearer expectations = fewer misunderstandings = smoother projects

## The Complete Solar Project Timeline

A typical solar installation at KIN follows this timeline from contract signing to system turn-on:

### Week 1-2: Design Phase

**What Happens:**
- Customer signs paperwork
- Design team receives signed contract
- Design engineer reviews roof, electrical panel, SDGE account
- 3D modeling of exact panel placement and electrical layout
- Customer receives design plan and final specifications

**Your Role as Closer:**
> "After you sign today, our design team takes over. They'll review your roof, your electrical setup, and your SDGE account. Within a week, they'll have created a detailed design showing exactly where your panels will go and how everything will be wired.
>
> You'll get a copy of the design so you know exactly what we're installing."

**Customer Communication:**
- Design team reaches out within 24-48 hours
- Customer may be asked clarifying questions (roof access, electrical panel location, etc.)
- No action required from customer unless they have questions

### Week 3: Permit Submission

**What Happens:**
- Complete design and engineering drawings are finalized
- KIN submits permit package to the city
- City begins review process

**Timeline Notes:**
- San Diego permits typically take 4-6 weeks
- Some fast-track programs available
- KIN handles all city communication

**Your Role as Closer:**
> "Once the design is finalized, we submit everything to the city for permits. The city reviews our engineering, makes sure everything is up to code, and then approves the project.
>
> This is totally out of your hands - we handle all the city paperwork and communication. You'll just watch the calendar."

**Potential Delays:**
- City might request additional information (rare, but happens)
- Rush availability in some cities
- We always push for fastest approval

### Week 7-10: Permit Approval & Installation Scheduling

**What Happens:**
- City approves the permit
- KIN receives approval documentation
- Installation team schedules the installation date
- Customer receives confirmation of installation date and details

**Your Role as Closer:**
> "Once the city approves your permit, we schedule the installation. Our crews are booked out 2-4 weeks, so we'll coordinate a date that works for you.
>
> You'll get a call from our installation coordinator with the exact date and time. We'll show up in KIN trucks with all the equipment and materials."

**Customer Confirmation:**
- Installation date confirmed
- Time window provided (usually morning appointment with completion by end of day)
- Customer provided with pre-installation checklist (access to roof, parking for trucks, etc.)

### Installation Day

**What Happens:**
- KIN installation crew arrives with all equipment and materials
- Electrical permits and inspections are pulled
- Solar panels are mounted to roof
- Electrical wiring and connection completed
- Inverter and monitoring equipment installed
- System powered on and tested
- Customer shown monitoring app

**Timeline:**
- Typical installation: 1-2 days
- Complex installations: 2-3 days
- System fully operational by end of installation day

**Your Role as Closer:**
> "Installation day, our crew shows up early morning with everything they need. By the end of the day, you'll have a complete solar system producing power.
>
> The crew will be professional, respectful, and safe. They'll protect your property, clean up after themselves, and leave you with a system that's ready to go."

**What the Customer Experiences:**
- Crew arrives in branded KIN trucks
- Roof work is noisy but only lasts a few hours
- Electrical work happens after panel mounting
- Customer can see system being tested
- Real-time monitoring app is activated
- Customer sees production numbers on their phone same day

**Pro Tip:** Call the customer the night before to confirm they're still available and excited.

### Post-Installation: City Inspection (1-2 weeks)

**What Happens:**
- City electrical inspector visits property
- Inspection typically takes 30-60 minutes
- Inspector verifies everything is code-compliant
- City provides approval paperwork if inspection passes
- (Rare) Inspection might flag minor issues requiring tweaks

**Your Role as Closer:**
> "A few days after installation, the city sends an electrical inspector. They're just making sure everything we installed is up to code. It's not a big deal - they usually just take a look and approve it.
>
> If there are any issues, we handle them immediately. But this rarely happens because our installation is meticulous."

**Customer Responsibility:**
- Provide access to inspector (usually just open the gate/door)
- No other action required

### Post-Installation: SDGE Approval (2-6 weeks)

**What Happens:**
- KIN submits interconnection request to SDGE
- SDGE reviews the system and your account
- SDGE installs a new smart meter (if needed)
- SDGE approves the system and sends final permission
- System becomes "live" and begins offsetting your bill

**Your Role as Closer:**
> "After the city inspection, we submit everything to SDGE for their approval. They review the system, verify it meets their standards, and then they activate your account.
>
> SDGE might need to install a new meter that tracks both your consumption and your solar production. Once they do that and approve everything, you're officially live. Your bill starts reducing immediately."

**Timeline:**
- SDGE approval: 2-6 weeks typically
- Smart meter installation: Usually same day as SDGE approval
- During waiting period: System is producing power but not yet interconnected (we'll discuss next steps)

**What You Tell Customers:**
> "You'll be producing solar power before SDGE approval. Once they approve, that power gets credited to your account and you see savings on your bill. Until then, you can see the production on your monitoring app, but the bill impact won't show until SDGE approves."

### System Turn-On: The Moment It All Begins

**What Happens:**
- SDGE sends final approval
- System is fully activated and interconnected
- Customer's account begins crediting solar production
- Monthly monitoring shows production and savings
- Savings appear on next SDGE bill

**Your Role as Closer:**
> "From today until system turn-on, you're going to be excited. You're going to watch your system on the monitoring app. You're going to see the production numbers. And then one day, SDGE approves it, and you're officially producing clean energy and saving money.
>
> That moment when you get your first SDGE bill with massive savings? That's the payoff. That's when it all becomes real."

## Potential Delays & How You Communicate Them

Every project has the potential for delays. Here's how to manage expectations:

### Delay Type 1: City Permit Delays

**Cause:**
- City slow reviewing
- Requests for additional information
- Staff shortage at city

**Timeline Impact:**
- Can add 2-4 weeks to total project
- Rarely blocks the entire project

**How You Communicate (At Signing):**
> "The city typically takes 4-6 weeks on permits. Sometimes it's faster, sometimes it's a little slower. We'll push for the fastest approval possible, and we'll keep you updated along the way."

**During Delay:**
> "The city is reviewing your permit. This is normal - they're just being thorough. We're following up with them twice a week to keep things moving. It'll be approved soon."

### Delay Type 2: Permit Revisions

**Cause:**
- City wants changes to design
- City wants clarification
- Code compliance questions

**Timeline Impact:**
- Can add 1-3 weeks
- Usually resolved quickly

**How You Communicate:**
> "The city asked for a minor clarification on our design. Our engineer is submitting the revision right now. This happens maybe 10% of the time and we usually resolve it in a week."

### Delay Type 3: Inspection Delays

**Cause:**
- City inspector schedule is full
- Weather prevents rescheduling
- Inspector wants adjustments

**Timeline Impact:**
- Usually adds only a few days
- Adjustments are rare

**How You Communicate:**
> "We're waiting for the city inspection. Their schedule is a little full right now. We're scheduled for [date]. Once they approve it, we move to the SDGE phase."

### Delay Type 4: SDGE Approval Delays

**Cause:**
- SDGE backlog
- Smart meter shortage
- System complexity
- Account review questions

**Timeline Impact:**
- Can add 2-6 weeks to interconnection
- But system is already installed and producing

**How You Communicate:**
> "SDGE has a backlog right now on interconnections. Don't worry - your system is already installed and producing power. You can see it on the monitoring app. Once SDGE approves it, that power gets credited to your bill. It's coming soon."

**During Long SDGE Wait:**
> "This is actually pretty common in California right now. The state is getting so much solar that SDGE has to review everything carefully. Your system is great - they're just thorough. Once they approve, you're golden."

## Operational Best Practices

### What Makes Projects Run Smoothly:
1. **Clear expectations** - Customer knows the timeline and process
2. **Regular communication** - Customer hears from KIN at key milestones
3. **Proactive problem-solving** - Issues are addressed before customer notices
4. **Professional coordination** - Every team follows processes
5. **Customer accessibility** - Customers can reach KIN when they need to

### What Causes Customer Frustration:
1. **Unclear timelines** - "It'll be done soon" is too vague
2. **Silent periods** - Customer hasn't heard from KIN in weeks
3. **Reactive problem-solving** - Customer has to call to find out there's a delay
4. **Finger-pointing** - "It's the city's fault" doesn't help the customer
5. **Inaccessibility** - Customer can't reach KIN when they have questions

## Key Takeaways

1. **Total timeline:** Signing to turn-on is typically 8-12 weeks
2. **Design phase:** 1-2 weeks (KIN handles)
3. **City permits:** 4-6 weeks (KIN handles, customer just waits)
4. **Installation:** 1-2 days (KIN handles, customer provides access)
5. **SDGE approval:** 2-6 weeks (KIN handles, customer just waits)
6. **Communication is key** - Set expectations and meet them
7. **You own the relationship** - From signature to turn-on and beyond

When you can walk a customer through this entire process with confidence, they feel safe signing with you. They know what to expect, they understand they're not alone, and they trust KIN to handle everything.

That's the power of operational knowledge: It closes deals and creates raving fans.
