# Roofing Assessment for Solar Installation

<Lead>Solar panels live on your roof for 25+ years. Before installing a system, we need to ensure the roof can support that weight and won't need major work during the system's lifespan.</Lead>

<Callout type="important" title="THE REALITY">
Most roofs that need replacing before solar will need replacing **during** the solar system's lifespan. A smart closer addresses this proactively and positions it as part of the value package — not a problem.
</Callout>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Four Key Assessment Areas">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Roof Age">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "0-5yr", label: "Excellent — outlasts the system" },
  { value: "5-10yr", label: "Good — ages together with solar" },
  { value: "10-15yr", label: "Acceptable — 10+ years remaining" },
  { value: "15-20yr", label: "Concerning — may need replacement" },
  { value: "20yr+", label: "Problematic — replacement likely soon" }
]} />

<PullQuote>"How old is your roof? With most asphalt roofs, you're looking at about 20-year lifespan. If your roof was getting toward the end of its life, we'd recommend addressing that before solar. But you're in good shape here."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Roof Condition">
**Green Flags:** Uniform color, no curling/cracking, no missing shingles, intact flashing, no sagging.

**Yellow Flags:** Some discoloration, minor cracking, few missing shingles (1-3), moss/algae (cosmetic).

**Red Flags:** Multiple missing/damaged shingles, significant curling, visible holes, sagging sections, visible rot.
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Roof Material">

| Material | Lifespan | Solar Compatibility |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Asphalt Shingles** | 15-25yr | Standard — most common |
| **Metal** | 40-70yr | Excellent — special fastening required |
| **Wood Shakes** | 20-40yr | Possible — assess carefully |
| **Spanish/Clay Tiles** | 50-100yr | Challenging — needs special framing |
| **Flat (Tar & Gravel)** | 15-20yr | Possible — engineer review for weight |
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Structural Assessment">

<Callout type="tip">
You're not a structural engineer. Do a visual walk-around, note concerns, and trust your engineer for the detailed assessment. An 8kW system (24 panels) weighs **1,000+ pounds**.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Our engineers will do a complete structural assessment to ensure your roof can safely support the solar system. They'll look at the rafters, the decking, and the overall integrity. It's one of the reasons we do an on-site survey."</PullQuote>
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Re-Roofing Conversation">

### Scenario 1: Roof Too Old

<PullQuote>"Jimmy, I want to be straight with you. Your roof is 22 years old, which puts it near the end of its lifespan. We could install solar on it today, but you'd face a roof replacement in the next 5-7 years. That means pulling off panels, replacing the roof, and reinstalling. That's expensive and a hassle."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
Position it as a package: New roof (25-year warranty) + new solar (25-year warranty) = **everything protected for 25 years**. They're replacing the roof anyway — doing it with solar planned in saves money.
</Callout>

### Scenario 2: Localized Damage

<PullQuote>"I noticed a few damaged shingles and the flashing around this vent needs attention. Those are small fixes — nothing major. I can have our roofing partner come out. Repairs take a day, then we're ready to install."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Red Flags That Require Engineer Review">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Obvious Structural Damage">
Sagging sections, visible rot.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Active Water Damage">
Stains in attic or interior.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Problematic Geometry">
Multiple levels, dormers, steep angles.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Flat Roof with Poor Drainage">
Pooling water or past water damage.
</Step>
</Steps>

<PullQuote>"I want our engineer to take a closer look before we commit. I'm flagging this for our team, and they'll do a full assessment. Either way, we'll make sure it's safe and sound before we install anything."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>
