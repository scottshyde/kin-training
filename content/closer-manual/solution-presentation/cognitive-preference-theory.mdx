# Cognitive Preference Theory in Solar Sales

<Lead>Not all customers think the same way. Understanding Cognitive Preference Theory allows you to identify your customer's dominant decision-making style and adapt your presentation to match how they naturally think.</Lead>

<Callout type="important" title="THE EDGE">
This is the difference between a generic presentation and a **personalized persuasion strategy** tailored to your specific customer's brain.
</Callout>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Four Cognitive Preferences">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "ANALYTICAL", label: "Logic & data — left brain" },
  { value: "RELATIONAL", label: "Connection & emotion — right brain" },
  { value: "DRIVER", label: "Results & speed — action-oriented" },
  { value: "CAUTIOUS", label: "Process & safety — systematic" }
]} />

<Callout type="insight">
Most people have a **dominant preference**, though everyone uses all four to some degree. Your job is to identify the dominant one within the first 5 minutes.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="1. The Analytical Customer">

<KeyTerm term="How They Think">Facts and figures matter most. They want detailed analysis, ask "Why?" and "How does it work?" repeatedly, and are skeptical of emotion.</KeyTerm>

**What They Value:** Technical specs, comparative data, long-term ROI, warranty details, performance guarantees, third-party certifications.

### How to Adapt

<Callout type="warning" title="INSTEAD OF">
"Most of our customers love their savings"
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Our customers see average annual savings of $2,847 in Year 1, increasing to $4,200 by Year 5."</PullQuote>

**Full Word Track:**

<PullQuote>"I can see you like to understand the details. Let me walk through exactly how we calculated your system. Based on your roof orientation at 210 degrees and an average daily irradiance of 5.2 kWh/m²/day, our 8.2kW system will produce approximately 13,650 kWh annually. That's based on actual production data from similar systems in your zip code over the past 5 years. The numbers don't lie."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
**Red flags you're losing them:** They keep asking "prove it," comparing you to competitors, mentioning blogs they've read, wanting to take materials home.

**Win them back:** More documentation, a technical deep-dive call with your engineer, independent testing data.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="2. The Relational Customer">

<KeyTerm term="How They Think">Relationships and trust drive their decisions. They buy from people they like. Emotional connection matters more than raw data.</KeyTerm>

**What They Value:** Customer stories, your personal background, company culture, testimonial videos, long-term relationship, values alignment.

### How to Adapt

<Callout type="warning" title="INSTEAD OF">
"Our equipment has 25-year warranties"
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"The Johnsons next door went solar with us three years ago. They told me last month they can't imagine having done it with anyone else. They love seeing their contribution to clean energy, and their kids are proud of them for it."</PullQuote>

**Full Word Track:**

<PullQuote>"Jimmy, I love your family's values here. You mentioned how important it is to teach your kids about environmental responsibility. That's something that really resonates with me too. That's actually why I got into solar — I want to look my kids in the eye and know I'm part of the solution, not the problem."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
**Red flags:** Looking at their phone, haven't asked about your background, seem disconnected.

**Win them back:** Be more personal and vulnerable. Share stories instead of data. Frame in terms of family values and legacy.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="3. The Driver Customer">

<KeyTerm term="How They Think">They want results and they want them fast. Efficiency matters most. They make decisions quickly and respect strength and confidence.</KeyTerm>

**What They Value:** Speed of installation, clear timeline, bottom-line ROI, competitive advantage, minimal hassle, decisiveness.

### How to Adapt

<Callout type="warning" title="INSTEAD OF">
A 45-minute detailed walkthrough
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Jimmy, I know you're busy. Here's the bottom line: Your investment is $X, your annual savings is $Y, and you're looking at full ROI in 7 years. After year 7, you're producing free power for the next 18 years. While other people pay SDGE's increases, you're done. Installation takes one day. We handle everything else. Should we get started?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
**Red flags:** Checking their watch, interrupting with "What's the bottom line?", seem bored.

**Win them back:** Cut the presentation in half. Focus exclusively on results and timeline. Be decisive and confident.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="4. The Cautious Customer">

<KeyTerm term="How They Think">They want everything done correctly and systematically. Concerned about risks. They value process, steps, structure, and guarantees.</KeyTerm>

**What They Value:** Clear processes, risk mitigation, references, understanding what could go wrong, comprehensive protection, detailed timelines.

### How to Adapt

<PullQuote>"I appreciate that you want to make sure we cover all the bases. That's the right approach. Let me walk you through our process step by step."</PullQuote>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Week 1-2: Design Phase">
Our engineer reviews your roof, electrical panel, and SDGE account.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Week 3: Permits">
We submit all permits. We have relationships with the city — typically 4-6 weeks.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Week 7-10: Installation">
Full crew, permits in hand, covered by $2M liability insurance.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="After Installation">
SDGE verification. 25-year warranty covers everything.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
**Red flags:** "What if" questions, mention of lawyers/accountants, anxious rather than interested.

**Win them back:** More documentation and guarantees. Offer references. Slow down. Don't pressure — they need time to build confidence.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Identifying Your Customer's Preference">

<KeyTerm term="Listen for Language Clues">The words they use tell you how they think.</KeyTerm>

- **Analytical:** *"How does this work?" "What's the ROI?" "Show me the data"*
- **Relational:** *"What did other customers think?" "How did you get into solar?"*
- **Driver:** *"What's the bottom line?" "How fast can you install?" "Let's move forward"*
- **Cautious:** *"What could go wrong?" "Can you guarantee this?" "Walk me through each step"*
