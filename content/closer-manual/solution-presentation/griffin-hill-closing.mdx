# Griffin Hill Closing System

<Lead>As a closer, you own routines 3 through 6 of the Griffin Hill system: Solution Presentation, Adapted Solution, Closing Interactions, and Fulfillment & Follow Up. This is where deals are won or lost.</Lead>

<Callout type="warning" title="PREREQUISITE">
Every play in this section assumes you have already completed a **thorough Needs Audit**. If you skipped it or rushed it, go back and finish before presenting.
</Callout>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Solution Presentation — Propose, Persuade, Convince">

<Callout type="important" title="THIS IS NOT A PRODUCT DEMO">
The SP is a **personalized recommendation** built entirely on what the homeowner told you during the Needs Audit. If your SP does not reference their words, their numbers, and their pain — it is a generic pitch and it will feel like one.
</Callout>

### Play 1: Reframe (CO + NA)

Before you present anything, summarize everything you learned. This proves you listened, confirms you understand, and creates the bridge to your proposal.

<PullQuote>"So let me make sure I've got this right. You've been in the home for about 8 years. Your summer bills are running around $350, and you've seen them go up every year. You mentioned you're frustrated because you feel like there's nothing you can do about it — SDG&E just keeps raising rates. And you said if you could cut that bill in half, you'd put that money toward your kids' college fund. Did I get that right?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
Wait for them to confirm. If they correct you, update your understanding. This is not a formality — it is the **foundation** of your entire presentation.
</Callout>

### Play 2: Transition via Benefit

Bridge from their situation to your solution using the benefit that matters most to **them**.

<PullQuote>"Well, here's the good news — based on everything you just told me, this is exactly the kind of situation where we can make a big difference. Let me show you what this looks like for your specific home."</PullQuote>

### Play 3: Proposal — The FABP Method

<KeyTerm term="FABP">Feature → Advantage → Benefit → Proof. Connect every feature to something they said they care about.</KeyTerm>

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "FEATURE", label: "What the product includes" },
  { value: "ADVANTAGE", label: "Why that feature matters generally" },
  { value: "BENEFIT", label: "Why it matters to THEM specifically" },
  { value: "PROOF", label: "Evidence it works" }
]} />

<PullQuote>"Your system is a 9.6 kW array — 24 panels on your south-facing roof. It's sized to offset 100% of your annual usage. That means that $350 summer bill drops to about $15 — just the grid connection fee. And this isn't hypothetical — here's the Rodriguez family on Elm Street. Same size home, similar usage. Here's their bill from last July: $12.47."</PullQuote>

### Play 4: Service Levels & Pricing

Present pricing with confidence. **Do not apologize for the cost** — tie it back to the savings they already agreed they want.

<PullQuote>"So there are two ways to go. Option one is a PPA at your rate per kWh — that's about 20-30% less than what SDG&E is charging you today, and your rate locks in. No upfront cost. Option two is a purchase, which gives you full ownership benefits and the tax credit. Based on what you told me, most homeowners in your situation go with the option that maximizes day-one savings."</PullQuote>

### Play 5: Call to Action Close

<PullQuote>"Based on everything we've gone over, I think this is a no-brainer for your family. Let's get you locked in tonight so we can start the permitting process and get you saving as soon as possible. I just need your signature right here to get the ball rolling."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Insurance Play">

<KeyTerm term="When to Use">The homeowner says "I just want to make sure," "This is a big commitment," or "What if something goes wrong?"</KeyTerm>

<PullQuote>"I totally get it — this is a big decision and you want to make sure you're protected. Here's what I want you to know. First, there's a 3-day right of rescission — if you sign tonight and wake up with second thoughts, you can cancel with zero consequences. Second, your system comes with a 25-year warranty. Third, KIN Home has been doing this for years and we're not going anywhere. You're not jumping off a cliff here — you've got a parachute, a backup parachute, and a safety net at the bottom."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
You are not overcoming an objection — you are **validating their concern** and showing them they are already protected.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Validation Play">

<KeyTerm term="When to Use">The homeowner raises a legitimate concern — roof condition, rate changes, contract terms. Things that deserve a real answer.</KeyTerm>

<PullQuote>"You know what? That's a really smart question, and honestly, I'd be asking the same thing if I were in your shoes. The fact that you're thinking this through tells me you're the kind of person who makes good decisions. And here's what I've found — the homeowners who ask the toughest questions up front are the happiest customers six months later."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="important" title="CRITICAL">
Validation without substance is empty. Validate, **then inform**.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Ignore Play">

Some objections are not real. They are reflexive responses, stall tactics, or filler.

**Examples of ignorable statements:**
- "We need to think about it" *(said immediately, before processing)*
- "We never make decisions the same day" *(often untrue)*
- "Let me do some research" *(usually means they want you to leave)*

<PullQuote>"I totally respect that. Let me ask you this — setting aside the timing for a second, does the savings itself make sense to you? Is this something you'd want to do if the timing was right?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
If they say yes, the objection was not about the product — it was about **comfort**. Continue building comfort. If they say no, you have a different problem to diagnose.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Overcoming Objections — Identify, Isolate, Evaluate">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Identify">
Find out what the objection **actually** is. Most homeowners give you the surface version, not the real version.

<PullQuote>"Absolutely, I want you to feel 100% confident. Help me understand — when you say you need to think about it, what specifically are you thinking through? Is it the savings, the timing, or something else?"</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Isolate">
Make sure this is the **only** concern. If you solve one and another pops up, you're chasing a moving target.

<PullQuote>"Okay, so the main concern is whether your roof will need to be replaced in the next few years. If we could address that — if you knew the roof was good — would you be ready to move forward tonight?"</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Evaluate — Simple vs. Complex">
**Simple objections** are based on misunderstanding. Handle them with **Feel/Felt/Found**:

<PullQuote>"I totally understand how you feel. A lot of homeowners I've worked with felt the same way. What they found was that we do a full roof assessment before we install, and if there's any issue, we address it as part of the project."</PullQuote>

**Complex objections** are based on real constraints — financial hardship, genuine roof issues, pending home sale. These require deeper discovery, not a word track. Sometimes the answer is "this isn't the right time" — saying that with integrity earns you a referral later.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Adapted Solution — When the First Proposal Doesn't Close">

<Callout type="important" title="DON'T GIVE UP. DON'T REPEAT YOURSELF.">
The Adapted Solution is a strategic reset. Their "no" gave you new information. Use it.
</Callout>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Reframe CO + NA + SP">
Summarize everything again, incorporating what you learned from their objection.

<PullQuote>"Okay, so let me step back. You told me your bill is $350, you want to save money for your kids' college fund, and the savings make sense. The concern is that you're not comfortable with a 25-year agreement because you might move in 5-7 years. Do I have that right?"</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Adapt the Proposal">
Change the solution to address their specific concern. Different financing, different size, different framing.

<PullQuote>"Let's look at the PPA option — no upfront cost and if you sell, the agreement transfers to the next buyer. Homes with solar sell faster and for about 4% more. Instead of a liability, this becomes a selling point."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Call to Action Close (Again)">

<PullQuote>"Does this version make more sense for your situation? Great — let's get this locked in so you start saving right away."</PullQuote>
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Closing Interactions — The Dangerous Delay Period">

<Callout type="warning" title="THE DANGER ZONE">
Every day that passes after a no-close, the probability of closing **drops**. Closing Interactions keep the deal alive.
</Callout>

### The Give/Get Framework

<KeyTerm term="Every CI Touchpoint">Must include both: Give something new (value) and Get a mini-commitment (action).</KeyTerm>

**Give examples:**
- Rate increase announcement
- Neighbor update: "The Johnsons just got their system turned on"
- Deadline: "The current incentive rate expires at the end of the month"
- An answer to something they asked

**Get examples:**
- "Can you pull up your SDGE bill and send me a photo?"
- "Have you had a chance to talk to your wife?"
- "I'll be in the neighborhood Thursday — can I stop by for 10 minutes?"

### CI Cadence

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "Day 1", label: "Text: Great meeting you" },
  { value: "Day 3", label: "Call: Give value + get commitment" },
  { value: "Day 5", label: "Text/drop-by: Share proof" },
  { value: "Day 7", label: "Call: Direct ask + new CTA" },
  { value: "Day 10+", label: "Monthly check-in — don't burn the bridge" }

<Callout type="insight">
The difference between following up and being annoying is **value**. If every touchpoint gives them something useful, you're being helpful. If every touchpoint is "just checking in," you're being a pest.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Fulfillment & Follow Up — Close the Loop">

The sale is not over when they sign. FF is where you build your reputation, your referral pipeline, and your proof library.

### Fulfill Promises

Everything you said during the sale **must happen**. A fulfilled promise turns a customer into an advocate. A broken promise turns them into a cancellation.

### Collect Proofs

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Google Review">
Send them the direct link — make it easy.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Before/After Bill Screenshot">
Visual proof for your next presentation.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Video Testimonial">
Even 30 seconds on their porch is gold.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Install Photo">
Their roof, their system, their pride.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
**When to ask:** After the system is activated and they've seen their first low bill. That is the peak of excitement.
</Callout>

### Harvest Referrals

<PullQuote>"By the way, who else do you know in the neighborhood who's been complaining about their SDG&E bill? I'd love to help them out the same way."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Referral Timing">Ask after fulfillment, not before. They need to experience the result before they'll stake their reputation on recommending you.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The CTA Close Pattern">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Summarize the Benefit">
What they get — in their words.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Create Urgency">
Why now — rate locks, install calendar, incentive deadlines.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Direct Ask">
What to do next — clear, simple, confident.
</Step>
</Steps>

<PullQuote>"So you're looking at saving $150 a month — that's $1,800 a year back in your pocket. Right now, you're locked in at today's rate before the next SDG&E increase hits. Let's get the paperwork started so we can get you on the install calendar."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="important" title="THE TEST">
Every CTA Close ties back to **their words** and **their pain**. If you find yourself making a generic pitch, you didn't do a good enough Needs Audit.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Closer Mindset: Integrity in the Close">

<PullQuote>The best closers do not need tricks. They need a thorough Needs Audit, a personalized proposal, and the confidence to ask for the business. The system gives you all three — execute it.</PullQuote>

- **Never pressure** someone into a deal that is wrong for them
- **Always tell the truth** about savings projections
- **Follow through** on every promise
- **Treat every homeowner** like your neighbor
- **Close with confidence**, not desperation

</Accordion>
