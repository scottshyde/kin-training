# Overcoming Objections in Close

<Lead>The best overcoming objection play-set is an exceptional needs audit. If you nail the needs audit, you may never even have to overcome an objection. The objection won't surface in the first place.</Lead>

<Callout type="important" title="THE CLOSER'S EDGE">
The difference between a good closer and an exceptional one is whether they get the business the **first time around** or have to come back multiple times. A quality needs audit is your secret weapon.
</Callout>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Three Types of Objections">

<KeyTerm term="Every objection falls into one of three categories">Identify the category first, then select the right play-set.</KeyTerm>

Understanding which category the objection falls into helps you respond with the right play-set.

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Play-Set 1: The Identification Process">

<Callout type="insight">
Use this play-set to fully understand and clarify the objection **before** jumping to solutions.
</Callout>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Identify">
- **Existence**: Confirm the objection exists — *"So cost is a concern for you?"*
- **Nature**: Understand what specifically they're concerned about — *"Tell me more about that"*
- **Restate**: Confirm your understanding — *"So you're concerned that the monthly payment might be higher than you expected?"*
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Isolate">
- **Other Concerns**: *"Are there any other concerns besides that?"* (Keep asking until they say no)
- **Biggest Barrier**: *"Of all the concerns you've mentioned, which one would be the biggest barrier to moving forward?"*
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Evaluate">
- **Simple or Complex**: Determine if this is straightforward to overcome or will require deep work
- **Qualification Check**: Does this objection relate back to something in your needs audit? Did you miss something?
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Play-Set 2: The Solution Process">

<Callout type="important" title="THE SIX-STEP SEQUENCE">
Once you've identified the objection, use this systematic approach to overcome it.
</Callout>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Empathize — Feel, Felt, Found">

<KeyTerm term="Feel, Felt, Found">The three-word framework that creates instant rapport while transitioning to the solve.</KeyTerm>

<PullQuote>"I know exactly how you feel. In fact, the Daniels next door felt the same way. What we've found at KIN is..."</PullQuote>

</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Solve">
- **Minimize the barrier** — Show how the concern is smaller than they think
- **Provide context** — Use comparison, proof, or logic to reframe the issue
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Benefit">
Connect back to their stated needs from the needs audit. Show how overcoming this objection **unlocks the benefits they said they wanted**.
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Proof">
Use a proof play — neighbor success story, data, or similar situation.

<PullQuote>"Jimmy is on his way to save $60,000 over the next 25 years. I know I can do the same for you."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={5} title="Relate">
Connect the overcoming objection back to their **original concerns**. Show how this solution addresses what matters to them.
</Step>

<Step number={6} title="CTAC — Call to Action Close">

<PullQuote>"That being said, let's go ahead and get you qualified. Who's name would you prefer to put this in?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
You're not asking permission to move forward — you're confirming the next logical step.
</Callout>
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Master Word Track">

<Callout type="important" title="MEMORIZE THIS FRAMEWORK">
Adapt it to the specific objection, but keep the structure intact.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"It makes perfect sense to me that you want to make the best decision. Based on our conversation, what are you not 100% on today? Can you help me better understand?

I know exactly how you feel. In fact, the Daniels next door felt the same way. What we've found at KIN is when we cut corners we diminish the customer experience. We give the best equipment, the best warranties, at a fair price.

Jimmy is on his way to save $60,000 over the next 25 years. I know I can do the same for you. That being said, let's go ahead and get you qualified. Who's name would you prefer to put this in?"</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Power Drill-Down Questions">

### "I want to think about it"

<PullQuote>"Help me understand what would be your perceived risk with moving forward?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"What boxes need to be checked with us for you to feel comfortable to roll forward?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"What else would you need to know before having this become a reality for you?"</PullQuote>

### "It's too expensive"

<PullQuote>"What would be a comfortable monthly investment for you?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"Is it the total price or the monthly payment that's the concern?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"Walk me through your biggest expense right now — the solar or the SDGE bill?"</PullQuote>

### "I need to talk to my spouse/family"

<PullQuote>"When are they available so we can all talk through this together?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"What do you think would be their main question or concern?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"If your spouse were here right now, what would you want them to understand first?"</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Objection Prevention Mindset">

<KeyTerm term="Education = Confidence">Being more educated on anything will always lead to confident decision-making.</KeyTerm>

When customers feel like they don't know enough, they hesitate. Your job is to **educate them** until they have the confidence to decide.

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "How", label: "solar works" },
  { value: "How", label: "their savings work" },
  { value: "What", label: "the warranty covers" },
  { value: "When", label: "the project timeline" }
]} />

The more informed they are, the more likely they move forward without objections.

<Callout type="insight">
You overcome most objections **before they become objections**. Deep discovery, clear communication, transparency, and social proof — that's the prevention playbook.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Common Objections by Category">

### Time-Based

- "I want to think about it"
- "Let me talk to my spouse/accountant"
- "Can we do this next quarter?"
- "I'm not ready yet"

<Callout type="tip">
Ask drill-down questions to understand if it's truly a timing issue or a hidden concern. Often "I need time" really means **"I don't feel confident enough yet."**
</Callout>

### Money-Based

- "It's too expensive"
- "The payment is higher than I expected"
- "I'm not getting enough savings"
- "What if my roof needs replacing?"

<Callout type="tip">
Go back to their needs audit. What savings number did they want? Reframe the investment against their current SDGE bill. Show the **25-year picture**.
</Callout>

### People-Based

- "My spouse needs to be here"
- "I need to talk to my accountant"
- "This is a big decision for our family"
- "My business partner won't agree to this"

<Callout type="tip">
Respect the decision-making process. Offer to present together. Get buy-in from the primary decision-maker in the room, then **schedule a time** to present to the other stakeholders.
</Callout>

</Accordion>
