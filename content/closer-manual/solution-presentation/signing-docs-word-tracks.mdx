# Signing Docs Word Tracks

The signing phase is where the deal becomes real. Your job is to walk customers through the paperwork with confidence, clarity, and reassurance.

<Accordion title="The Moment of Truth: Walking Through Paperwork">
The signing phase is where the deal becomes real. Your customer has committed verbally, and now they need to sign the Home Improvement Agreement (HIA) and related documents. This is a critical moment where last-minute doubts can surface. Your job is to walk them through the paperwork with confidence, clarity, and reassurance.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Pre-Signing Mindset & Setup">
Before you even pull out the documents, set the right tone:

### Mental Preparation
- You've already earned the business through your needs audit and solution presentation
- The documents are simply formalizing what you've already agreed to
- Your confidence will transfer to the customer
- This is not a sales conversation - this is an administrative process

### Physical Setup
- Have documents organized and in the right order
- Use a pen that writes smoothly (poor pen quality conveys lack of professionalism)
- Sit beside the customer, not across from them (collaborative, not adversarial)
- Have a clean folder or binder ready for their copy of signed documents
- Prepare to walk them through each page systematically
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Opening Word Track">
Start with a reassuring opening that sets expectations and removes anxiety:

> "Jimmy, we're going to take a few minutes and walk through some paperwork. I know paperwork can seem intimidating, but I promise you there are no surprises here. Everything we're signing today is exactly what we've already discussed. These documents just formalize our agreement and protect both you and KIN. By the end of this, you'll have a clear copy of everything we're doing, the timeline, and the warranty coverage. Sound good?"

This opening accomplishes several things:
- Acknowledges potential anxiety
- Sets expectations for what's coming
- Confirms this is expected and normal
- Promises clarity and protection
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Walking Through the HIA (Home Improvement Agreement)">
The HIA is your primary document. Walk through it strategically, hitting the key sections:

### Section 1: Customer Information & System Details

**Word Track:**
> "First, let's make sure all your information is correct. [Point to each field] Your name, address, phone number, email. I'm going to send you updates here throughout the project, so make sure this email is the one you check regularly.
>
> Here's the system we're installing: [number] of panels, [equipment details], and here's your estimated production. This is what our team engineered specifically for your home based on the site survey."

**Key Points:**
- Verify information is accurate
- Highlight email - set expectation for future communication
- Explain the system configuration - show you've customized it for them
- Point out the estimated annual production - this becomes a selling point

### Section 2: System Cost & Financing Option

**Word Track for PPA:**
> "Here's the investment piece. Your total system cost is $[amount]. Now, you selected our PPA option, which is perfect for your situation. This means:
> - You pay zero dollars down today - no out-of-pocket investment
> - We own and maintain the system for 25 years
> - You simply pay a reduced rate for the solar power you use
> - The rate increases 3.5% annually, which is significantly less than SDGE's increases
>
> So let me show you the math: SDGE is projected to increase 18% this year alone. Your solar bill increases just 3.5% per year. That's the difference right there."

**Word Track for Loan/Cash:**
> "Your total investment is $[amount]. With the loan, you'll own the system immediately, which means you qualify for the federal tax credit of 30% - that's $[amount] back in your pocket next tax season. Your monthly payment is $[amount], which is [less/same/slightly more] than your current SDGE bill. And after [loan term], you own it free and clear."

**Key Points:**
- Restate the financing choice they made
- Remind them of the key benefits of their choice
- Show the math on savings
- Mention the tax credit if applicable
- Make the monthly payment relatable to their current bill

### Section 3: Warranty Coverage

**Word Track:**
> "One of the reasons we stand out from other solar companies is our warranty. Let me walk you through what's covered:
>
> **Product Warranty**: 25 years on the panels and inverter. If anything fails, we replace it - no cost to you.
>
> **Workmanship Warranty**: 25 years on the installation. Our team installed this - we stand behind every wire, every roof penetration, everything.
>
> **Performance Guarantee**: Your system will produce at least 80% of the rated capacity for 25 years. If it doesn't, we fix it.
>
> **Battery Backup** [if applicable]: Your battery is covered for 10 years.
>
> This is one of the best warranties in the industry, and it's why customers choose KIN. You're protected."

**Key Points:**
- Emphasize the length and comprehensiveness of warranty
- Highlight that KIN installed it (not subcontractors)
- Connect back to your competitive advantage
- Reassure them about long-term protection

### Section 4: Timeline & Next Steps

**Word Track:**
> "After you sign today, here's what happens.
>
> **Week 1-2**: Our design team finalizes the exact electrical layout and roof design
>
> **Week 3**: We submit to the city for permits. This typically takes 4-6 weeks
>
> **Week 7-10**: Once permits are approved, we schedule your installation. We'll call you to confirm the date
>
> **Installation Day**: Our team arrives at [time], and we have you producing solar energy by [end of day/next day]
>
> **After Installation**: We handle the SDGE interconnection approval - you don't have to do anything. Once they approve it, you're live
>
> The total timeline from today to your system being live is typically 8-12 weeks. You'll get updates from our team throughout - no surprises."

**Key Points:**
- Give them a clear timeline
- Show them they don't have to do anything
- Set realistic expectations
- Remind them you're handling the coordination
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Protecting Your Investment (Monitoring & Maintenance)">
If your company offers monitoring, maintenance plans, or other add-ons:

**Word Track for Monitoring:**
> "We include 25 years of professional monitoring with your system. This means our team watches your system 24/7. If there's ever a performance dip or any issue, we catch it and fix it before you even know there's a problem. You'll also have a mobile app where you can see real-time production and savings. It's like having your own solar engineer watching your system."

**Word Track for Maintenance:**
> "We recommend our maintenance plan, which covers annual inspections and cleaning. Dirt and pollen can reduce your production over time. For just $[amount]/year, we make sure your system is always operating at peak efficiency. It's like getting $[savings] back annually."
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Handling Last-Minute Concerns During Signing">
Even though you've overcome objections, new concerns sometimes surface when customers see the documents. Here's how to handle them:

### "The Price Seems High"

**Word Track:**
> "I hear you. Let me show you the context. The average cost per watt for solar in San Diego right now is $2.50 to $3.00. We're at $[your rate], which is [better/competitive]. And remember, you're getting the best equipment, 25-year warranties, and in-house installation. You're not paying for a middleman - you're paying for quality."

### "I Don't Want the System Touching My Roof"

**Word Track:**
> "I completely understand that concern. Our installation process is engineered to protect your roof. We:
> - Use roof-rated fasteners that are sealed with premium flashing
> - Never drill through the wrong areas
> - Our team has installed [number] systems in the neighborhood with zero roof leaks
>
> In fact, a properly installed solar system can add to your roof's value and longevity by providing shade and weather protection. Your roof is in good hands."

### "What If My SDGE Bill Goes Down?"

**Word Track:**
> "Great question. With a PPA, you only pay for the power you use from solar. If your bill goes down because of conservation or efficiency improvements, your solar bill goes down proportionally. You always get the benefit. You never pay more than what the system produces."

### "When Will I See the Savings?"

**Word Track:**
> "You'll see the savings immediately - from the first bill. Instead of paying SDGE their full rate, you're paying us our reduced rate. By Year 3, once we account for SDGE's rate increases and your system's full optimization, your savings will be dramatic. By Year 10, you'll have saved [amount]. By Year 25, you'll have saved [amount]. This is a long-game investment in your family's financial future."
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Signing Sequence">
Once all sections are discussed and understood, follow this sequence:

### Step 1: The Walkthrough Complete
> "Does everything here make sense? Are there any questions before we finalize?"

[Listen carefully - address any remaining concerns]

### Step 2: The Commitment Confirmation
> "Perfect. You're making a great decision for your family. Let's get this signed."

### Step 3: The Signing Instructions
> "I'm going to need your signature here [point], initial here [point], and here [point]. The date is [date]. And I'll need the same from your spouse [if applicable]."

### Step 4: The Second Witness (if required by your state)
> "I'm going to sign as the representative from KIN, and that completes the agreement."

### Step 5: The Document Review
> "Here's your copy - you'll keep this. I'm keeping one for our records. You'll receive a digital copy by email within 24 hours as well. Keep this somewhere safe - you'll need it when it comes time for your annual inspection."
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Post-Signing: The Critical Next Words">
This is where many closers make a mistake. Don't just say goodbye. Reinforce the decision and set expectations:

**Word Track:**
> "Jimmy, I want you to know - you made the right decision today. You just protected your family from rising energy costs and invested in clean energy. Our team is going to take great care of you from here.
>
> Here's what's going to happen next: [Name] from our design team will reach out to you within 24 hours to schedule your design review. You'll get all the technical details and confirm the timeline. You're going to love what we've designed for you.
>
> If you have any questions in the meantime, reach out to me directly. I'm here for you."

[Shake hands firmly, make eye contact, smile]
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Signature Checklist">
Before your customer leaves, verify:

- [ ] All signature lines are signed (not initialed where full signature required)
- [ ] Both spouses have signed (if applicable)
- [ ] Date is filled in completely
- [ ] No blank sections remain
- [ ] Customer has a clear copy
- [ ] You have contact information correct
- [ ] Email address is accurate (critical for updates)
- [ ] Phone number has area code
- [ ] Customer understood warranty coverage
- [ ] Customer understands timeline
- [ ] Customer knows next steps and who will contact them
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Tone Reminders">
Throughout the signing process:

1. **Stay calm and confident** - Your demeanor should convey that this is routine and expected
2. **Use simple language** - Avoid jargon; explain every term
3. **Move at their pace** - Don't rush. Let them read. Answer every question
4. **Show enthusiasm** - You're not trying to hide anything. You're excited about what you're delivering
5. **Maintain eye contact** - Builds trust and confidence
6. **Smile and be warm** - This is a celebration of their good decision, not an interrogation
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Common Mistakes to Avoid">
- **Rushing through the documents** - Slow down, let them absorb
- **Skipping the warranty section** - This is your differentiator; don't minimize it
- **Being defensive about price** - Address it confidently with context
- **Not setting timeline expectations** - Vagueness creates anxiety
- **Assuming they understand** - Ask, "Does that make sense?" frequently
- **Leaving blank spaces** - Go back and fill everything in before they sign
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Key Takeaway">
The signing phase is not an adversarial negotiation - it's the formalization of a partnership you've already built. Your customer has said yes. Now you're just documenting it. Walk them through with clarity, confidence, and warmth, and you'll set them up for a smooth installation experience and a lifetime of positive feelings about their decision to go solar.
</Accordion>
