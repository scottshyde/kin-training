# Solidifying the Appointment

<Lead>Every interaction with a prospect is an opportunity to get them moving closer to their yes. The bigger the commitment you get before the appointment, the higher your close rate during the appointment.</Lead>

<Callout type="important" title="THE PRINCIPLE">
The person with the most specific, confirmed commitment closes more business than the person who takes a loose "maybe" appointment.
</Callout>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Commitment Hierarchy">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Generic Interest (~40% show rate)">
"Yeah, that sounds interesting." Lowest commitment. They might show, might not.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Time Commitment (~60% show rate)">
"OK, let's do it Thursday at 10." They've committed to a specific time. This is your baseline.
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Logistical Confirmation (~80% show rate)">
"Thursday at 10, and both of you will be there?" Both decision makers confirmed. Shows intentionality.
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Billing Confirmation">
"And your address for the appointment is 123 Main St." All details confirmed and documented. Makes it real in their mind.
</Step>

<Step number={5} title="Behavioral Confirmation (~85% show rate)">
"Pull your SDGE bill so I can review it before we meet?" They take an action. Creates investment.
</Step>

<Step number={6} title="Stakeholder Confirmation">
"Let me know if your wife has any questions between now and Thursday?" Decision makers aligned. Creates positive momentum.
</Step>

<Step number={7} title="Expectation Setting (~95% show rate)">
"I'll have our design engineer call you Wednesday to confirm, sound good?" Clear handoff. They expect follow-up. You control the next touch.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Building Commitment: The Phased Approach">

<Callout type="insight">
Don't ask for a giant commitment all at once. Build it in phases — each small commitment becomes the foundation for the next bigger one.
</Callout>

### Phase 1: The Appointment Time

<PullQuote>"Perfect. So I'd like to get you on the books. Are you available next Thursday, during the day? What works better for you — morning or afternoon?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Ownership Principle">When they choose the time, they have more ownership of the appointment. Let them choose — don't suggest.</KeyTerm>

### Phase 2: Location Confirmation

<PullQuote>"OK, Thursday at 10 AM. And we're at 123 Main Street, Carlsbad, right? Did I get that address right?"</PullQuote>

### Phase 3: Decision Makers Confirmation

<Callout type="important" title="THIS IS CRITICAL">
Do not proceed with just one decision maker unless you have no choice.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Will both of you be here? I want to make sure we're all on the same page, because decisions like this work best when everyone's involved."</PullQuote>

If they say no:

<PullQuote>"I really think it's important that you're both here so there are no surprises. Can we reschedule for a time that works for both of you?"</PullQuote>

### Phase 4: The Bill Request

<PullQuote>"One thing that'll make our appointment way more efficient — can you pull your most recent SDGE bill? I'm going to review it beforehand so when we sit down, I can walk you through exactly how much you're spending and how solar changes that number."</PullQuote>

### Phase 5: Expectation Setting

<PullQuote>"Tomorrow, our office will confirm this appointment and send you an email with all the details. Then I'm going to review your bill and prepare some preliminary numbers. I might have one quick question for you — would that be OK? Our goal is to come super prepared so we make the best use of your time on Thursday."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Reconfirmation Call">

<PullQuote>"Hey Jimmy! It's [Your Name] from KIN. I'm just calling to confirm our appointment this morning at 10. We're looking forward to meeting with you and showing you exactly how much you can save with solar. You still good for this morning?"</PullQuote>

**If they cancel:**

<PullQuote>"No problem at all. When would be better for you? Let's get you on the calendar."</PullQuote>

**If they've changed their mind:**

<PullQuote>"Totally understand — this is a big decision. Can I ask what changed? Here's the thing — I really think it's worth 30 minutes to just see the numbers. What if we make it really quick?"</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Special Situations">

### They Sound Hesitant

<PullQuote>"I can feel there's maybe some hesitation here. Can I ask — what's making you not 100% sure?"</PullQuote>

### "I Want to Think About It"

<PullQuote>"I hear you — this is a big decision. But thinking about it alone at home is tougher than seeing the actual numbers with an expert who can answer your questions. Let's block out time Thursday. You see the numbers, and then you can think about it from an informed position. Fair?"</PullQuote>

### "I'll Try to Make It"

<PullQuote>"I want to make sure we're clear on this. When I block time on my calendar, I'm turning away other customers who want that time slot. So I need to know — are you definitely coming Thursday at 10, or should I keep looking for someone who's more interested?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
This is a confidence move — it works. Set the expectation that your time has value.
</Callout>

</Accordion>
