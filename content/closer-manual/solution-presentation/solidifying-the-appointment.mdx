# Solidifying the Appointment: Getting the Biggest Commitment

## The Philosophy: Commitment Momentum

Every interaction with a prospect is an opportunity to get them moving closer to their yes. The bigger the commitment you get **before** the appointment, the higher your close rate **during** the appointment.

Think of commitment as momentum. Small commitments lead to bigger commitments. Your job is to get the biggest possible commitment before they hang up the phone or leave your presence.

> **Principle:** The person with the most specific, confirmed commitment closes more business than the person who takes a loose "maybe" appointment.

## The Commitment Hierarchy

Commitments aren't all equal. Some are stronger than others. Your goal is to climb this ladder:

### Level 1: Generic Interest ("Yeah, that sounds interesting")
- Lowest commitment level
- They might show up, they might not
- They don't really understand what they're committing to

### Level 2: Time Commitment ("OK, let's do it Thursday at 10")
- They've committed to a specific time
- This is your baseline

### Level 3: Logistical Confirmation ("Thursday at 10, and both of you will be there?")
- Both decision makers confirmed
- They understand what will happen
- Shows intentionality

### Level 4: Billing Confirmation ("And your address for the appointment is 123 Main St")
- All details confirmed and documented
- They see you've written it down
- Makes it real in their mind
- Higher show rate

### Level 5: Behavioral Confirmation ("Pull your SDGE bill so I can review it before we meet?")
- They take an action between now and the appointment
- Creates investment in the process
- Significantly increases show rate

### Level 6: Stakeholder Confirmation ("Let me know if your wife has any questions between now and Thursday?")
- Decision makers are all aligned
- They're discussing it together
- Creates positive momentum

### Level 7: Expectation Setting ("I'll have our design engineer call you Wednesday to confirm, sound good?")
- Clear handoff expectations
- They expect follow-up
- Builds trust in your process
- You control the next touch point

## Building Commitment: The Phased Approach

Don't ask for a giant commitment all at once. Build it in phases, making each small commitment the foundation for the next bigger one.

### Phase 1: The Appointment Time

**Word Track:**
> "Perfect. So I'd like to get you on the books. Are you available next Thursday, during the day? What works better for you - morning or afternoon?"

[Wait for their choice - don't suggest, let them choose. Ownership increases commitment]

> "Thursday morning works great. What time is best - 9 o'clock, 10 o'clock, or 11 o'clock?"

[Again, let them choose. They're building ownership]

**Key Principle:** When they choose the time, they have more ownership of the appointment.

### Phase 2: The Location Confirmation

**Word Track:**
> "OK, Thursday at 10 AM. And we're at 123 Main Street, Carlsbad, right? Did I get that address right?"

This serves multiple purposes:
- Confirms they understand where they live (seems simple, but catch errors now)
- Shows you've written it down
- Makes the appointment more concrete
- Gives them one more micro-commitment

### Phase 3: The Decision Makers Confirmation

**This is Critical.**

**Word Track:**
> "Perfect. Thursday at 10 at 123 Main. One more thing - will both of you be here? I want to make sure we're all on the same page, because decisions like this work best when everyone's involved."

**Why This Matters:**
- If only one decision maker will be there, you know you'll have issues
- Gives you a chance to address it now ("Should we reschedule so your wife can be here too?")
- Confirms both are invested in seeing this through

**If They Say No:**
> "I really think it's important that you're both here so there are no surprises. Can we reschedule for a time that works for both of you?"

Don't proceed with just one decision maker unless you have no choice.

### Phase 4: The Billing Document Request

**Word Track:**
> "Great. One thing that'll make our appointment way more efficient - can you pull your most recent SDGE bill? I'm going to review it beforehand so when we sit down, I can walk you through exactly how much you're spending and how solar changes that number.
>
> Do you have it handy, or will you need to go dig it out?"

**Why This Works:**
- They take an action (small investment)
- You review it (you're preparing, which builds confidence)
- Sets them up for a detailed, personalized conversation
- Shows you're organized and thorough

**If They Say "I don't know where it is":**
> "No problem - it's super easy. Log into your SDGE account on your phone or computer, or look in your mailbox for the paper one. You can take a picture of it or screenshot it and text it to me. I'll have it ready to review before Thursday."

### Phase 5: The Expectation-Setting Confirmation

**Word Track:**
> "Perfect. So here's what happens between now and Thursday. Tomorrow, our office will confirm this appointment and send you an email with all the details - our address, parking information, what to expect during the appointment, and what to bring.
>
> Then, I'm going to review your bill and prepare some preliminary numbers. I might have one quick question for you - would that be OK? I'll just text you. Our goal is to come super prepared so we make the best use of your time on Thursday.
>
> Does that all make sense? Any questions for me?"

This accomplishes:
- Sets clear expectations
- Shows your professionalism
- Gives them a heads-up about future contact
- Gives you permission to reach out
- Ends on a question (engagement)

## The Follow-Up Call: Reconfirming the Appointment

The night before or morning of the appointment, call to confirm. This serves multiple purposes:

### The Reconfirmation Word Track

**Word Track:**
> "Hey Jimmy! It's [Your Name] from KIN. I'm just calling to confirm our appointment this morning at 10. We're looking forward to meeting with you and showing you exactly how much you can save with solar. You still good for this morning?"

**Why This Approach:**
- Shows you're organized and professional
- Gives them one more chance to reschedule if needed
- Increases show rate (they remember they have it)
- Builds excitement ("we're looking forward to meeting with you")
- Asks a yes-or-no question (gets their verbal commitment again)

**If They Say "I Forgot" or "I Need to Reschedule":**
> "No problem at all. When would be better for you? Let's get you on the calendar."

[Don't show frustration - reschedule professionally]

**If They Say "We Actually Decided Not To":**
> "Totally understand - this is a big decision. Can I ask what changed your mind? [Listen] OK, here's the thing - I really think it's worth 30 minutes of your time to just see the numbers. What if we make it really quick? Could we do it another time that works better?"

[Try to save it, but don't be pushy]

## The Biggest Commitment: Pre-Appointment Actions

The highest-commitment appointments are ones where the prospect takes an action between booking and the appointment.

### Request 1: Pull the SDGE Bill
Already covered above - this is your baseline.

### Request 2: Email Address Confirmation
**Word Track:**
> "What's the best email for us to send you the appointment confirmation and any updates? [Confirm spelling] Got it - [repeat it back]"

### Request 3: Take a Roof Photo
**Word Track (optional but powerful):**
> "This is optional, but it would be super helpful - could you take a photo of your roof? Just go outside, snap a picture, and text it to me. That way, when I'm preparing for our appointment, I can see what we're working with."

This is a bigger ask, but if they do it:
- They've invested time
- You have the information
- You can reference it in the appointment
- Shows your organization

### Request 4: Discuss With Spouse
**Word Track:**
> "Between now and Thursday, feel free to chat with your wife about this. If either of you has questions, just text me or give me a call. I'm here to help you think through this."

This is genius because:
- It gives them permission to discuss positively
- It positions you as supportive
- They might raise objections now (easier to handle)
- They'll be more aligned by the appointment

## Special Situations: Getting Bigger Commitments

### Situation 1: They Sound Hesitant About the Appointment

**Word Track:**
> "I can feel like there's maybe some hesitation here. Can I ask - what's making you not 100% sure?"

[Listen for real concern]

> "Here's my thought - I wouldn't be pushing this if I didn't think it was worth your time. I'm confident that if you look at the numbers, you're going to want to move forward or at least understand the option better. Can we just do it? What would make you feel better about coming?"

### Situation 2: They Want to "Think About It" Instead of Booking

**Word Track:**
> "I hear you - this is a big decision. But here's what I've found - thinking about it alone at home is tougher than seeing the actual numbers with an expert who can answer your questions.
>
> How about this - let's block out time on Thursday. You see the numbers, you understand the process, and then you can think about it from an informed position. Fair?"

### Situation 3: They're Only Half-Committed ("I'll Try to Make It")

**Word Track:**
> "I want to make sure we're clear on this. When I block time on my calendar, I'm turning away other customers who want that time slot. So I need to know - are you definitely coming Thursday at 10, or should I keep looking for someone who's more interested?"

[This is a confidence move - it works]

> "Great - so I'm confirmed that Thursday at 10 is locked in. Thank you."

## The Day-Of Appointment: Commitment Handoff

When they arrive for the appointment, your opening should acknowledge all the commitment they've made:

**Word Track:**
> "Hey Jimmy! Thanks so much for coming in. I know you're busy, and I really appreciate you taking the time. I reviewed your SDGE bill, talked to our design team, and I'm really excited about what we can do for you. Let's dive in."

This acknowledges:
- Their time commitment
- Your preparation
- Excitement about helping them
- You're ready to move forward

## The Numbers: Why Commitment Matters

**Show Rate Correlation:**
- Generic interest: ~40% show rate
- Time confirmed: ~60% show rate
- Both decision makers confirmed: ~80% show rate
- Bill pulled and reviewed beforehand: ~85% show rate
- All five commitment phases: ~95% show rate

The difference between a loose appointment and a locked-in appointment is the difference between breaking even on leads and actually closing business.

## Key Takeaways on Solidifying Appointments

1. **Get commitment in phases** - Don't ask for everything at once
2. **Let them choose details** - Time selection, location confirmation - they own it
3. **Confirm decision makers** - Non-negotiable
4. **Get the bill before the appointment** - Sets you up for success
5. **Create expectations** - They know what's coming
6. **Reconfirm the night before** - Increases show rate
7. **Request pre-appointment actions** - Investment leads to show rate
8. **Use confidence** - Your expectation of showing up transfers to them
