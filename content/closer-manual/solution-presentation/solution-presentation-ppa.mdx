# Solution Presentation PPA

## Overview

The Solution Presentation follows this flow:

- Case Open
- Rapport
- Set Expectations
- Position Statement
- Reframe (CO, NA)
- Deeper Needs Audit (Reframe NA, OPEC)
- Transition in Terms of Benefit
- Proposal (Power Problem, Save Money Tab, Commonly Asked Questions)
- Service Levels and Pricing
- Call to Action Close

## Deeper Needs Audit

Use **OPEC** framework and ask drill down questions.

> **Tip:** Signals that Solidify PPA:
> 1. Lower Income
> 2. Lower tax liability/no tax liability
> 3. Only wants lowest monthly bill/day 1 savings
> 4. 2+ Batteries that might create sticker shock with large package price
> 5. Moving soon and will be renting the house out
> 6. Heavy doesn't want to have debt or pay interest option close PPA/Cash

### Opportunity Query Examples

**How long do you plan on living in the home?**

- Would you sell it, rent it out, or pass it on to family?

**Lifestyle Changes and Usage:**

- When we design your solar system we base it off of your annual usage. Do you anticipate any lifestyle changes that will increase how much power you use.. Such as A/C, EV, Jacuzzi.

> **Tip:** Don't size any of this unless it's happening within 1-1.5 years. Unless they are 100% adamant about it. "Our system is like legos. We can always add panels later."

**Time-of-Use Patterns:**

- As you know SDGE conveniently charges you 4x the amount for power between 4pm-9pm. Would you say you are more conservative at that time or do you just use power whenever you want?
  - If they say they are conservative pour gas on the fire.
  - If they say they use it whenever you want, say, "GOOD!"

**Night Time Consumption:**

- It's important for us to know how much power you consume at night time in order to size your battery appropriately. What major appliances do you use when it gets dark? (A/C, Laundry, Dishwasher, EV)

> **Tip:** Shift whatever usage you can to the daytime to provide the customer more freedom and require a smaller battery.

**Social Proof:**

- Do you know a friend or family member that has gone solar that has had a really good experience or even a bad one? Tell me about it.
  - What did they tell you about the program they chose?

**Tax Liability Assessment:**

- There are several different ways you can go solar with different tax benefits. In order for me to determine what program fits best for you, about how much do you pay in federal income taxes?
  - "I have no idea." Yeah, taxes are complicated. No worries. If you don't mind me asking how much do you make a year?

> **Tip:** If they don't know how much they pay in taxes that usually means they don't pay a lot in taxes. If they make a ton example 150k+ you know they have higher tax liability

**Motivations:**

- Most of my clients when going solar wanted a few things. They wanted control over their bill. They wanted to use more power without having to pay a premium, and lastly they wanted to do their part for the environment. What motivates you most to go solar?

### Problems Query Examples

**Probe for Previous Objections:**

- Yesterday you shared with me that you **haven't gone solar yet because**... Example: you felt your bill was too low, and that you didn't want to lease the system. Did I get that right?

**For Spouses:**

- (Spouse) What's been holding you back from going solar?

**Follow-up Concerns:**

- Since we last spoke what else have you thought of that's been holding you back from going solar?

**Past Research:**

- When you last looked into solar what are some things you were looking for that you didn't find?

## Proposal

### Opening

> Thanks for getting your bill for me. We're not going to be doing anything with the gas portion, our main goal today is to completely eliminate your electric bill. Last month you used "x" kwh and that cost you $230. A kwh is a measurement for energy, like a gallon of gas for your home.

### Energy Production Baseline

> This graph shows us how much power you guys used over the last year, which gives us a baseline for how many panels we need to offset that.

### Break Down Charges

- **Electricity Generation**
  - Only $44 of your $130 bill...
- **Transmission**
- **Distribution**
  - These are just fancy words for shipping and handling
  - When you go solar we eliminate all these charges.

### Rate Increases Education

**Word Track:**

> I'm going to show you a real example of this...

**Using Historical Data:**

- Donn Johnson Bill
  - Year 2010 & Address
  - $49.67 divided by 359 kWh = 14 cents a kWh
- Total bill divided by kWh = Their cost per kWh
- If they have a **CARE** discount show TOU Rate schedule on SDGE.com instead of doing their bill.

### Rate Increase Data

- **2024:** 17.6%
  - Your average bill is $250. This means that next year alone your bill is going up $44 a month.
- **2025:** 10.5%
- **2026:** 9.2%
- **2027:** 7.7%
- **Total:** That's a 54% increase in just 4 years.

**Monopoly Framing:**

> It's almost like we are in an unwritten contract with SDGE where they get to dictate what the rate increases are, and we're at their mercy. I mean, think about it, you've lived here 10 years. Have you ever had the opportunity to use a different utility company? Exactly! They're a monopoly.

### Three Routes Framework

Present the three options:

1. You pay a power bill with SDGE until you die.
2. You cover the cost up front and buy the solar outright.
3. We replace SDGE for $0 money out of pocket cutting your bill by 30-40% day 1.

**Closing Question:**

> Which of these options makes the most sense for you? Ya, most of my clients choose the exact same option.

### PPA Explanation

**Word Track:**

> Let's jump into what that looks like for you. The program that fits your goals the most is called a power purchase agreement. This means we replace SDGE and become your utility provider for the next 25 years. Instead of the rates going up 18% next year and 54% over the next 4 years, by going solar with KOTA your rate increase will be capped at just 3.5%. This accounts for inflation and maintaining your system.

**Qualifications:**

> With a power purchase agreement there are $0 upfront costs. In order to qualify you need a to own the title of the property, and have at least a 650 credit score. Are we good there?

### Net Metering

**Explanation:**

> The last thing we need to go over to During the day you will produce all of your energy from the solar. All of the excess energy that you don't use will go to your battery. During the night time you will use the energy stored on your battery that you produced earlier in the day.

**Grid Connection:**

> Now, SDGE doesn't do anything for free. They will charge you $16 a month to be connected to their grid.

**True Up:**

> Each month you will have a true up which is a final count of how much your solar produced vs how much you consumed. If you used 3 times the amount of energy than what you've historically done, obviously you would pay SDGE the difference.

**System Sizing:**

> At KOTA we always oversize the system putting in the best situation to not pay SDGE.
