<Lead>The Solution Presentation follows a structured flow from case open through rapport, needs audit, proposal, and close. This guide covers the complete PPA presentation word tracks.</Lead>

<Accordion title="Case Open: Rapport & Walk Around">

### Rapport

Build initial connection by asking about **Family, Occupation, Hobbies**:
- How long have you lived here?
- Is this the first time you've installed solar on a home?

### Walk Around

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Check the Electrical Panel">
Assess capacity and condition.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Look for Battery Placement">
Identify mounting location.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Roof Condition">
Check for obstructions, damage, age.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Check for Trees">
Shading assessment.
</Step>
<Step number={5} title="Compliment the Property">
"What have you guys done with the place?"
</Step>
</Steps>

### Set Expectation

<PullQuote>"Thank you for having me today. First off, I want to ask you a couple of questions to get a good understanding of your situation. After that we'll break down your bill and show you how we're able to save you money. Then we'll go over some commonly asked questions, and show you what everything costs. Sound fair?"</PullQuote>

### Position Statement

<PullQuote>"My goal today is to help you understand that since you've lived here you have already been paying for solar. You just don't have it yet. What I mean is you have already been paying SDGE since the first day you moved in. All we'll be doing today is redirecting your money toward an asset, instead of a never-ending liability in SDGE where the bleeding never stops."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Deeper Needs Audit (OPEC Framework)">

<KeyTerm term="OPEC Framework">Use OPEC — Opportunity, Problems, Effects, Consequences — with drill-down questions at every stage.</KeyTerm>

<Callout type="insight" title="SIGNALS THAT SOLIDIFY PPA">
1. Lower income
2. Lower tax liability / no tax liability
3. Only wants lowest monthly bill / day-1 savings
4. 2+ batteries that might create sticker shock
5. Moving soon and will be renting
6. Doesn't want debt or interest — close PPA or Cash
</Callout>

### Opportunity Queries

**How long do you plan on living in the home?**
- Would you sell it, rent it out, or pass it on to family?

**Lifestyle Changes:**

<PullQuote>"When we design your solar system we base it off your annual usage. Do you anticipate any lifestyle changes that will increase how much power you use — A/C, EV, Jacuzzi?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Don't size anything unless it's happening within 1-1.5 years. *"Our system is like legos. We can always add panels later."*
</Callout>

**Time-of-Use Patterns:**

<PullQuote>"As you know SDGE conveniently charges you 4x the amount for power between 4pm-9pm. Would you say you are more conservative at that time or do you just use power whenever you want?"</PullQuote>

- If conservative: **pour gas on the fire**
- If they use freely: say **"GOOD!"**

**Night Time Consumption:**

<PullQuote>"It's important for us to know how much power you consume at night in order to size your battery appropriately. What major appliances do you use when it gets dark? A/C, laundry, dishwasher, EV?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Shift whatever usage you can to daytime — more freedom for the customer, smaller battery required.
</Callout>

**Social Proof:**

<PullQuote>"Do you know a friend or family member that has gone solar that has had a really good experience or even a bad one? Tell me about it. What did they tell you about the program they chose?"</PullQuote>

**Tax Liability Assessment:**

<PullQuote>"There are several different ways you can go solar with different tax benefits. In order for me to determine what program fits best for you, about how much do you pay in federal income taxes?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
If they don't know how much they pay in taxes, that usually means they don't pay much. If they make $150k+, you know they have higher tax liability.
</Callout>

**Motivations:**

<PullQuote>"Most of my clients when going solar wanted a few things. They wanted control over their bill. They wanted to use more power without having to pay a premium. And lastly they wanted to do their part for the environment. What motivates you most to go solar?"</PullQuote>

### Problems Queries

<PullQuote>"Yesterday you shared with me that you haven't gone solar yet because... [example: you felt your bill was too low, and that you didn't want to lease the system]. Did I get that right?"</PullQuote>

- **(Spouse):** "What's been holding you back from going solar?"
- **Follow-up:** "Since we last spoke what else have you thought of that's been holding you back?"
- **Past Research:** "When you last looked into solar what are some things you were looking for that you didn't find?"

</Accordion>

### Low Bill (Version 1)

<PullQuote>"Based on our conversation up to this point I'm confident we will keep your payments fixed so they'll never go up."</PullQuote>

### Low Bill (Version 2)

<PullQuote>"Based on our conversation up to this point I'm confident that we will completely eliminate your bill, instead of throwing your money away each month toward a never-ending expense."</PullQuote>

### Roof

<PullQuote>"Based on our conversation up to this point I'm confident that we can give you a roof + solar for the same, or less than you currently pay SDGE."</PullQuote>

### Moving

<PullQuote>"Based on our conversation up to this point I'm confident that we can save you 50% on your bill while you currently live here, and allow for an easy transfer when you sell the home."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="important" title="REMEMBER THE BLACK SWAN">
Tie the transition directly to the core concern you uncovered in discovery.
</Callout>

**Call to Action:**

<PullQuote>"I'm ready to jump into your bill and show you how we can achieve that. Are you ready for me to move forward?"</PullQuote>

### The Three Routes

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Stay with SDGE">
You pay a power bill with SDGE until you die.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Buy Outright">
You cover the cost up front and buy the solar outright.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Replace SDGE ($0 Out of Pocket)">
We replace SDGE for $0 money out of pocket, cutting your bill by **30-40% day 1**.
</Step>
</Steps>

<PullQuote>"Which of these options makes the most sense for you? Ya, most of my clients choose the exact same option."</PullQuote>

### PPA Explanation

<PullQuote>"The program that fits your goals the most is called a power purchase agreement. This means we replace SDGE and become your utility provider for the next 25 years. Instead of the rates going up 18% next year and 54% over the next 4 years, by going solar with KIN your rate increase will be capped at just 3.5%."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Qualifications: $0 upfront costs. Must own the title of the property and have at least a **650 credit score**. "Are we good there?"
</Callout>

### Net Metering

<PullQuote>"During the day you produce all of your energy from solar. All excess goes to your battery. During the night you use the energy stored on your battery that you produced earlier in the day."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">

### The 10-Year Comparison

<PullQuote>"Here we have a graph comparing the next 10 years. In red, SDGE at a 10% annual increase. By going solar with KIN, we cap the increase at just 3.5%."</PullQuote>

**Current State:**

<PullQuote>"Currently your average bill is $263. If you stay with SDGE, in 5 years you'd be paying $385, and in 10 years $620. Still using the exact same amount of power."</PullQuote>

**Solar With KIN:**

<PullQuote>"When going solar with KIN you'll be able to use 15% more power. Instead of paying SDGE $263 that continues to climb, I've got your payments all the way down to just $190. In five years it will be $218, and in 10 years $259 — which is still less than you currently pay."</PullQuote>

### The Powerful Close

<PullQuote>"It'd be like as if SDGE came to you and said: you've been a faithful customer. So here's what we're going to do. We're going to give you 10% more power so you can use the A/C more freely. Now, instead of increasing rates by 18% this year and 54% over 4 years — I'm going to bring you all the way down to just $190, giving you immediate savings of $103 a month from day 1. If SDGE said that, would you take that deal?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
If they're gung-ho about battery backup and have blackout protection: *"And on top of that we're going to give you a backup battery that allows you to have your essential needs met in the event of a blackout."*
</Callout>
