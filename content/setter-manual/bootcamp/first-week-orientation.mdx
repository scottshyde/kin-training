# First Week Orientation: Your Starting Point

## Welcome to KOTA

You've decided to join our team. That's a great decision. Your first week will set the tone for your success here.

This orientation covers everything you need to know, from schedule to mindset to what to bring.

## Day 1: Welcome & Onboarding

### Morning: Paperwork & Admin
- Complete I-9 verification and tax forms
- Review and sign employee handbook
- Set up email, phone, and CRM access
- Take company photo for directory
- Review company policies and code of conduct

### Afternoon: Meet Your Team
- Meet your team manager and fellow setters
- Tour the office and get familiar with locations
- Review organizational structure
- Understand your role and reporting structure
- Get to know your territory and customer base

### Evening: Initial Training
- Watch company orientation video
- Review KIN Home's mission and values
- Understand our products: residential solar systems
- Overview of the setter role in the sales process
- Preview of the week's schedule

## Day 2: Product & Process Knowledge

### Solar 101
- **What is solar?** How photovoltaic cells convert sunlight to electricity
- **System components:** Panels, inverters, racking, wiring, monitoring
- **How it works:** Grid-tied systems and net metering
- **Common sizes:** 4kW to 10kW typical residential systems
- **Performance:** What customers can expect in production and savings

### The Sales Process
- **Setter role:** You find customers and set appointments with closers
- **Why setters matter:** 50% of a closer's job is working with qualified appointments
- **Customer journey:** From prospect to closed customer
- **Your responsibility:** Qualifying prospects and scheduling site surveys
- **Key metric:** Appointment show rate and quality

### CRM System Training
- Log into the company CRM
- Enter a sample prospect (practice activity)
- Schedule a mock appointment
- Review prospect notes and history
- Understand follow-up procedures

## Day 3: Door-to-Door Fundamentals

### Territory Assignment
- Review your assigned territory (neighborhood/area)
- Understand demographic breakdown
- Review previous year's performance in your area
- Identify high-opportunity streets and homes
- Get familiar with local communities

### The Knock & Introduction
- Professional appearance standards
- What to wear (company polo, clean pants, comfortable shoes)
- First impression importance (3 seconds!)
- The approach: confident, friendly, non-threatening
- The opener: "Hi, I'm [name] with KIN Home..."

### Initial Discovery
- Ask about current utility company and bill
- Assess interest in solar
- Identify pain points (high bills, environmental concerns)
- Determine decision-maker availability
- Build rapport and trust

### Getting to Closer
- Transition from setter to closer conversation
- Introduce the closer (or schedule appointment)
- Explain what happens next in the process
- Answer basic questions (without overselling)
- Confirm appointment in CRM

## Day 4: Scripts & Role-Playing

### Core Scripts
1. **The Door Knock**
   - "Hi, my name is [name]. I'm out talking to families in the neighborhood about solar energy..."

2. **The Problem Identification**
   - "Can I ask you a quick question? Are you unhappy with your current electric company?"

3. **The Qualifier**
   - "Do you own your home? Great! Have you thought about solar?"

4. **The Closer Handoff**
   - "Let me get my manager who specializes in designing solar systems..."

### Your First Role-Play
- Manager plays the homeowner
- You do the door knock
- Practice your approach and questions
- Receive feedback
- Practice again (repetition builds confidence)

### Common Responses & How to Handle Them
- "Not interested" → "I understand, can I ask why?"
- "Too expensive" → "Actually, most families don't pay much at all..."
- "We rent" → "Thanks, can I leave information for future reference?"
- "Call me later" → "I'd be happy to! What's the best number?"

## Day 5: First Territory Walk & Ride-Along

### Territory Orientation
- Walk your assigned area with team lead
- Identify best doors to knock on
- Note obstacles and challenges
- Understand neighborhood dynamics
- Get comfortable with your area

### Ride-Along with Experienced Setter
- Observe how a top performer approaches doors
- Watch how they handle objections
- See how they qualify prospects
- Notice their energy and confidence
- Ask questions afterward

### Your First Solo Door Knocks
- Start with 5-10 doors in low-pressure area
- Focus on getting comfortable with the process
- Don't worry about perfect conversion yet
- Goal: Build confidence and get comfortable talking to strangers
- Manager shadowing at first, then independent

## Expectations Going Forward

### Week 2-4: Building Consistency
- Door knocking 5 days per week
- Minimum 30-50 doors per day
- 3-5 quality appointments per day
- Daily check-ins with team lead

### Success Markers
- **Comfort level:** Confident door knocking without anxiety
- **Consistency:** Same activity daily
- **Appointment quality:** Closers appreciate your work
- **Appointment show rate:** 70%+ of your appointments close-ready
- **Volume:** 5-10 appointments per week

## Remember

Your first week sets the tone. Work hard, ask questions, observe everything, and stay positive. You'll be up to speed faster than you think!
