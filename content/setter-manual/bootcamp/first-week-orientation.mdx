# First Week Orientation: Your Starting Point

<Lead>Welcome to KIN. Your first week will set the tone for your success here. This orientation covers everything you need to know, from schedule to mindset to what to bring.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Day 1: Welcome & Onboarding">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Morning: Paperwork & Admin">
Complete I-9 verification and tax forms. Review and sign employee handbook. Set up email, phone, and CRM access. Take company photo. Review policies and code of conduct.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Afternoon: Meet Your Team">
Meet your team manager and fellow setters. Tour the office. Review organizational structure. Understand your role and reporting structure. Get to know your territory.
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Evening: Initial Training">
Watch company orientation video. Review KIN Home's mission and values. Understand our products: residential solar systems. Overview of the setter role. Preview the week's schedule.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

**The Knock & Introduction:**

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "3 SEC", label: "First Impression Window" },
  { value: "CONFIDENT", label: "Friendly, Non-Threatening" }
]} />

<Callout type="tip">
Professional appearance standards: company polo, clean pants, comfortable shoes. Smile. You belong here.
</Callout>

**Initial Discovery → Getting to Closer:**

Ask about current utility and bill. Assess interest. Identify pain points. Determine decision-maker availability. Build rapport. Then transition to booking the closer appointment.

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Day 4: Scripts & Role-Playing">

**Core Scripts:**

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="The Door Knock">

<PullQuote>"Hi, my name is [name]. I'm out talking to families in the neighborhood about solar energy..."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="The Problem Identification">

<PullQuote>"Can I ask you a quick question? Are you unhappy with your current electric company?"</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="The Qualifier">

<PullQuote>"Do you own your home? Great! Have you thought about solar?"</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="The Closer Handoff">

<PullQuote>"Let me get my manager who specializes in designing solar systems..."</PullQuote>
</Step>
</Steps>

**Common Responses:**

| Response | Your Reply |
|----------|-----------|
| "Not interested" | "I understand, can I ask why?" |
| "Too expensive" | "Actually, most families don't pay much at all..." |
| "We rent" | "Thanks, can I leave information for future reference?" |
| "Call me later" | "I'd be happy to! What's the best number?" |

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Day 5: First Territory Walk & Ride-Along">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Territory Orientation">
Walk your assigned area with team lead. Identify best doors. Note obstacles. Understand neighborhood dynamics.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Ride-Along with Experienced Setter">
Observe a top performer's door approach, objection handling, qualifying process. Notice their energy and confidence. Ask questions afterward.
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Your First Solo Door Knocks">
Start with 5-10 doors in a low-pressure area. Focus on getting comfortable with the process. Don't worry about perfect conversion yet.
</Step>
</Steps>

<KeyTerm term="Day 5 Goal">Build confidence and get comfortable talking to strangers. Manager shadows first, then independent.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>
