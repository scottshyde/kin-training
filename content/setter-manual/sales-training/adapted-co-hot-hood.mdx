# Adapted CO — Hot Hood

A "hot hood" is a neighborhood where KIN has recently done installs, where solar activity is high, or where multiple neighbors are already customers. It's your best territory — and it requires a slightly different approach than cold ground.

---

## Why Adapt the Case Open Here

In a standard cold approach, the case open's job is to establish credibility from scratch. In a hot hood, you have something better than credibility — you have evidence. Physical, visible, next-door evidence.

The adapted case open leads with that evidence immediately.

---

## The Adapted Opening

> *"Hey — I was just next door with the [Customer Name] family. They just went through the install and I was checking in on how things went. While I was in the neighborhood I figured I'd knock — do you mind if I ask you two quick questions?"*

What's different:
- The neighbor is the entire Case Open
- No need to explain KIN or the product — it's visible
- The tone is casual and already inside the social circle
- "Checking in" signals service, not sales

---

## When You Have Multiple Installs

If you have two or more customers on the same street or block:

> *"We've got three families on this block who just went through the program — the [Name 1]s, the [Name 2]s, and the [Name 3]s. I wanted to swing by while I was here and make sure you had the chance to see the same numbers."*

The list creates social proof that compounds. One neighbor is a data point. Three is a pattern.

---

## The Panel Check in a Hot Hood

In a hot hood, you can often point to a visible system on a nearby roof:

> *"You can actually see Sean's panels from here — do you see those on the roof over there? He went from $180 to $99. That's what I want to show you the numbers on."*

Visible evidence at a distance is more persuasive than anything you can say. Use it.

---

## Handling "I've Heard About It Already"

In a hot hood, prospects have often already heard about the program from neighbors. This is a gift:

> *"Good — that actually saves me some explaining. What did they tell you about it?"*

Let them tell you. Whatever they say gives you your opening. If they heard something positive, validate it. If they heard something wrong or negative, correct it with specificity.

---

## Adapted Needs Audit Bridge

In a hot hood, the needs audit bridge can reference the social context:

> *"The reason most of your neighbors decided to go forward was that the old reason for not doing it — the debt, the price, the savings not being enough — doesn't apply the same way anymore. What's held you back when you've heard about it?"*

You're borrowing the collective experience of their neighbors rather than one invented "Sean." This is more credible and more immediate.

---

## The Tone Shift

Hot hood energy should be warmer and less formal than cold territory. You're not introducing yourself to strangers — you're a person who was already on this block for good reasons. Let that reality show in how you carry yourself.

Move slower. Smile more. Reference the neighborhood. You belong here.
