# Adapted CO — Hot Hood

<Lead>A "hot hood" is a neighborhood where KIN has recently done installs, where solar activity is high, or where multiple neighbors are already customers. It's your best territory — and it requires a slightly different approach.</Lead>

<Callout type="insight">
In a standard cold approach, the Case Open establishes credibility from scratch. In a hot hood, you have something better than credibility — you have **evidence**. Physical, visible, next-door evidence.
</Callout>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Adapted Opening">

<PullQuote>"Hey — I was just next door with the [Customer Name] family. They just went through the install and I was checking in on how things went. While I was in the neighborhood I figured I'd knock — do you mind if I ask you two quick questions?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="What's Different">The neighbor IS the entire Case Open. No need to explain KIN or the product — it's visible. "Checking in" signals service, not sales.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="When You Have Multiple Installs">

<PullQuote>"We've got three families on this block who just went through the program — the [Name 1]s, the [Name 2]s, and the [Name 3]s. I wanted to swing by while I was here and make sure you had the chance to see the same numbers."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
One neighbor is a data point. Three is a **pattern**. The list creates social proof that compounds.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Panel Check">

In a hot hood, you can often point to a **visible system** on a nearby roof:

<PullQuote>"You can actually see Sean's panels from here — do you see those on the roof over there? He went from $180 to $99. That's what I want to show you the numbers on."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Visual Evidence">Visible evidence at a distance is more persuasive than anything you can say. Use it.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Handling 'I've Heard About It Already'">

This is a **gift**, not a roadblock:

<PullQuote>"Good — that actually saves me some explaining. What did they tell you about it?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Let them tell you. Whatever they say gives you your opening. Positive = validate. Wrong/negative = correct with specificity.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Adapted Needs Audit Bridge">

<PullQuote>"The reason most of your neighbors decided to go forward was that the old reason for not doing it — the debt, the price, the savings not being enough — doesn't apply the same way anymore. What's held you back when you've heard about it?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
You're borrowing the **collective experience** of their neighbors rather than one invented "Sean." More credible. More immediate.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Tone Shift">

<KeyTerm term="Hot Hood Energy">Warmer and less formal than cold territory. You're not introducing yourself to strangers — you're a person who was already on this block for good reasons.</KeyTerm>

Move slower. Smile more. Reference the neighborhood. **You belong here.**

</Accordion>
