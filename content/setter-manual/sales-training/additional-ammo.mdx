# Additional Ammo

<Lead>These aren't scripts for structured moments in the pitch — they're your toolkit for everything that doesn't fit a category. The unexpected comments, the curveball responses, the moments where you need something real to say.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The 'Looked at It' Opener">

<Callout type="insight">
When a prospect says they've "looked at solar before," that's a **warm lead** — not a rejection. They're already in the consideration window.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Oh good — that actually makes this easier. Most people who've looked at it looked at the old version. What specifically didn't make sense when you did?"</PullQuote>

Then listen. Whatever they name is your **Black Swan**. Solve it.

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Top Mindset: The Three Rules">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "1", label: "Be interesting — boring loses before the pitch starts" },
  { value: "2", label: "Have fun — real enjoyment, not performed" },
  { value: "3", label: "Measure inputs, not outcomes" }
]} />

<KeyTerm term="Input Over Outcome">You can control how many doors you knock. You can't control who answers. Measure yourself on inputs.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Triple Choice Murder Line">

<PullQuote>"There are really only three categories of people who go solar right now. The ideal situation is zero money down — most people can do that. The fortunate few actually want to put some cash in to lower their monthly. And then there are people who are just exempt from doing it upfront at all. Which sounds most like you?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Why It Works">Creates a taxonomy the prospect self-selects into — and none of the options are bad. There's no "not interested" option in the framing.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="'You're Knocking on My Door for This?'">

<PullQuote>"I know, right? Honestly, door to door is the only way we keep our overhead low enough to make the numbers work the way they do. That's part of why Sean's saving $42 a month instead of $20. It's a little old school but it works."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Disarm with humor and honesty. Don't be defensive.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="'Is This Solar?'">

<PullQuote>"It's part of it — it's actually a battery backup program. The solar is how we power it. But the main thing we're doing is replacing your SDGE bill. Two questions?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="The Frame Shift">"Battery backup program" shifts the frame. Most people have solar fatigue. Nobody has battery fatigue yet.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="'Savings Isn't Enough' Reframes">

**Control:**

<PullQuote>"The bigger thing for most people isn't the savings, it's the control. You've never had any say in what SDGE charges you. This gives you that."</PullQuote>

**Predictability:**

<PullQuote>"SDGE has never lowered its rates. Not once in the last decade. With solar, you know exactly what your energy cost is for the next 25 years."</PullQuote>

**Ownership:**

<PullQuote>"You're not just saving money — you're paying for something you'll own. When your car is paid off, you still have a car. When your system is paid off, you have a power plant on your roof."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Moving Objection">

<PullQuote>"Two things happen with solar when you sell. Option one: it transfers to the next buyer — and educated realtors are seeing solar as a selling feature. Option two: if the buyer doesn't want it, we handle the payoff at closing. Either way, you're not stuck."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Follow with: *"How long are you planning to stay?"* — If more than 2 years, the math almost always works.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="'We Don't Do Business at the Door'">

<PullQuote>"I completely respect that — and I'm not asking you to do anything at the door. All I'm asking for is 90 seconds to see if this even qualifies your home. My manager handles all of the actual business. I'm just the scout."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="The Separation">Separating yourself from "the deal" lowers the stakes. You're not selling — you're qualifying.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Takeaway Lines">

<PullQuote>"To be honest, not every home qualifies. I'd hate for you to miss out without at least knowing."</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"The only thing I'm certain about is that SDGE isn't going to call you with a better offer."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Booking Same-Day">

<PullQuote>"Is now actually a decent time? My manager is in the area and I could have them here in about 45 minutes. Or I can set something for later this week if that's better."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
The "now" option closes faster than anything. If they say yes, call the manager immediately.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Key Vocabulary">

| **Say This** | **Not This** |
|----------|----------|
| **Investment** | Payment |
| **System** | Panels |
| **Replace your bill** | Save you money |
| **Qualify your home** | Sell you solar |
| **Zero down** | Free |

<KeyTerm term="Language Signals Intent">"Investment" signals value. "Payment" signals obligation. Choose accordingly.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>
