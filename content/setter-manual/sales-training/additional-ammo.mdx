# Additional Ammo

These aren't scripts for structured moments in the pitch — they're your toolkit for everything that doesn't fit a category. The unexpected comments, the curveball responses, the moments where you need something real to say.

---

## The "Looked at It" Opener

When a prospect says they've "looked at solar before," that's actually a warm lead — not a rejection. They're already in the consideration window.

> *"Oh good — that actually makes this easier. Most people who've looked at it looked at the old version. What specifically didn't make sense when you did?"*

Then listen. Whatever they name is your Black Swan. Solve it.

---

## Top Mindset: The Three Rules

Before any shift, internalize these:

1. **Be interesting.** A boring rep loses before the pitch starts. People give time to people they find engaging.
2. **Have fun.** Not performed fun — real enjoyment of the interaction. If you're not enjoying it, they can tell.
3. **Tie your success to your input, not your outcomes.** You can control how many doors you knock. You can't control who answers. Measure yourself on inputs.

---

## The Triple Choice Murder Line

When cost comes up and they say they don't want to pay upfront, use this:

> *"There are really only three categories of people who go solar right now. The ideal situation is zero money down — most people can do that. The fortunate few actually want to put some cash in to lower their monthly. And then there are people who are just exempt from doing it upfront at all. Which sounds most like you?"*

The "murder line" works because it creates a taxonomy that the prospect self-selects into — and none of the options are bad. There's no "not interested" option in the framing.

---

## "You're Knocking on My Door for This?"

If they seem surprised or slightly annoyed by the door approach:

> *"I know, right? Honestly, door to door is the only way we keep our overhead low enough to make the numbers work the way they do. That's part of why Sean's saving $42 a month instead of $20. It's a little old school but it works."*

Disarm it with humor and honesty. Don't be defensive.

---

## "Is This Solar?"

> *"It's part of it — it's actually a battery backup program. The solar is how we power it. But the main thing we're doing is replacing your SDGE bill. Two questions?"*

The phrase "battery backup program" shifts the frame. Most people have solar fatigue. Nobody has battery fatigue yet.

---

## "Savings Isn't Enough" Reframes

When a prospect isn't motivated by savings alone, shift to control and predictability:

**Control:**
> *"Here's the thing about saving $40 a month — that's real money. But the bigger thing for most people isn't the savings, it's the control. You've never had any say in what SDGE charges you. This gives you that."*

**Predictability:**
> *"SDGE has never lowered its rates. Not once in the last decade. With solar, you know exactly what your energy cost is for the next 25 years. It's the same payment every month. That's worth something."*

**Reframe from savings to ownership:**
> *"You're not just saving money — you're paying for something you'll own. When your car is paid off, you still have a car. When your system is paid off, you have a power plant on your roof."*

---

## The Moving Objection

When they say they're planning to move or might sell:

> *"Two things happen with solar when you sell. Option one: it transfers to the next buyer — and educated realtors are actually seeing solar as a selling feature, especially in California. Option two: if the buyer doesn't want it, we handle the payoff at closing. Either way, you're not stuck."*

Follow with:
> *"How long are you planning to stay?"*

If it's more than 2 years, the math almost always works.

---

## "We Don't Do Business at the Door"

This one sounds firm, but it's actually a policy, not a feeling. Respond to the policy:

> *"I completely respect that — and I'm not asking you to do anything at the door. All I'm asking for is 90 seconds to see if this even qualifies your home. My manager handles all of the actual business. I'm just the scout."*

Separating yourself from "the deal" lowers the stakes significantly. You're not selling — you're qualifying.

---

## Takeaway Lines

When someone is on the fence and you want to create genuine scarcity:

> *"To be honest, not every home qualifies. I'd hate for you to miss out without at least knowing."*

> *"I'm not going to push you — but I'd feel bad if your neighbor ended up qualifying and you missed the window."*

> *"The only thing I'm certain about is that SDGE isn't going to call you with a better offer."*

---

## Booking Same-Day

Same-day appointments are gold. If the prospect is home and available:

> *"Is now actually a decent time? My manager is in the area and I could have them here in about 45 minutes. Or I can set something for later this week if that's better."*

The "now" option closes faster than anything. If they say yes, call the manager immediately.

---

## Key Vocabulary

These word choices matter more than you think:

| Say This | Not This |
|----------|----------|
| Investment | Payment |
| System | Panels |
| Replace your bill | Save you money |
| Qualify your home | Sell you solar |
| Zero down | Free |

Language signals intent. "Investment" signals value. "Payment" signals obligation. Choose accordingly.
