# The Complete Door Approach: From Knock to Appointment

Your goal at the door is simple but specific: set a quality appointment with both decision makers present, with the SDGE bill, with realistic expectations about what happens next. You're not closing the deal at the door. You're opening the conversation and moving them to the next step.

<Accordion title="The Complete Door Approach: Word Tracks (60 Seconds)">
### Part 1: Rapport (14 seconds)

**Word Track:**
> "Hey, how are you? I'm with LGCY. I take care of your neighbor Sean."

[Keep it brief. Smile. Be genuine.]

**What's happening:**
- Establishing friendliness
- Name-dropping neighbor (social proof)
- Introducing company
- 14 seconds total

### Part 2: Position (10 seconds)

**Word Track:**
> "I took his SDGE budget and redirected it toward solar."

[Paint the picture. They already understand the concept.]

**What's happening:**
- Showing relevance (neighbor, same neighborhood)
- Explaining what you do simply
- "Redirect" is powerful language (not "replace," not "eliminate" - just redirect)

### Part 3: Benefit (9 seconds)

**Word Track:**
> "Protecting him from the 18% increase coming this year."

[Show urgency and benefit clearly.]

**What's happening:**
- Benefit statement (protection, savings, relief)
- Urgency (rate increase is coming)
- Personal relevance (hits them in their bill)

### Part 4: Proof (20 seconds)

**Word Track:**
> "Sean paid $141 dollars with SDGE. I replaced that, and now he pays just $99 bucks."

[Specific numbers are powerful. Math is proof.]

**What's happening:**
- Concrete proof (not abstract claims)
- Specific customer result ($42/month savings)
- Social proof (neighbor you know)
- Credibility (exact numbers)

### Part 5: Ask for Permission (10 seconds)

**Word Track:**
> "Do mind if I ask you just 2 questions?"

[Not pushy. Light. Easy yes.]

**What's happening:**
- Asking permission (respectful)
- Only 2 questions (low commitment)
- Preparing for next phase

**Their response will usually be:**
- "Sure, yeah" → Proceed
- "I'm not interested" → Ask one question anyway
- "Not now" → Get info to follow up later
- Door closes → Move to next
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Complete Door Approach: Full Flow (60-90 seconds)">
Once you get permission, you have 2 questions (plus follow-ups). Here's the flow:

### Question 1: Discovery

**Word Track:**
> "What's been holding you back from going solar?"

[Let them answer. Really listen. This is your needs audit beginning.]

**Their likely responses:**
- "I haven't really thought about it"
- "Cost"
- "I'm not sure if it works"
- "I want to, but..."

**Your follow-up:**
> "I get it. What else?"

[Listen for all concerns. Don't argue.]

### Question 2: Clarification

**Word Track:**
> "[Listen to their concerns] And is there anything else?"

[Get everything on the table.]

[After they've listed concerns, move to rapport building and introduction:]
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Rapport Building">
**Word Track:**
> "How long have you lived in the home? Wow! 10 Years!? How have you gotten rid of all these solar guys?!"

[Laugh. Build connection. Show you're not just another pushy salesman.]
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Introduction and Timeline">
**Word Track:**
> "So listen, my name is Cody. I work with [company] doing solar installs in the neighborhood. I'm not here for a decision today. Based on our conversation up to this point, I'm confident that we can put you in a much better financial position than with SDGE."

[Setting expectations: this is an opening, not a close.]
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Reframe">
**Word Track:**
> "Jimmy, you've shared with me that you haven't gone solar yet because [concern A] and [concern B]. Did I get that right? Got it. Is there anything else?"

[Confirm understanding. Get full picture.]

[Wait for "That's it." Don't move forward until they confirm.]
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Mini Solution Presentation (Optional)">
If you have time and they're engaged, a 30-second value prop:

**Word Track:**
> "Jimmy, you've been paying SDGE for the 10 years that you've lived here, and will continue to pay them for as long as you live in the home. What you'll find is that solar is already in your budget. All we do is redirect the same money you pay SDGE, and simply put it toward solar.

> It'd be like if you went to the grocery store to buy a gallon of milk. You've got one gallon that's $3 dollars, and one that's $5 dollars. They taste exactly the same. Which one would you pick?

> Most people don't overpay for something, if they don't have to. That's exactly your current situation with SDGE right now. You are overpaying for power, when you don't have to."

[This is the value prop. Not a full presentation, just a taste.]
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Schedule Next Event: Booking the Appointment">
**Word Track:**
> "Jimmy, I'm not here for a decision today. Based on our conversation up to this point, I'm confident that we can put you in a much better financial position than with SDGE. By going solar, you'll know exactly how much you pay each month, and protect yourself from the 18% increase this year.

> So let's just get some numbers in front of you and see if this is a good fit. Does that sound fair?"

[This is your transition to booking.]

### Booking Questions (The Solidification Process)

**Question 1: Time**
> "Perfect. Let's get you on the books. Are you available next Thursday during the day? What works better for you - morning or afternoon?"

[Let them choose. Their choice = their ownership.]

**Question 2: Time specific**
> "Morning works great. What time is best - 9 o'clock, 10 o'clock, or 11 o'clock?"

[Again, their choice.]

**Question 3: Location confirmation**
> "OK, Thursday at 10 AM. And we're at [address], right? Did I get that right?"

[Confirm address.]

**Question 4: Decision makers**
> "Perfect. One more thing - will both of you be here? I want to make sure we're all on the same page."

[Non-negotiable: both decision makers must be present.]

**Question 5: Bill request**
> "Great. One thing that'll make our appointment way more efficient - can you pull your most recent SDGE bill? I'm going to review it beforehand so when we sit down, I can walk you through exactly how much you're spending and how solar changes that number.

> Do you have it handy, or will you need to go dig it out?"

[Critical: Get the bill before appointment.]

**Question 6: Introduction**
> "Perfect. So here's what happens: Tomorrow, our office will confirm this appointment and send you an email with all the details. Then I'm going to review your bill and prepare some numbers.

> Our team will call you - my manager actually - to confirm everything and answer any questions.

> Does that all make sense? Any questions for me?"

[Setting expectations. They know what's coming.]
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Hot Live Transfer">
If your manager is present (during blitz), you might hand off immediately:

**Word Track:**
> "Actually, my manager is right here. Let me introduce you. [Introduce] They're going to confirm all the details and answer any other questions you might have. I'm going to keep going down the street. Good luck!"

[Clean handoff. Professional. They feel taken care of.]
</Accordion>

YOU: "No worries. How long have you lived in the home?"

HOMEOWNER: "About 8 years."

YOU: "Eight years!? How have you gotten rid of all these solar guys? [Laugh]"

HOMEOWNER: [Laughs] "Yeah, I don't know, they never really stuck around long."

Based on our conversation, I'm confident we can put you in a much better financial position than with SDGE. Jimmy, you've told me you're concerned about reliability and cost. Did I get that right?"

So let's get some numbers in front of you and see if this works. Does that sound fair?"

HOMEOWNER: "Yeah, OK."

YOU: "Great. When would work - next Thursday?"

HOMEOWNER: "Yeah, Thursday could work."

YOU: "Morning or afternoon?"

HOMEOWNER: "Morning probably."

YOU: "Perfect - 10 o'clock work for you?"

HOMEOWNER: "Sure."

YOU: "OK, Thursday 10 AM at [address]?

HOMEOWNER: "Yeah."

YOU: "And your wife will be here too, right?"

YOU: "Perfect. I really appreciate it. See you Thursday. [Handshake]"
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Key Principles to Remember">
### Principle 1: It's Not About You
You're an opening, not the closer. You're the appetizer, not the meal.

### Principle 2: Permission is Always Respected
If they say no or not interested, say: "No worries. If you ever decide to explore it, here's my card."

### Principle 3: Both Decision Makers Matter
You can't set the appointment without both. If spouse isn't available, reschedule.

### Principle 4: The Bill is Essential
Don't skip this. Get the SDGE bill before the appointment.

### Principle 5: Tone is Everything
Friendly, confident, respectful. Not pushy, salesy, or desperate.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Metrics That Matter">
**Success looks like:**
- 10 doors knocked
- 7-8 conversations had
- 3-4 appointments set
- 100% of appointments have both decision makers + SDGE bill

When your door approach is working, you'll see these numbers consistently.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The 30-Day Mastery Challenge">
**Week 1:** Learn the script. Practice saying it naturally.

**Week 2:** Hit 10 doors/day. Practice the script at every door.

**Week 3:** Refine based on feedback. What's working? What needs adjustment?

**Week 4:** Consistency. Same script, same energy, same results.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Key Takeaway">
The door approach is your opening statement. Make it confident, make it relevant, make it respectful.

Get to the appointment with both decision makers, the SDGE bill, and realistic expectations.

Then let the closer do their job. That's the art of the door approach.
</Accordion>
