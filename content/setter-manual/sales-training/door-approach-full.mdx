# The Complete Door Approach: From Knock to Appointment

<Lead>Your goal at the door is simple but specific: set a quality appointment with both decision makers present, with the SDGE bill, with realistic expectations about what happens next. You're not closing the deal at the door. You're opening the conversation and moving them to the next step.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Complete Door Approach: Word Tracks (60 Seconds)">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Rapport (14 seconds)">

<PullQuote>"Hey, how are you? I'm with KIN Home. I take care of your neighbor Sean."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="What's Happening">Establishing friendliness, name-dropping a neighbor for social proof, introducing the company — all in 14 seconds.</KeyTerm>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Position (10 seconds)">

<PullQuote>"I took his SDGE budget and redirected it toward solar."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
"Redirect" is powerful language — not "replace," not "eliminate." It implies the money is already being spent.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Benefit (9 seconds)">

<PullQuote>"Protecting him from the 18% increase coming this year."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Urgency + Benefit">Rate increase creates urgency. Protection creates benefit. Both land in one sentence.</KeyTerm>
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Proof (20 seconds)">

<PullQuote>"Sean paid $141 dollars with SDGE. I replaced that, and now he pays just $99 bucks."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
Specific numbers are powerful. Math is proof. A concrete customer result beats an abstract claim every time.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={5} title="Ask for Permission (10 seconds)">

<PullQuote>"Do you mind if I ask you just 2 questions?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="The Responses">**"Sure, yeah"** → Proceed. **"I'm not interested"** → Ask one question anyway. **"Not now"** → Get info to follow up. **Door closes** → Move to next.</KeyTerm>
</Step>
</Steps>

<Accordion title="The Complete Door Approach: Full Flow (60-90 seconds)">

Once you get permission, you have 2 questions plus follow-ups:

**Question 1: Discovery**

<PullQuote>"What's been holding you back from going solar?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="important">
Let them answer. **Really listen.** This is your needs audit beginning.
</Callout>

Likely responses: "I haven't really thought about it" · "Cost" · "I'm not sure if it works" · "I want to, but..."

**Follow-up:**

<PullQuote>"I get it. What else?"</PullQuote>

**Question 2: Clarification**

<PullQuote>"And is there anything else?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Get Everything on the Table">Listen for all concerns. Don't argue. After they've listed everything, move to rapport building.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Rapport Building">

<PullQuote>"How long have you lived in the home? Wow! 10 Years!? How have you gotten rid of all these solar guys?!"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Laugh. Build connection. Show you're not just another pushy salesman.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Introduction and Timeline">

<PullQuote>"So listen, my name is [Name]. I work with KIN Home doing solar installs in the neighborhood. I'm not here for a decision today. Based on our conversation up to this point, I'm confident that we can put you in a much better financial position than with SDGE."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Setting Expectations">This is an opening, not a close. Make that clear so they drop their guard.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Reframe">

<PullQuote>"Jimmy, you've shared with me that you haven't gone solar yet because [concern A] and [concern B]. Did I get that right? Got it. Is there anything else?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="warning">
Wait for **"That's it."** Do not move forward until they confirm you've heard all their concerns.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Mini Solution Presentation (Optional)">

<Callout type="insight">
If you have time and they're engaged, deliver a 30-second value prop — not a full presentation, just a taste.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Jimmy, you've been paying SDGE for the 10 years that you've lived here, and will continue to pay them for as long as you live in the home. What you'll find is that solar is already in your budget. All we do is redirect the same money you pay SDGE, and simply put it toward solar."</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"It'd be like if you went to the grocery store to buy a gallon of milk. You've got one gallon that's $3 dollars, and one that's $5 dollars. They taste exactly the same. Which one would you pick?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"Most people don't overpay for something, if they don't have to. That's exactly your current situation with SDGE right now. You are overpaying for power, when you don't have to."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Schedule Next Event: Booking the Appointment">

<PullQuote>"Jimmy, I'm not here for a decision today. Based on our conversation up to this point, I'm confident that we can put you in a much better financial position than with SDGE. By going solar, you'll know exactly how much you pay each month, and protect yourself from the 18% increase this year. So let's just get some numbers in front of you and see if this is a good fit. Does that sound fair?"</PullQuote>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Time">

<PullQuote>"Perfect. Let's get you on the books. Are you available next Thursday during the day? What works better for you — morning or afternoon?"</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Time Specific">

<PullQuote>"Morning works great. What time is best — 9 o'clock, 10 o'clock, or 11 o'clock?"</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Location Confirmation">

<PullQuote>"OK, Thursday at 10 AM. And we're at [address], right? Did I get that right?"</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Decision Makers">

<PullQuote>"Perfect. One more thing — will both of you be here? I want to make sure we're all on the same page."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="important">
Non-negotiable: **both decision makers must be present.**
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={5} title="Bill Request">

<PullQuote>"Great. One thing that'll make our appointment way more efficient — can you pull your most recent SDGE bill? I'm going to review it beforehand so when we sit down, I can walk you through exactly how much you're spending and how solar changes that number."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={6} title="Introduction / Expectations">

<PullQuote>"Perfect. So here's what happens: Tomorrow, our office will confirm this appointment and send you an email with all the details. Then I'm going to review your bill and prepare some numbers. Our team will call you — my manager actually — to confirm everything and answer any questions. Does that all make sense?"</PullQuote>
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Hot Live Transfer">

<KeyTerm term="When to Use">If your manager is present during blitz, hand off immediately for the highest conversion.</KeyTerm>

<PullQuote>"Actually, my manager is right here. Let me introduce you. They're going to confirm all the details and answer any other questions you might have. I'm going to keep going down the street. Good luck!"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Clean handoff. Professional. They feel taken care of.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Complete Script (Actual Flow)">

<Callout type="insight" title="HEAR HOW IT SOUNDS">
Here's the full conversation, start to finish, so you can hear the natural rhythm.
</Callout>

**YOU:** *"Hey, how are you? I'm [Name] with KIN Home. I take care of your neighbor Sean."*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"Oh hey, yeah, I know Sean."*

**YOU:** *"I took his SDGE budget and redirected it toward solar, protecting him from the 18% increase coming this year. Sean paid $141 dollars with SDGE. I replaced that, and now he pays just $99 bucks. Do you mind if I ask you just 2 questions?"*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"Sure, yeah."*

**YOU:** *"What's been holding you back from going solar?"*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"Well, honestly, I'm not sure if it's reliable. And I'm worried about the cost."*

**YOU:** *"I get it. What else?"*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"I don't know, that's probably it."*

**YOU:** *"No worries. How long have you lived in the home?"*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"About 8 years."*

**YOU:** *"Eight years!? How have you gotten rid of all these solar guys?"* [Laugh]

**HOMEOWNER:** [Laughs] *"Yeah, I don't know, they never really stuck around long."*

**YOU:** *"Well, I'm [Name]. I work with KIN Home doing solar installs in the neighborhood. I'm not here for a decision today. Based on our conversation, I'm confident we can put you in a much better financial position than with SDGE. Jimmy, you've told me you're concerned about reliability and cost. Did I get that right?"*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"Yeah, that's it."*

**YOU:** *"Perfect. Here's the thing — you've been paying SDGE for 8 years, and you'll keep paying them. Solar is already in your budget. We redirect your SDGE money to solar instead. It's like buying milk — you wouldn't pay more for the same product, right? You're actually paying more right now. So let's get some numbers in front of you and see if this works. Does that sound fair?"*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"Yeah, OK."*

**YOU:** *"Great. When would work — next Thursday?"*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"Yeah, Thursday could work."*

**YOU:** *"Morning or afternoon?"*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"Morning probably."*

**YOU:** *"Perfect — 10 o'clock work for you?"*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"Sure."*

**YOU:** *"OK, Thursday 10 AM at [address]?"*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"Yeah."*

**YOU:** *"And your wife will be here too, right?"*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"Yeah, I'll make sure she's here."*

**YOU:** *"Perfect. One last thing — can you pull your SDGE bill so I can review it before we meet?"*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"Yeah, I think I have one inside."*

**YOU:** *"Awesome. So Thursday at 10, you and your wife, our team will send you an email with details, and I'll review your bill. You guys ready?"*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"Yeah, sounds good."*

**YOU:** *"Perfect. I really appreciate it. See you Thursday."* [Handshake]

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Key Principles to Remember">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="It's Not About You">
You're an opening, not the closer. You're the appetizer, not the meal.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Permission is Always Respected">
If they say no or not interested: *"No worries. If you ever decide to explore it, here's my card."*
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Both Decision Makers Matter">
You can't set the appointment without both. If spouse isn't available, reschedule.
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="The Bill is Essential">
Don't skip this. Get the SDGE bill before the appointment.
</Step>

<Step number={5} title="Tone is Everything">
Friendly, confident, respectful. Not pushy, salesy, or desperate.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>
