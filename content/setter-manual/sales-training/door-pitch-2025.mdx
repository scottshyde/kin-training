# Door Pitch 2025

<Lead>The 2025 pitch runs on the same five-part framework as 2026 — Rapport, Position, Benefit, Proof, AFP. The difference is in the proof point and the SDGE education sequence. Knowing both versions makes you more adaptable in the field.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Five-Part Structure">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Rapport">

<PullQuote>"Hey, how are you? I'm with KIN Home. I helped your neighbor Sean over on [street]."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Position">

<PullQuote>"We're qualifying homeowners for the new virtual utility plant."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Benefit">

<PullQuote>"For zero money down."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Proof">

<PullQuote>"Sean was paying [amount] to SDGE. I replaced that bill — now he pays [lower amount] for solar and battery backup."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Use **real numbers** from your actual pipeline — specificity is credibility.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={5} title="AFP">

<PullQuote>"This is completely different than what you've heard before. Do you mind if I ask you just two questions?"</PullQuote>
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Needs Audit Bridge">

<PullQuote>"Sean didn't do old solar for years because the price, the debt, and the savings just didn't make sense — what stopped you from looking at it?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="The Permission Slip">This borrows Sean's objection so they don't have to volunteer theirs. It's an invitation, not a cross-examination.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="SDGE Education">

<Callout type="important" title="THE THREE-PART STORY">
When the homeowner wants to understand why their bill is high:
</Callout>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="San Onofre">
Shut down in 2012. The region lost **2,200 megawatts** of locally generated power overnight.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Desert Import">
San Diego now imports electricity from the desert. Hundreds of miles of transmission infrastructure — someone pays for that.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Delivery Charges">
**~80%** of a typical SDGE bill is delivery. Conservation doesn't help — you pay delivery fees regardless.
</Step>
</Steps>

<PullQuote>"Most people think if they turn things off, they'll lower their bill. But 80% of what you're paying isn't for the energy — it's just to have the wire connected to your house."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Solve">

<PullQuote>"What we do is replace your SDGE bill. Instead of paying the utility every month, you're paying into your own system. Solar generates the energy, the battery stores it, and you stop paying SDGE for delivery."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
Bridge to the virtual utility plant concept: your home feeds excess energy back into the grid. You go from a **customer** of the utility to a **participant** in the grid.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The SDGE Hypothetical Close">

<PullQuote>"Hypothetically — if SDGE called you tonight and said, 'We'll lock in your current bill at $180 a month for the next 25 years, and at the end of 25 years we just stop charging you' — would you take that deal?"</PullQuote>

Almost everyone says **yes**.

<PullQuote>"That's essentially what solar does. Except instead of paying SDGE, you're paying into something you own."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Takeaway and Booking">

<PullQuote>"I want to be honest — not every home qualifies. Let me do a quick panel check while I'm here, grab your bill, and in about 90 seconds I'll know whether it even makes sense for you."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="The 90-Second Split">The "panel check" moves you to see the roof. The "90 seconds" language removes pressure — brief and contained.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Set Expectations">

<PullQuote>"Two things happen when my manager comes by: first, just two questions — same ones I asked. And second, if it makes sense, they'll design a system with exact rates, do a soft credit check, and walk you through paperwork if you want to move forward."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
Reps who set expectations clearly have significantly **lower no-show rates**. The homeowner knows what they're agreeing to.
</Callout>

</Accordion>
