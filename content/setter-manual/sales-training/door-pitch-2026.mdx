# Door Pitch 2026

<Lead>The door pitch is the first 60 seconds of every conversation. It's not a sales pitch — it's an engineered sequence designed to get one thing: an AFP. Everything after depends on nailing this.</Lead>

<Callout type="important" title="THE FIRST-WORDS PRINCIPLE">
The first 60 words carry more weight than the next 6,000.
</Callout>

<Accordion title="The Five-Part Structure">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Rapport (~12 words)">
Open human. Sound like a neighbor, not a rep.

<PullQuote>"Hey, how are you? I'm with KIN Home. I helped your neighbor Sean over on Maple."</PullQuote>

The neighbor name is your social proof before you've said anything about solar. If you don't have a name, use the cross street.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Position (~7 words)">
Name what you're doing here. Be specific.

<PullQuote>"We're helping homeowners qualify for the new virtual utility plant program."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Virtual Utility Plant">"Virtual utility plant" is intentional — it's accurate, it's different, and it creates curiosity without triggering the "solar guy" defense.</KeyTerm>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Benefit (~4 words)">
One crisp line. Don't overload.

<PullQuote>"For zero money down."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
That's it. Don't explain. Don't elaborate yet.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Proof (~20 words)">
Real numbers from a real neighbor. Specificity builds credibility.

<PullQuote>"Sean was paying $141 to SDGE. I replaced that bill — now he pays just $99 for solar and a battery backup."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
Use actual numbers from your pipeline whenever possible. Made-up numbers feel made-up. Real numbers land.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={5} title="AFP — Ask for Permission (~18 words)">
This is the close of your pitch. You're not asking for time, you're asking for permission.

<PullQuote>"This is completely different than the other thousand guys who've knocked on your door. Do you mind if I ask you just two questions?"</PullQuote>

The phrase "completely different than the other thousand guys" does two things: it acknowledges their experience and separates you from it in the same breath.
</Step>
</Steps>
</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Full 60-Word Pitch">

<Callout type="important" title="MEMORIZE THIS">
This is the complete sequence. Practice it until it sounds like a conversation, not a script.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Hey, how are you? I'm with KIN Home — I helped your neighbor Sean. We're qualifying homeowners for the new virtual utility plant for zero money down. Sean paid $141 with SDGE, I replaced that — he now pays just $99 for battery and solar. This is completely different than the other thousand guys. Do you mind if I just ask you two questions?"</PullQuote>
</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Needs Audit Bridge">
Once they say yes to two questions, you've opened the case. Now pivot to the Needs Audit:

<PullQuote>"Sean didn't do solar for years because the price, the debt, and the savings just didn't add up — what stopped you from looking at it?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
This opens their story without interrogating them. You're borrowing Sean's objection as a permission slip for them to voice theirs.
</Callout>
</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="SDGE Education (If They Engage)">
If they want to understand why bills are so high, here's the brief version:

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "2012", label: "San Onofre shut down" },
  { value: "2,200 MW", label: "of local power lost" },
  { value: "~80%", label: "of your SDGE bill is delivery" },
  { value: "$0", label: "saved by conserving on delivery charges" }
]} />

- San Onofre nuclear plant shut down in 2012 — we lost 2,200 megawatts of local power
- San Diego now imports power from the desert
- Delivery charges now make up ~80% of your SDGE bill
- You can't reduce delivery charges by conserving — you pay them regardless

<PullQuote>"Most people try to save energy to lower their bill. But 80% of your bill is for delivery — not usage. You can turn everything off and still owe SDGE."</PullQuote>
</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Solve">

<PullQuote>"What we do is replace your SDGE bill entirely. Instead of paying the utility, you pay into your own system. Same concept as owning vs renting."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Then move into the SDGE hypothetical close.
</Callout>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Takeaway & Booking">
If they're engaged but not committing:

<PullQuote>"I want to be upfront — not every home qualifies. Let me do a quick panel check while I'm here, grab your bill, and I'll know in 90 seconds if it even makes sense for you."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="The 90-Second Split">Roof check + bill pull + booking. Three actions compressed into one natural moment so the prospect doesn't feel the weight of a decision.</KeyTerm>
</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Common Responses">

**"Is this solar?"**

<PullQuote>"It's part of it — but it's actually a battery backup program that works with or without panels. Can I ask you two quick questions?"</PullQuote>

**"You're knocking on my door for this?"**

<PullQuote>"I know, weird, right? Honestly, door to door is the only way we keep costs low enough to make the numbers work. That's part of why Sean saves $42 a month. Two questions?"</PullQuote>

**"Not interested."**

<PullQuote>"Completely fair — most people feel that way until they see the bill comparison. Mind if I just show you one number real quick before I go?"</PullQuote>
</Accordion>
