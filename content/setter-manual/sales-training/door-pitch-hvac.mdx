# Door Pitch — HVAC

<Lead>HVAC is not a separate product line — it's an extension of the energy conversation. Most reps miss HVAC opportunities because they're scanning for "solar people." Instead, scan for energy problems. HVAC is often the loudest one.</Lead>

<Callout type="important" title="THE RULE">
Don't lead with HVAC. Lead with the energy problem. HVAC is the solve.
</Callout>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Scenarios That Invite the HVAC Conversation">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="New Build with Inadequate HVAC">
New construction homes frequently come with undersized, builder-grade HVAC systems that can't keep up with California summers. These homeowners are already frustrated.

<PullQuote>"A lot of the newer builds out here came with systems that just aren't sized for this climate. Are you finding your place gets warm in the afternoon?"</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="New Homeowner / Flipper">
Someone who just moved in may have inherited an aging system they don't fully understand yet.

<PullQuote>"How long have you been here? The reason I ask — a lot of homes in this area have older units, and new owners usually find out the hard way in July."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Home Without A/C (Family Expanding)">
If you spot signs of a young family and the home doesn't have visible A/C:

<PullQuote>"Do you have central air? The reason I ask is we're helping some families in the area get cooling set up before summer — for zero down if they qualify."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Older Home Getting Hotter">
San Diego heat is increasingly intense. Older homes with no insulation upgrades and old HVAC struggle.

<PullQuote>"These older homes weren't built for how hot it's gotten the last few years. Are you finding it tough to keep up in summer?"</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={5} title="Existing Solar Owner with True-Up Bills">
If someone has solar but still gets a large true-up, HVAC efficiency is often part of the problem.

<PullQuote>"A lot of solar customers are still getting hammered on their true-up because their HVAC is eating more than the panels can cover. Has that been your experience?"</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={6} title="Homeowner Mentions A/C in Needs Audit">
If HVAC comes up organically during a solar needs audit:

<PullQuote>"You mentioned your cooling bills — that's actually something we can address directly as part of what we're doing. Let me make sure I flag that for my manager when they come by."</PullQuote>
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Critical Rule">

<Callout type="warning" title="DO NOT SELL HVAC FIRST">
Do not sell HVAC until you've circled back through the solar/power problem. HVAC-only deals are possible, but they're a fallback — not a strategy.
</Callout>

The goal is always to solve the energy problem **holistically**. An efficient HVAC system paired with solar + battery is the full solution. Don't short-circuit that by chasing an HVAC-only close too early.

<KeyTerm term="The Loop">Identify the energy/cooling problem → Present solar + battery as the primary solve → Add HVAC as a bonus upgrade for qualified homes.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Two Routes">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "ROUTE 1", label: "Wants Solar + HVAC" },
  { value: "ROUTE 2", label: "Not Solar, But Has HVAC Pain" }
]} />

**Route 1: Solar + HVAC**

The homeowner is interested in solar and mentions cooling concerns. Frame it as a perk, not a second pitch:

<PullQuote>"As an added benefit for homes that qualify, we can upgrade your HVAC system at the same time — same financing, zero down. It's essentially a bonus for going solar."</PullQuote>

**Route 2: HVAC-Only**

If they shut down the solar conversation but express real frustration with their cooling:

<PullQuote>"Totally fair — solar isn't for everyone. But you mentioned your system struggles in the heat — mind if I ask you one question about that before I go?"</PullQuote>

Then move into the HVAC-only pitch below.

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="HVAC-Only Door Pitch">

<KeyTerm term="Same Structure">When solar is off the table but HVAC is a real need, use the same five-part framework.</KeyTerm>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Rapport">

<PullQuote>"Hey, how are you? I'm with KIN Home — we do energy upgrades out here in [area]."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Position">

<PullQuote>"We're helping qualifying homeowners upgrade their heating and cooling systems."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Benefit">

<PullQuote>"For zero money down."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Proof">

<PullQuote>"We just helped the [neighbor name] a few doors down — their old unit was costing them an extra $80-90 a month in electricity. New system cut that significantly."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={5} title="AFP">

<PullQuote>"This might not be for you, but do you mind if I ask you two quick questions about your current setup?"</PullQuote>
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Needs Audit for HVAC">

**Old unit:**

<PullQuote>"How old is the system? Have you had any service calls in the last year?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"Is it struggling to keep up on hot days?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"Do you have a sense of what you're spending monthly on cooling?"</PullQuote>

**New unit:**

<PullQuote>"Is it keeping up with the heat, or are there rooms that stay warm?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"What was the reason you went with that system?"</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Solve">

<PullQuote>"For homes that qualify, we can replace your system entirely — more efficient, right-sized for your square footage — for zero down. The energy savings usually offset the monthly cost. It's worth at least knowing the number."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Then book the appointment for the manager to run the full assessment.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="What 'Qualify' Means">

<KeyTerm term="Scarcity as a Selling Tool">Not every home qualifies for $0 down HVAC — and that's actually an advantage. The qualification creates scarcity and positions your company as selective.</KeyTerm>

<PullQuote>"Honestly, the hardest part is not all homes qualify. That's why I want to get someone out to actually take a look before I make any promises."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>
