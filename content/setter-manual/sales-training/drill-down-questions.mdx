# Drill Down Questions

Most reps ask a question, hear an answer, and move to their next scripted line. That's not a conversation — it's a checklist. Drill down questions are what turn a script into a real exchange.

> People don't act on information you give them. They act on conclusions they reach themselves. Drill down questions are how you help them get there.

<Accordion title="Simple Drill Downs">
These are your bread and butter. They're short, open, and they communicate genuine interest.

> *"Can you elaborate on that?"*

> *"Tell me more."*

> *"How do you mean?"*

> *"How so?"*

> *"What do you mean by that?"*

That's it. Five phrases that can keep a conversation alive for as long as the prospect has something to say. When in doubt, use one of these. Silence with a forward-leaning posture also works.

The magic of these phrases is what they signal: *I'm listening and I want to understand, not just respond.*
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Advanced Drill Downs">
These are purpose-built for specific moments in the needs audit. Use them when you need to help a prospect confront the real cost of their status quo.

### The Dream Scenario Question
Use this after they've described a frustration or concern.

> *"In a dream scenario, what would you want your energy situation to look like?"*

This moves them from the problem to the ideal — and once they articulate the ideal, your solution becomes the bridge between where they are and where they want to be.

### The $120 x 12 Math
Use this when the prospect is focused on a monthly savings number that seems small.

> *"$10 a month doesn't sound like much — but $10 times 12 months is $120 a year. Over 25 years that's $3,000. Does $3,000 feel different than $10?"*

Numbers that seem trivial monthly become significant when stretched across a system's lifetime. Let them do the math with you.

### The 5-Year / 10-Year Projection
Use this when they're anchored to today's bill.

> *"SDGE rates have gone up about 5-7% every year for the last decade. If your bill is $180 today, what do you think it's going to be in 5 years? Ten years?"*

This creates urgency without manufactured pressure — the math creates it.

### The Upfront Guy Flow
For the prospect who wants to pay cash but the ROI isn't obvious at their current bill:

1. *"What payback period were you hoping for?"*
2. *(They answer — say, 5 years)*
3. *"SDGE isn't going to offer you anything financial in return for staying with them, right? They don't give money back."*
4. *(They agree)*
5. *"So hypothetically — if instead of paying SDGE your monthly cost was the same as what you're paying now, but at the end of 25 years the payment just stopped — would that work?"*

You're not arguing about upfront cost. You're reframing monthly-to-solar as equal to monthly-to-SDGE, with ownership at the end.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Discipline of Drilling Down">
Drilling down requires one thing most reps struggle with: **silence.** After you ask a follow-up question, stop talking. Don't fill the space. Don't offer the answer. Let them think.

The prospect is processing. Give them room to process. The answer they land on themselves is worth ten times the answer you'd have handed them.

> *You cannot drill down while you're talking.*
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="When to Use Them">
- Any time a prospect gives a short or vague answer
- When you sense there's more behind an objection than what they said
- When someone gives you a surface-level "not interested"
- When their stated reason for hesitating doesn't match their energy

The goal is always the same: understand what's actually going on. Not what they said, but what they mean.
</Accordion>
