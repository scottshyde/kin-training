# Drill Down Questions

<Lead>Most reps ask a question, hear an answer, and move to their next scripted line. That's not a conversation — it's a checklist. Drill down questions are what turn a script into a real exchange.</Lead>

<PullQuote>People don't act on information you give them. They act on conclusions they reach themselves. Drill down questions are how you help them get there.</PullQuote>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Simple Drill Downs">

<Callout type="important" title="YOUR BREAD AND BUTTER">
Five phrases that can keep a conversation alive for as long as the prospect has something to say.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Can you elaborate on that?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"Tell me more."</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"How do you mean?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"How so?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"What do you mean by that?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="What These Signal">I'm listening and I want to understand — not just respond.</KeyTerm>

<Callout type="tip">
When in doubt, use one of these. Silence with a forward-leaning posture also works.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Advanced Drill Downs">

### The Dream Scenario Question

Use after they've described a frustration:

<PullQuote>"In a dream scenario, what would you want your energy situation to look like?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
This moves them from the problem to the ideal — and once they articulate the ideal, your solution becomes the bridge.
</Callout>

### The $120 x 12 Math

Use when monthly savings seem small:

<PullQuote>"$10 a month doesn't sound like much — but $10 times 12 months is $120 a year. Over 25 years that's $3,000. Does $3,000 feel different than $10?"</PullQuote>

### The 5-Year / 10-Year Projection

Use when they're anchored to today's bill:

<PullQuote>"SDGE rates have gone up about 5-7% every year for the last decade. If your bill is $180 today, what do you think it's going to be in 5 years? Ten years?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Why This Works">The math creates urgency — not manufactured pressure.</KeyTerm>

### The Upfront Guy Flow

For the cash buyer where ROI isn't obvious:

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Ask">
"What payback period were you hoping for?"
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Reframe">
"SDGE isn't going to offer you anything financial in return for staying with them, right? They don't give money back."
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Hypothetical">
"If instead of paying SDGE your monthly cost was the same, but at the end of 25 years the payment just stopped — would that work?"
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Discipline of Drilling Down">

<Callout type="important" title="THE HARDEST PART">
Drilling down requires one thing most reps struggle with: **silence.**
</Callout>

After you ask a follow-up question, **stop talking**. Don't fill the space. Don't offer the answer. Let them think.

<PullQuote>You cannot drill down while you're talking.</PullQuote>

The answer they land on **themselves** is worth ten times the answer you'd have handed them.

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="When to Use Them">

- Any time a prospect gives a **short or vague** answer
- When you sense there's **more behind** an objection
- When someone gives a surface-level "not interested"
- When their stated reason **doesn't match their energy**

<KeyTerm term="The Goal">Always the same: understand what's actually going on. Not what they said — what they mean.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>
