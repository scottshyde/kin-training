# Getting the Bill

<Lead>The utility bill is the foundation of every solar proposal. Without it, you're guessing. With it, you can show exact numbers — and exact numbers close appointments.</Lead>

<Callout type="important" title="THE SKILL">
Getting the bill at the door separates good reps from great ones.
</Callout>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Why the Bill Matters">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "kWh", label: "Annual usage → system size" },
  { value: "$/mo", label: "Current spend → comparison baseline" },
  { value: "NEM", label: "1.0, 2.0, or 3.0 → affects proposal" },
  { value: "RATE", label: "Some plans make solar way more valuable" }
]} />

<PullQuote>"The reason I want to see your bill is so I don't waste your time. If the numbers don't make sense for your situation, I'll tell you upfront. If they do, I want to show you exactly how it pencils out."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="SDGE — Step by Step">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Find Out How They Get Their Bill">

<PullQuote>"Do you get your bill online, or does it come in the mail?"</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="If Online — Pull It Up">

<PullQuote>"Mind if we pull it up real quick? It takes about 30 seconds."</PullQuote>

Navigate to: **myenergycenter.com** → Billing → View Detailed Bill PDF

<Callout type="tip">
The **detailed bill PDF** (not the summary screen) contains the annual usage data needed for an accurate proposal.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Capture Key Data">
- Annual kWh (usually last page or usage history chart)
- Current monthly average
- Rate plan / NEM status
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Edison — Step by Step">

<Callout type="insight">
Edison requires a quick verification code — slightly different flow.
</Callout>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Let Them Know">

<PullQuote>"Edison requires a quick verification code — they'll send it to your phone or email. Do you mind pulling that up while I'm here?"</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Navigate">
**sce.com/customer-service/self-service-forms/login-copy-bill**
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="While They Verify">
Check the meter box. The meter number on the physical meter matches the account — useful if there's confusion.
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Once In">
Look for the annual usage summary. The **12-month total kWh** is the key number.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Green Button Data (Detailed Analysis)">

For complex usage patterns or high bills:

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Log Into myenergycenter.com">
Navigate to Usage → Green Button Data.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Select Parameters">
12 months, 15-minute interval.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Download .csv File">
Send to your manager for precise system sizing and proposal accuracy.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Handling Resistance">

### "I don't want to log in at the door."

<PullQuote>"Totally fair. What if I just got your bill amount? I can run a rough estimate now and we'll pull the exact numbers when my manager comes by."</PullQuote>

### "I don't know my login."

<PullQuote>"No problem — the paper bill has your account number on it. Or we can use the estimate method. Do you have any idea roughly what you pay per month on average?"</PullQuote>

### "Why do you need my bill?"

<PullQuote>"Honestly, just to make sure the numbers work in your favor before I have my manager come out. I'm not going to waste your evening if it doesn't make sense — and I can't know that without seeing usage."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="If You Can't Get the Bill">

<Callout type="tip">
Don't abandon the appointment. Use the estimate path.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"What would you say your average monthly bill is — summer and winter?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Quick Estimate">Roughly 500-700 kWh per $100 of SDGE bill. Not precise, but enough to determine if the conversation is worth continuing. The manager will confirm at the sit.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>
