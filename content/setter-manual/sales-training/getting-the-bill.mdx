# Getting the Bill

The utility bill is the foundation of every solar proposal. Without it, you're guessing. With it, you can show exact numbers — and exact numbers close appointments.

Getting the bill at the door is a skill. Done well, it feels like a helpful service. Done poorly, it feels like an interrogation. The difference is framing.

---

## Why the Bill Matters

The bill tells you:
- Annual kWh usage (what size system they need)
- Current monthly spend (your baseline for the comparison)
- Whether they're NEM 1.0, 2.0, or 3.0 (affects the proposal)
- Whether they have any existing solar credits
- Rate plan (some rate plans make solar significantly more valuable)

> *"The reason I want to see your bill is so I don't waste your time. If the numbers don't make sense for your situation, I'll tell you upfront. If they do, I want to show you exactly how it pencils out."*

---

## SDGE — Step by Step

**1. Find out how they get their bill:**
> *"Do you get your bill online, or does it come in the mail?"*

**If online:**
> *"Mind if we pull it up real quick? It takes about 30 seconds."*

Navigate to: **myenergycenter.com** → Billing → View Detailed Bill PDF

The detailed bill PDF (not the summary screen) contains the annual usage data needed for an accurate proposal.

**If paper:**
Ask if they have last month's bill handy. If not, use the online path above.

**Key data points to capture:**
- Annual kWh (usually on the last page or the usage history chart)
- Current monthly average
- Rate plan (NEM status if solar present)

---

## Edison — Step by Step

Edison is a slightly different flow because their login process involves phone or email verification.

**1. Let them know:**
> *"Edison requires a quick verification code — they'll send it to your phone or email. Do you mind pulling that up while I'm here?"*

**2. Navigate to:** **sce.com/customer-service/self-service-forms/login-copy-bill**

**3. While they're verifying**, check the meter box. The meter number on the physical meter matches the account — useful if there's any confusion.

**4. Once in:** Look for the annual usage summary. The 12-month total kWh is the key number.

---

## Green Button Data (For Detailed Analysis)

For prospects with complex usage patterns or high bills, Green Button Data gives the most accurate picture:

1. Log into **myenergycenter.com**
2. Go to: **Usage → Green Button Data**
3. Select: **12 months, 15-minute interval**
4. Download the **.csv** file
5. Send to: **dwest@yourenergybest.com**

The manager will use this for precise system sizing and proposal accuracy.

## Handling Resistance

**"I don't want to log in at the door."**
> *"Totally fair. What if I just got your bill amount? I can run a rough estimate now and we'll pull the exact numbers when my manager comes by."*

**"I don't know my login."**
> *"No problem — the paper bill has your account number on it. Or we can use the usage estimate method. Do you have any idea roughly what you pay per month on average?"*

**"Why do you need my bill?"**
> *"Honestly, just to make sure the numbers work in your favor before I have my manager come out. I'm not going to waste your evening if it doesn't make sense — and I can't know that without seeing usage."*

---

## If You Can't Get the Bill

Don't abandon the appointment. Use the estimate path:

> *"What would you say your average monthly bill is — summer and winter?"*

Then estimate annual kWh at roughly 500-700 kWh per $100 of bill (SDGE). It's not precise, but it's enough to determine whether the conversation is worth continuing, and the manager will confirm at the sit.
