<Accordion title="Case Open — Turn Suspects Into Prospects">

<Callout type="insight" title="FIVE PLAYS IN SEQUENCE">
The Case Open is your entire door approach. Each play has a specific job — think of them like plays in a playbook.
</Callout>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Rapport (Who I Am)">

Your first job is to be a human being, not a salesperson. You have about five seconds before they decide whether to listen or shut the door.

<PullQuote>"Hey! How's it going? My name is [First Name] — I'm out here in the neighborhood today working with a few of your neighbors on [Street Name]."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Use their name if visible on mailbox or doorbell. Smile. Relax your shoulders. **You belong here.** Reference the neighborhood for social proof. Do not launch into solar.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Positioning (What I Do)">

Now they know who you are. Tell them what you do — framed around the homeowner, not around you.

<PullQuote>"I work with KIN Home — we're a local energy company that helps homeowners in [City/Neighborhood] lock in a lower electricity rate so they stop overpaying SDG&E every month."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Lead with the Outcome">Say "lower electricity rate" — not "solar panels." Mention locality for trust. Name the utility to make it real.</KeyTerm>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Benefit (Why They Should Care — The 5 Ps)">

Every homeowner cares about at least one of the 5 Ps:

| P | What It Means | Door Example |
|---|--------------|-------------|
| **Profit** | Save or make money | "Most homeowners on this street are saving $80-150/month" |
| **Prestige** | Status, being smart | "Your neighbors the Johnsons just got set up" |
| **Pleasure** | Feels good, easy | "The whole process takes about 45 minutes and we handle everything" |
| **Preservation** | Protect what you have | "Rates went up 8% this year and they're projected to keep climbing" |
| **Pain Relief** | Fix a problem | "Are you tired of those summer bills hitting $400+?" |

<PullQuote>"The reason I stopped by — SDG&E raised rates again this year, and most of your neighbors are paying way more than they need to. We've been helping homeowners on [Street] lock in a rate that's 20-30% lower than what they're paying now, and it doesn't cost anything to switch."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Proof (Evidence)">

People believe what they can see. Proof backs up your benefit claim with something tangible.

<PullQuote>"Actually, let me show you real quick — here's what the Rodriguez family was paying before and after. Same house, same usage, $140 less per month."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
**Types of proof:** Point to a neighbor's roof. Show a savings screenshot on your phone. Name-drop a recent install with real numbers.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step number={5} title="Schedule Next Event (SNE)">

You are not closing a sale — you are booking a time for the closer to sit down with both decision makers.

<PullQuote>"What I'd love to do is have my energy specialist stop by and show you guys exactly what your savings would look like — it takes about 30-45 minutes. Is your spouse usually home in the evenings or on weekends? What works better, tomorrow around 5 or Saturday morning?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="warning">
Always give **two time options**. Confirm both decision makers. Ask for the utility bill. Lock the time — **"tomorrow at 5:30"** not "sometime this week."
</Callout>
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Needs Audit Basics for Setters">

<KeyTerm term="Your Job">Get enough info that the closer walks into a warm, qualified appointment — not a cold pitch. You start the process at the door; the closer finishes it during the sit.</KeyTerm>

**The Reframe Play:**

<PullQuote>"So like I mentioned, we've been helping homeowners on [Street] cut their electricity costs. Before I schedule a time for my specialist to come out, let me ask you a couple quick questions to make sure this actually makes sense for your home."</PullQuote>

**Opportunity Queries (Status Quo & Vision):**

<PullQuote>"How much is your electric bill running in the summer?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"What would you do with an extra $100-150 a month?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"How long have you been in the home?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"Do you and your spouse make decisions on things like this together?"</PullQuote>

**Problem Queries (Frustrations & Pain):**

<PullQuote>"What frustrates you most about your electricity bill?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"Have you noticed rates going up the last couple years?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"Have you looked into solar before? What held you back?"</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Drill Down">

<Callout type="important" title="THE GOLD IS IN THE 2ND AND 3RD ANSWER">
When they give you an answer, do not move on immediately. Drill down. The drill-down is not interrogation — it's invitation.
</Callout>

**YOU:** *"What frustrates you most about your electric bill?"*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"It's just so expensive in the summer."*

**YOU:** *"Yeah, I hear that a lot. How expensive are we talking?"*

**HOMEOWNER:** *"Last August it was like $380."*

**YOU:** *"Wow, $380. And that's just going to keep going up with these rate hikes. What would it mean for your family if you could cut that in half?"*

<KeyTerm term="What Just Happened">A vague complaint became a specific, emotional pain point with a dollar amount the closer can use in the proposal.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Common Mistakes to Avoid">

<Callout type="warning" title="AVOID THESE">
Every one of these kills appointments before they start.
</Callout>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Talking Too Much About Solar Technology">
They don't care about panels and inverters at the door. They care about **their bill**.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Skipping the Proof Play">
Without proof, your benefit claim is just a sales pitch.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Not Drilling Down on Answers">
A surface-level "yeah my bill is high" is not enough. Get the number. Get the emotion.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Forgetting Both Decision Makers">
A one-legged sit is a wasted closer appointment.
</Step>
<Step number={5} title="Being Vague on SNE">
"I'll have someone reach out" is weak. **"Tomorrow at 5:30"** is strong.
</Step>
</Steps>
