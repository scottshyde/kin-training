# Griffin Hill: The Integrity Sales System for Setters

Griffin Hill is a six-step sales system built on integrity. As a setter, your job lives in the first two steps: **Case Open** and the beginning of the **Needs Audit**. Master these and you will consistently turn cold doors into qualified appointments.

<Accordion title="The 6-Step Process Overview">
Griffin Hill organizes the entire sales cycle into six routines. Each one builds on the last. Skip a step or execute it poorly and everything downstream suffers.

| # | Routine | Abbreviation | Who Owns It |
|---|---------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | Case Open | CO | **Setter** |
| 2 | Needs Audit | NA | **Setter starts, Closer finishes** |
| 3 | Solution Presentation | SP | Closer |
| 4 | Adapted Solution | AS | Closer |
| 5 | Closing Interactions | CI | Closer |
| 6 | Fulfillment & Follow Up | FF | Closer / Ops |

**Your focus:** Routines 1 and 2. Everything you do at the door determines whether the closer has a real opportunity or a dead lead. A great Case Open and a strong start to the Needs Audit is the difference between a sit that closes and one that cancels.

Every routine ends the same way: **Schedule Next Event (SNE)**. For you, that means booking the appointment with both decision makers present and the utility bill available.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Case Open — Turn Suspects Into Prospects">
The Case Open is your entire door approach. It has five plays that you execute in sequence. Think of them like plays in a playbook — each one has a specific job.

### Play 1: Rapport (Who I Am)

Your first job is to be a human being, not a salesperson. Rapport is about connection. You have about five seconds before they decide whether to listen or shut the door.

**At the door:**
> "Hey! How's it going? My name is [First Name] — I'm out here in the neighborhood today working with a few of your neighbors on [Street Name]."

Key principles:
- Use their name if you can see it on the mailbox or doorbell camera
- Smile. Relax your shoulders. You belong here
- Reference the neighborhood — it creates social proof and localizes you
- Do not launch into solar. You are a neighbor helping neighbors right now

### Play 2: Positioning (What I Do)

Now they know who you are. Tell them what you do — but frame it around the homeowner, not around you.

**At the door:**
> "I work with KIN Home — we're a local energy company that helps homeowners in [City/Neighborhood] lock in a lower electricity rate so they stop overpaying SDG&E every month."

Key principles:
- Lead with the outcome, not the product. "Lower electricity rate" not "solar panels"
- Mention locality — "local energy company" builds trust
- Name the utility company. It makes it real and specific

### Play 3: Benefit (Why They Should Care — The 5 Ps)

This is where you hook them. Every homeowner cares about at least one of the 5 Ps:

| P | What It Means | Door Example |
|---|--------------|-------------|
| **Profit** | Save or make money | "Most homeowners on this street are saving $80-150/month" |
| **Prestige** | Status, being smart | "Your neighbors the Johnsons just got set up — they said you guys might be interested too" |
| **Pleasure** | Feels good, easy | "The whole process takes about 45 minutes and we handle everything" |
| **Preservation** | Protect what you have | "Rates went up 8% this year and they're projected to keep climbing — this locks you in" |
| **Pain Relief** | Fix a problem | "Are you tired of those summer bills hitting $400+?" |

Pick the P that fits the conversation. If you see a high electric bill area, lead with Pain Relief or Profit. If you just signed their neighbor, lead with Prestige.

**At the door:**
> "The reason I stopped by — SDG&E raised rates again this year, and most of your neighbors are paying way more than they need to. We've been helping homeowners on [Street] lock in a rate that's 20-30% lower than what they're paying now, and it doesn't cost anything to switch."

### Play 4: Proof (Evidence)

People believe what they can see. Proof backs up your benefit claim with something tangible.

**Types of proof at the door:**
- Point to a neighbor's roof: "See the Garcias' house right there? We just finished their install last month"
- Show your phone: a savings screenshot, a Google review, a before/after bill
- Name-drop: "Yeah, the Martins on the corner — they were skeptical too but they're saving $120/month now"

> "Actually, let me show you real quick — here's what the Rodriguez family was paying before and after. Same house, same usage, $140 less per month."

### Play 5: Schedule Next Event (SNE)

This is the close of your Case Open. You are not closing a sale — you are booking a time for the closer to sit down with both decision makers.

**At the door:**
> "What I'd love to do is have my energy specialist stop by and show you guys exactly what your savings would look like — it takes about 30-45 minutes. Is your spouse usually home in the evenings or on weekends? What works better, tomorrow around 5 or Saturday morning?"

Key principles:
- Always give two time options (either/or close)
- Confirm both decision makers will be present
- Ask for the utility bill: "Can you grab a recent SDG&E bill? That's how we calculate your exact savings"
- Lock the time. Be specific — "tomorrow at 5:30" not "sometime this week"
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Needs Audit Basics for Setters">
The Needs Audit is about gathering information so the closer can build a compelling proposal. You start this process at the door — the closer finishes it during the sit.

Your job is to get enough info that the closer walks into a warm, qualified appointment, not a cold pitch.

### The Reframe Play

Before you start asking questions, briefly restate what you already established in the Case Open. This transitions smoothly into discovery.

> "So like I mentioned, we've been helping homeowners on [Street] cut their electricity costs. Before I schedule a time for my specialist to come out, let me ask you a couple quick questions to make sure this actually makes sense for your home."

### Query Plays with Drill Down

There are two types of queries you will use at the door. You do not need to run the full Needs Audit — that is the closer's job. But you need to ask enough to qualify and build urgency.

**Opportunity Queries (Status Quo & Vision):**

These help you understand where they are now and where they want to be.

- "How much is your electric bill running in the summer?" *(Status quo)*
- "What would you do with an extra $100-150 a month?" *(Vision)*
- "How long have you been in the home?" *(Qualification)*
- "Do you and your spouse make decisions on things like this together?" *(Decision process)*

**Problem Queries (Frustrations & Pain):**

These uncover the emotional driver that makes them want to act now.

- "What frustrates you most about your electricity bill?" *(Frustration)*
- "Have you noticed rates going up the last couple years?" *(Industry challenge)*
- "Have you looked into solar before? What held you back?" *(Barrier)*

### The Drill Down

When they give you an answer, do not move on immediately. Drill down. The gold is in the second and third answer, not the first.

**Example:**
> **You:** "What frustrates you most about your electric bill?"
> **Homeowner:** "It's just so expensive in the summer."
> **You:** "Yeah, I hear that a lot. How expensive are we talking?"
> **Homeowner:** "Last August it was like $380."
> **You:** "Wow, $380. And that's just going to keep going up with these rate hikes. What would it mean for your family if you could cut that in half?"

That drill down just turned a vague complaint into a specific, emotional pain point with a dollar amount the closer can use in the proposal.

### What to Hand Off to the Closer

After your door conversation, make sure the closer knows:
- Homeowner name(s) and who the decision makers are
- Approximate monthly bill / summer bill
- Key pain point or motivation (why they said yes)
- Any objections or concerns they raised
- Whether they have been through a solar pitch before
- Appointment time and whether both decision makers confirmed
- Utility bill status (did they grab it, or does closer need to request it)

<Accordion title="Applying Griffin Hill at the KIN Home Door">
Here is a complete door sequence using all five Case Open plays plus setter-level Needs Audit queries.

### Full Door Flow Example

**Rapport:**
> "Hey! How are you guys doing today? I'm Marcus with KIN Home — I've been out here on Elm Street all afternoon."

**Positioning:**
> "We're a local energy company that helps homeowners in Chula Vista lock in a lower electricity rate."

**Benefit (Pain Relief + Profit):**
> "With SDG&E raising rates again, most homeowners on this block are overpaying by $100-200 a month. We help fix that."

**Proof:**
> "Actually, your neighbor two doors down — the Nguyens — they just switched over last month. They were paying $320 in the summer and now they're locked in at about $180."

**Needs Audit Queries:**
> "Real quick — how much is your bill running in the summer?"
> *(They say $280)*
> "And that's been going up, right? How long have you been in the home?"
> *(6 years)*
> "Okay, so you've probably seen it climb every single year. What would you guys do with an extra $100-150 a month?"

**SNE:**
> "Here's what I want to do — I'm going to have my energy specialist come by and run the exact numbers for your house. It takes about 30 minutes and there's zero obligation. Does your wife usually get home by 5:30 or is 6:00 better? Perfect — and can you have a recent SDG&E bill handy? That's how we calculate your exact savings."

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Talking too much about solar technology.** They do not care about panels and inverters at the door. They care about their bill
- **Skipping the Proof play.** Without proof, your benefit claim is just a sales pitch
- **Not drilling down on answers.** A surface-level "yeah my bill is high" is not enough. Get the number, get the emotion
- **Forgetting to confirm both decision makers.** A one-legged sit is a wasted closer appointment
- **Being vague on SNE.** "I'll have someone reach out" is weak. "Tomorrow at 5:30" is strong
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Setter Mindset">
Griffin Hill is built on integrity. That means:

- You are genuinely helping homeowners save money. Act like it
- You are not tricking anyone into an appointment. You are qualifying them for an opportunity
- If solar does not make sense for their home, say so. That honesty will earn you referrals
- Every door is practice. The six-step system rewards consistency and repetition over talent
- Your metrics matter: doors knocked, conversations had, appointments booked, sits that held. Track them daily

The system works when you work the system. Master Case Open. Start the Needs Audit. Schedule Next Event. Do it with integrity. Repeat.
</Accordion>
