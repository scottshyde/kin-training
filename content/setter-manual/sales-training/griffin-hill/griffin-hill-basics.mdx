# Griffin Hill: The Integrity Sales System

## What is Griffin Hill?

Griffin Hill is a proven sales system focused on **Integrity Sales** - building genuine relationships, discovering real needs, and offering solutions that actually help.

It's used by thousands of successful closers across industries (not just solar). It works because it's based on human psychology, not manipulation.

At KIN, we use the Griffin Hill framework. Every training you receive is built on these principles.

---

## The Four Phases of Griffin Hill

The complete sales cycle has four distinct phases:

### Phase 1: Case Open

**What happens:**
- You open the conversation
- Build initial rapport
- Introduce yourself and your company
- Get permission to ask questions
- Begin needs discovery

**Duration:** 2-5 minutes

**Tools:** Door approach script, rapport building

**Success metric:** Appointment scheduled with both decision makers

**For Setters:** This is your job. You execute the door approach to set appointments.

---

### Phase 2: Needs Audit

**What happens:**
- You ask comprehensive questions
- Discover their real pain points and goals
- Uncover objections before they surface
- Build relationship through conversation
- Qualify for ability to move forward

**Duration:** 15-30 minutes

**Tools:** Needs audit questions, active listening

**Success metric:** Customer identifies their own need for solar

**For Closers:** This is your foundation. Everything else flows from a great needs audit.

---

### Phase 3: Solution Presentation

**What happens:**
- You present a customized solution
- Address their specific concerns
- Show how solar solves their stated problems
- Build compelling case for moving forward
- Overcome remaining objections

**Duration:** 20-40 minutes

**Tools:** Proposal, financial comparison, testimonials, objection handling

**Success metric:** Customer says yes to next step

**For Closers:** This is where the magic happens. Your needs audit data makes this powerful.

---

### Phase 4: Close

**What happens:**
- You ask for the commitment
- Handle final objections
- Get signature on contract
- Confirm next steps
- Build excitement for installation

**Duration:** 10-20 minutes

**Tools:** Contract, signing scripts, confirmation

**Success metric:** Signed agreement

**For Closers:** This is the finish line. Make it smooth and celebratory.

---

## The Framework: Case Open → Needs Audit → Solution → Close

```
     Case Open
         ↓
    Needs Audit
         ↓
   Solution Presentation
         ↓
      The Close
         ↓
    Signed Customer
```

Each phase builds on the previous one. Skip a phase or do it poorly, and the next phase gets harder.

---

## Why Griffin Hill Works

### Reason 1: It's Conversation-Based
Instead of presenting features, you're asking questions and listening. People like being listened to.

### Reason 2: It Builds Genuine Relationship
By asking about their life, concerns, and goals, you build real connection - not just transactional.

### Reason 3: It Uncovers Real Objections Early
Instead of objections surfacing at the end (when it's too late), they surface during needs audit (when you can address them).

### Reason 4: It Positions You as Problem-Solver
You're not a salesman trying to close a deal. You're a professional trying to help them solve a real problem.

### Reason 5: It Actually Works
High-integrity sales systems have higher close rates, better customer satisfaction, and more referrals than manipulative systems.

## The Core Principle: Integrity

Integrity in sales means:
- You genuinely care about the customer's best interest
- You don't manipulate or pressure
- You're honest about limitations
- You recommend what's actually best for them
- You follow through on promises
- You're transparent about costs and timelines

When you operate with integrity:
- Customers trust you
- You feel good about the sale
- Customers refer you
- Long-term business grows
- You sleep well at night

## The Two Core Skills

Everything in Griffin Hill builds on two core skills:

### Skill 1: Asking Great Questions

The person who asks the questions controls the conversation. Great questions:
- Open the customer's mind
- Make them think deeply
- Help them discover their own answers
- Build rapport through genuine curiosity
- Uncover hidden concerns

Master this and you master sales.

### Skill 2: Active Listening

After you ask the question, you must actually listen. Not:
- Think about your next question
- Plan your response
- Judge what they're saying
- Wait for your turn to talk

But:
- Hear what they're saying
- Understand the emotion behind it
- Clarify if you don't understand
- Reflect back what you heard
- Let silence exist while they think

Master this and you master relationships.
