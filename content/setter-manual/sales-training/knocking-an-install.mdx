# Knocking an Install

<Lead>One completed installation in a neighborhood is worth more than cold territory. A happy customer is a walking testimonial, a social proof anchor, and a referral map waiting to be activated. This technique is advanced — don't attempt it until you've mastered the Needs Audit and the Appointment Solidification sequence.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Step 1: Start with the Customer">

<Callout type="important" title="RELATIONSHIP FIRST">
Before you knock any neighbors, knock the install customer. The goal is to harvest social capital, not just referral names.
</Callout>

Open with a genuine check-in:

<PullQuote>"Hey [Name] — Dave, from KIN. Just wanted to swing by and make sure everything's been smooth since the install. How's the system been treating you?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Not a Pitch Moment">Let them talk. Let them complain if they need to. This is relationship maintenance — not selling.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Step 2: Gather Testimonial Material">

Once they're in a positive place, ask:

<PullQuote>"What are two or three of your favorite parts about the experience so far?"</PullQuote>

Listen closely. Whatever they say becomes your social proof at the neighbor's door:

<PullQuote>"[Customer name] down the street just had theirs installed — he told me the thing he loves most is [what they said]."</PullQuote>

Then push for the peak:

<PullQuote>"And what do you love most about the system overall?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="The Headline">The "most" question gets you the headline. Use it.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Step 3: Harvest Referrals">

<PullQuote>"Since you've had it installed, who in the neighborhood has asked you about the project?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Wait. Let them think. **Don't rush.** Most installed customers have had at least one neighbor comment or ask.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Can you point them out on this map?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Warm Referrals">These are people who already have curiosity and social connection — not cold leads. Treat them accordingly.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Step 4: Get Permission">

<PullQuote>"Do you mind if I knock on their door and let them know you had a great experience? I won't say anything you wouldn't say yourself — I'll just tell them you're happy with it and see if they're curious."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
The explicit permission does two things: it **protects the relationship** with your customer, and it gives you a social proof opener that almost always results in the door opening wide.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Neighbor Script">

<PullQuote>"Hey — I'm Dave with KIN Home. I was just next door with [Customer Name] — they just had a system installed and I was checking in on them. [Customer] mentioned you'd asked about it."</PullQuote>

*(Pause — let them respond.)*

<PullQuote>"I figured I'd swing by real quick while I was in the neighborhood. They said they couldn't be happier with how it went. Do you mind if I ask you two questions?"</PullQuote>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Completely Honest">
Everything you say is verifiable.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="The Neighbor's Name IS the Case Open">
Their name does all the heavy lifting.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Curiosity Is Pre-Built">
The social warmth and interest are already there.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="You're Not Cold — You're Connected">
This changes the entire dynamic before you say a word.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="What Not to Do">

<Callout type="warning" title="DON'T KNOCK BEFORE YOU'VE HARVESTED">
Get the referral names and permission first. Showing up at a neighbor's door saying "your neighbor said to come by" without that permission is a **violation of trust**.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning" title="DON'T ASK FOR REFERRALS BEFORE CHECKING IN">
If you show up and the customer isn't happy, this whole sequence falls apart — and now you've damaged a relationship. Check first.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning" title="DON'T OVER-ASK">
Getting 2-3 **strong** referral names is better than 10 weak ones. Quality over quantity.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />
