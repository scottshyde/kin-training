# Lead Calling

<Lead>Lead calling is one of the highest-leverage activities a rep can do between door shifts. Warm leads convert at a significantly higher rate than cold doors. The call is simple: open friendly, establish context, offer value, ask two questions.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Warm Lead Script">

<PullQuote>"Hey, this is Dave — is this Jimmy? It's been a minute, how have you been?"</PullQuote>

*(Wait for response. Be genuinely warm here.)*

<PullQuote>"We're helping homeowners claim federal solar credits before they phase out this year. We've also got some new incentives going for zero-down battery and solar combinations that weren't available when we last spoke. Do you mind if I ask you two quick questions?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="The Urgency">"Before they phase out" creates real urgency — federal incentives do change. "Zero-down battery" is often new information for people who looked at solar years ago.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Cold Lead Script">

<PullQuote>"Hey, this is Dave — is this Jimmy? Nice reconnecting with you."</PullQuote>

*(Proceed with the same body as the warm script.)*

<Callout type="tip">
The only difference is the opener. Warm leads get **"it's been a minute"** — signaling existing relationship. Cold leads get **"nice reconnecting"** — technically accurate and warmer than "I don't think we've spoken."
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Two Questions">

When they agree, the questions are:

<PullQuote>"Are you a homeowner?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"Do you know roughly what you're paying SDGE per month?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Dispositional Qualifying">These qualify the lead and give you a foundation for the pitch. Homeowner with a real bill = conversation. If not, politely end and move on.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Handling 'I Already Have Solar'">

<PullQuote>"Perfect — a lot of people with solar are still getting bigger true-up bills than they expected because the rules changed under NEM 3.0. Has that been your experience?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
If yes, pivot to **battery storage**. A battery paired with an existing system can dramatically improve economics under the new rate structure.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Handling 'Not Interested'">

<PullQuote>"Totally fair. Can I just ask — was it more the upfront cost side, or just not the right time when you looked at it?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Not Overcoming — Understanding">This question isn't to overcome the objection — it's to understand it. Sometimes the answer opens a door. Sometimes it doesn't. Either way, you end the call with real information.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Voicemail">

<Callout type="warning" title="KEEP IT UNDER 20 SECONDS">
Brief voicemails get callbacks. Long ones get deleted.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Hey [name], this is Dave from KIN Home — giving you a quick call because we've got some new solar and battery options that look pretty different from what was available before. Worth a 2-minute conversation if you're curious. Call or text me back at [number]."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="The Follow-Up">Text them immediately after leaving the voicemail. The text is often what gets the response.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />
