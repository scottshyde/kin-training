# Lead Calling

Lead calling is one of the highest-leverage activities a rep can do between door shifts. Warm leads convert at a significantly higher rate than cold doors. The call is simple: open friendly, establish context, offer value, ask two questions.

<Accordion title="Warm Lead Script">
> *"Hey, this is Dave — is this Jimmy? It's been a minute, how have you been?"*

*(Wait for response. Be genuinely warm here.)*

> *"We're helping homeowners claim federal solar credits before they phase out this year. We've also got some new incentives going for zero-down battery and solar combinations that weren't available when we last spoke. Do you mind if I ask you two quick questions?"*

The phrase "before they phase out" creates real urgency — federal incentives do change. The "zero-down battery" framing is often new information for people who looked at solar years ago.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Cold Lead Script">
> *"Hey, this is Dave — is this Jimmy? Nice reconnecting with you."*

*(Proceed with the same body as the warm script.)*

The difference between warm and cold is the opener. Warm leads get "it's been a minute" — which signals existing relationship. Cold leads get "nice reconnecting" — which is technically accurate and creates a warmer frame than "I don't think we've spoken."
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Two Questions">
When they agree to two questions, the questions are:

1. *"Are you a homeowner?"*
2. *"Do you know roughly what you're paying SDGE per month?"*

These are dispositional — they qualify the lead and give you a foundation for the pitch. If they're a homeowner with a real bill, you have a conversation. If not, you politely end the call and move on.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Handling 'I Already Have Solar'">
> *"Perfect — a lot of people with solar are still getting bigger true-up bills than they expected because the rules changed under NEM 3.0. Has that been your experience?"*

If yes, pivot to battery storage. A battery paired with an existing system can dramatically improve economics under the new rate structure.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Handling 'Not Interested'">
> *"Totally fair. Can I just ask — was it more the upfront cost side, or just not the right time when you looked at it?"*

This question isn't to overcome the objection — it's to understand it. Sometimes the answer opens a door. Sometimes it doesn't. Either way, you end the call with real information.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Voicemail">
If they don't answer, leave a brief voicemail. Keep it under 20 seconds:

> *"Hey [name], this is Dave from KIN Home — giving you a quick call because we've got some new solar and battery options that look pretty different from what was available before. Worth a 2-minute conversation if you're curious. Call or text me back at [number]."*

Text them immediately after leaving the voicemail. The text is often what gets the response.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Call Volume and Timing">
Best times to call:
- **Tuesday–Thursday, 5–7pm** — people are home from work and not yet fully settled
- **Saturday, 10am–12pm** — high answer rate for homeowners

Call every lead at least three times across different days and times before marking it cold. Most people don't answer unknown numbers on the first attempt.
</Accordion>
