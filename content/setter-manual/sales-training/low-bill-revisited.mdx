# Low Bill — The Full Flow

<Lead>A low bill is one of the most common conversation stoppers for new reps. The prospect says their bill is $60 a month and the rep mentally writes off the lead. That's a mistake. A low bill means fewer panels — not no deal.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Opening Question">

<PullQuote>"Are you concerned you'd be paying more with solar than you are now?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Name It First">This names the objection before they do. It shows you've had this conversation before, you're not surprised, and you have something to say about it.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Why Low Bill Does Not Equal No Deal">

<Callout type="insight">
A lower bill means a **smaller system** — 8-10 panels instead of 25-30. That system is less expensive to finance and produces enough to cover their usage.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"The beauty of a low bill is that you actually need fewer panels. A lot of people in your situation end up with a smaller system that costs less per month than what you're paying SDGE right now."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Conservative Energy Use Question">

<PullQuote>"Do you conserve energy pretty aggressively between 4pm and 9pm?"</PullQuote>

Most low-bill households do — avoiding laundry, dishwashers, and high-draw appliances during peak hours.

<PullQuote>"If it didn't cost you more — if your energy cost was the same or less — what would you use more freely?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Latent Demand">This opens the conversation about hidden demand. They've been holding back. Solar frees them to use energy normally — and even expand (EV, HVAC upgrades) without increasing cost.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Relationship-Building Sequence">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Name Exchange">
"I'm [name], by the way — what's yours?"
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Confirm the Bill">
"And you said under $50? Is that year-round or just winter?"
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Length of Ownership">
"How long have you been in the home?"
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="The Monopoly Question">
"What made you choose SDGE?" *(Answer is always: I didn't.)*
"Right — monopoly. So you've just been paying whatever they charge."
</Step>
<Step number={5} title="Financial Benefit">
"Has SDGE ever offered you anything financial in return for being a loyal customer?" *(They haven't. Let that land.)*
</Step>
<Step number={6} title="Future Timeline">
"How long are you planning to stay in the home?"
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Reframe Close">

<PullQuote>"So here's what I'm hearing: your bill is low, you're conservative with energy, and you're not sure it pencils out. What I'd want to show you is that with a system sized for your current usage, the math actually works in your favor. The question isn't whether you save enough — it's whether you want to keep paying a company that's never given you anything back, or own something instead."</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"What's really holding you back from at least seeing the numbers?"</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Bill Under $50 Nuance">

<Callout type="tip">
Two possibilities with a sub-$50 bill — and one question separates them.
</Callout>

1. **Truly low users** — good candidates for a small system, especially with EV or expansion plans
2. **Existing solar** — conversation shifts to battery storage and true-up optimization

<PullQuote>"Is that $50 after solar credits, or is that just your regular bill?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="The One Question">That one question saves a lot of wasted energy.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>
