# The Needs Audit

The Needs Audit is the keystone of the Griffin Hill system. This is what separates a good closer from an exceptional one — the closer who gets the business the first time versus the one who has to keep going back.

> "A good negotiator prepares, going in, to be ready for possible surprises; a great negotiator aims to use their skills to reveal the surprises they're certain to find." — Chris Voss

<Accordion title="The Mindset">
Go in with a mindset of discovery. Your job here is not to present. Your job is to learn.

Ask yourself: **How can I get the customer to solve their own problem?**

Research from *Never Split the Difference* frames it this way: *"Negotiation is not an act of battle — it's a process of discovery. The goal is to discover as much information as possible."*

Most reps engage in selective listening — hearing only what they want so they can jump to their solution. That is the enemy of a good Needs Audit. Quiet your mind and actually listen.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Five Principles">
### 1. Active Listening
- Ask questions
- Use drill-down / elaborate questions
- Validate what they say
- Use their name often
- Reframe and mirror statements back

*From Never Split the Difference: "By listening intensely, a negotiator demonstrates empathy and shows a sincere desire to better understand what the other side is experiencing. When individuals feel listened to, they tend to listen to themselves more carefully... they become less defensive and more willing to listen to other points of view."*

### 2. Open-Ended Questions
- Process of discovery
- Get the customer to self-solve
- Ask questions that drive them to their own conclusions
- Framework: **OPEC** — Opportunity, Problems, Effects, Consequences

### 3. Sharing Builds Trust
- Research shows the first objection is rarely the real objection
- People buy from people they like and trust
- Your job is to discover as much information as possible

### 4. Purposeful Face-Time
- Quality conversations over quantity
- The more time you spend with them, the higher your close rate
- Every minute is an investment — move them from the door to the panel, from the panel into the house

### 5. Position — Don't Sell
- This is **not** the time to discuss your solution
- Create anticipation
- No Whac-A-Mole: don't just react to whatever surfaces. Guide the conversation.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Handling Objections Inside the Needs Audit">
When an objection surfaces during the Needs Audit, the goal is not to overcome it — it's to **understand it fully**, then continue discovering.

### If You Don't Know What to Say
> "That's exactly why I'm here — other than that, what else has held you back from battery + solar?"

> "Thanks for bringing that up, that's something our energy engineers handle — other than that, what else has held you back?"

### Low Bill (1)
> "I hear you. Are you concerned that if you went solar you'd be paying more than what you currently pay? What else?"

*Variations: "We're not using much power" / "It's just the two of us" / "It's too expensive" / "The cost" / "Doesn't pencil" / "Not enough savings"*

### Low Bill (2) — They're comfortable with their bill or say no to Low Bill 1
> "I'm with you. Most of my clients don't go solar to save 10-20 bucks. They do it because we completely eliminate their bill. What else has held you back from going solar?"

### "I'm Moving"
> "Awesome, where you guys headed? Are you moving in the next few months or in the next year or two? I get it. Are you concerned that if you go solar you'll be investing money into something that isn't your forever home?"

- If yes: *"I'm with you. Other than that, is there anything else that's held you back?"*
- If no: *"I understand. It sounds like your main concern is that if you move it could be a hassle to sell the house. Right? Other than moving, is there anything else?"*

*Variation: "We don't know how long we're going to be here."*

### "We Need a New Roof"
> "I'm with you. Do you have any leaks right now?... How long have you had it?... I totally get it. Have you received any roof quotes?... What's been holding you back from getting a new roof?... 'We don't have the money.' I understand — not a lot of people have 20-30k just laying around to pay cash for a roof. Other than being concerned about the roof, is there anything else that's held you back from going solar?"

### "Not a Priority Right Now"
> "I get it. Are you worried you'd have to take money away from your current projects in order to pay for solar?"

### "We Have So Many Projects Going On" (Hassle)
> "I'm with you. It sounds like to me that solar is something you want to do but you feel like you'd be spreading yourself too thin — right? Aside from that, is there anything else that's held you back?"

*Follow-up: "If you don't mind me asking, what makes you think it'd be a hassle?"*

### "I've Heard Bad Things About Solar"
> "Of course. What are some of the bad things you've heard? What else? What else? I totally get it — what are some good things you've heard?"

### "I'm Going to Be Dead in 10 Years"
> "Yeah, I'm really glad you brought that up. Come on — you've got a lot longer than that. Well... let's play that hypothetical out. Say you weren't here in 10 years and you already had the solar. What's the situation you want to avoid? How would you want that to play out?"
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Drill Down Framework">
Every question in the Needs Audit should accomplish one of two things:

1. **Learn the reality of the prospect's status quo**
2. **Stimulate the prospect to discover how much they want what you're offering**

If your question doesn't do at least one of those, it's filler. Cut it.
</Accordion>
