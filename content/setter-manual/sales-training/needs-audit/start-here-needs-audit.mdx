# The Needs Audit: The Keystone of Everything

## The Foundation: Why Master This First

The needs audit is the keystone of the Griffin Hill system. This is what separates a good closer from an exceptional one. This is the difference between the closer that gets the business the first time around vs. the closer that has to go back to the home multiple times.

**The best overcoming objection play-set is a good Needs Audit. If the objection never surfaces because of a quality Needs Audit then you WIN the first time around.**

You cannot move forward in this training until you've mastered the needs audit. Not because it's hard, but because everything else depends on it.

---

## The Core Principle: Law of Occupied Space

There's a principle in sales called the "Law of Occupied Space."

**The principle:** The person who asks the questions occupies the space of authority. The person who answers questions is in a position of compliance.

When you ask great questions:
- You're in control of the conversation
- The customer is engaged in discovery
- You position yourself as the expert
- The customer lowers their defenses
- They start solving their own problems (which is powerful)

When the customer asks questions:
- They're in control
- You're defending
- You're positioned as needy (not authoritative)
- The conversation becomes adversarial
- They stay closed

**Master this principle and you master the sale.**

## The Needs Audit Mindset

Before you learn the questions, master the mindset:

### Mindset 1: You're a Diagnostician, Not a Salesman

Your role is to **discover** what the customer needs, not to convince them they need solar.

Think of it like visiting a doctor. You don't want the doctor to tell you immediately what medicine you need. You want them to ask questions, understand your situation, and then recommend something based on that understanding.

Be the diagnostician. Ask questions. Listen. Understand. Then prescribe.

### Mindset 2: Your Job is to Help Them Discover Their Own Problem

The most powerful realization for the customer is when **they** discover why they need solar, not when **you** tell them.

**Weak approach:** "You need solar because you're paying too much for energy."

**Strong approach:** "How long have you been paying SDGE? [Listen] And in that time, how have you seen your bill change? [Listen] Yeah, most people are surprised how much it keeps going up. That's actually what we solve for people like you."

In the second approach, **they** discovered that energy costs are rising. That's more powerful than you telling them.

### Mindset 3: Engagement Comes from Discovery

Here's a magic principle: **The more questions you ask, the more interested the customer becomes.**

Why? Because engagement is an active process, not passive. When someone is answering questions and thinking deeply, they're engaged.

When you monologue about solar, they're passive and bored.

When you ask great questions and listen deeply, they're active and interested.

### Mindset 4: The Goal is Information, Not Agreement

Don't try to get them to agree with your points during the needs audit. Just gather information.

You're not trying to convince them. You're trying to understand them.

Agreement comes later, in the solution presentation.

## The Needs Audit Routine

### Purpose of the Needs Audit (Remember these)

1. **Get information needed to build a compelling proposal** - You can't create a good quote without understanding their situation
2. **Build relationship with prospect** - Asking about their life creates connection
3. **Help decision makers reach conclusions** - When you ask the right questions, they convince themselves
4. **Break down emotional barriers and defenses** - Questions open them up; you don't
5. **Qualify for need, want, ability to pay, and decision making** - You need to know if they're viable before you spend time

---

### The Five Qualification Areas

Before you present a solution, you need to qualify for five things:

**1. Need (Pain Point)**
- Do they actually have a problem with their current energy situation?
- Is energy cost bothering them?
- Are they interested in alternatives?

**2. Want (Interest)**
- Do they actually want to solve the problem?
- Or are they just venting about it?
- Would they consider a solution?

**3. Ability to Pay (Financial)**
- Can they afford solar?
- Do they have credit, home equity, or cash?
- Are they willing to invest?

**4. Decision-Making Power**
- Is the person you're talking to the decision maker?
- Or do you need both spouses?
- Is there another decision maker (parent, partner, etc.)?

**5. Timeline**
- When would they want to install?
- This month? This year? Thinking about it?
- Is there urgency, or is this theoretical?

## The Needs Audit Questions (Master These)

### Phase 1: Rapport & Permission (1-2 minutes)

**Word Track:**
> "Hey Jimmy, thanks for taking the time. Before we dive into solar, I'd love to ask you a few questions just to understand your situation better. That way I can make sure we're a good fit. Sound fair?"

[Get their agreement to be asked questions]

### Phase 2: History & Current Situation (3-5 minutes)

**Question 1: Home Tenure**
> "How long have you lived in the home?"

Why: Understanding their timeline, stability, investment level.

**Question 2: Energy Awareness**
> "And during the time you've lived here, how have you seen your SDGE bill change?"

[Listen. They might say "gone up," "stayed the same," "I don't really track it."]

**Question 3: Probing Energy Attitudes**
> "What does your typical month look like? Like, in summer, what's your average bill? And in winter?"

[Getting usage pattern]

**Question 4: Problem Acknowledgment**
> "And how does that make you feel - the amount you're paying?"

[Emotional engagement - are they bothered by it?]

### Phase 3: Solar Awareness (2-3 minutes)

**Question 5: Past Exposure**
> "Have you looked into solar before, or is this new?"

[Assess prior knowledge]

**If they've looked into it:**
> "What did you learn? What made you interested [or not interested]?"

**If they haven't:**
> "Interesting. Well, that's why I'm here - to answer any questions you might have."

### Phase 4: Decision-Making (2-3 minutes)

**Question 6: Decision Makers**
> "Is this a decision you make by yourself, or is there someone else - like your spouse - who would need to be involved?"

[Critical: If spouse isn't present, you might reschedule]

**If spouse/partner is present:**
> "[To spouse] And how do you feel about exploring solar?"

[Gauge both decision makers' interest level]

### Phase 5: Objection Discovery (5-10 minutes)

**Question 7: The Opening**
> "So here's my question: What's been holding you back from going solar?"

[This is the magic question. Let them talk. Listen.]

[They might list several concerns. Let them finish, then ask:]

**Question 8: Drill Down**
> "OK, and is there anything else holding you back?"

[Keep asking until they say "No, that's it."]

**Question 9: Isolate the Biggest**
> "Of all those things you mentioned, which one would be the biggest barrier to moving forward?"

[Helps you prioritize in presentation]

### Phase 6: Restate & Confirm (2 minutes)

**Word Track:**
> "So if I understand you correctly, Jimmy, you haven't gone solar yet because [objection 1], [objection 2], and [objection 3]. And of those, [objection] is the biggest one. Did I get that right?"

[Confirm you understood]

> "And is there anything else I haven't asked about?"

[Open-ended check - any other concerns?]

## The Needs Audit in Action (Real Example)

**YOU:** "How long have you lived in the home?"

**CUSTOMER:** "About 10 years."

**YOU:** "And during those 10 years, how have you seen your SDGE bill change?"

**CUSTOMER:** "Oh man, it's gone up like crazy. We used to pay like $100 a month and now it's $150."

**YOU:** "Really - $100 to $150. That's significant. What does it look like in summer vs. winter?"

**CUSTOMER:** "Summer's the killer. Like $180, $190 some months."

**YOU:** "So summer it's $190, winter is lower. And how does that make you feel - paying that much?"

**CUSTOMER:** "Honestly, it's frustrating. Feels like we have no control."

**YOU:** "Yeah, that's what I hear a lot. Have you looked into solar before?"

**CUSTOMER:** "We've thought about it, but we never really dug in."

**YOU:** "Cool. Well, I'm here to answer any questions. One thing though - is this something you and your wife would both decide together, or is it just you?"

**CUSTOMER:** "Yeah, we'd both need to be on board."

**YOU:** "Good to know. So my real question is: What's been holding you back from going solar?"

**CUSTOMER:** "Well, I'm not sure if our roof can handle it. And I don't know how much it costs. And honestly, the whole thing seems complicated."

**YOU:** "OK - so roof concerns, cost questions, and complexity. Is there anything else?"

**CUSTOMER:** "No, that's probably it."

**YOU:** "Of those three - the roof, the cost, and the complexity - which one would be the biggest barrier for you?"

**CUSTOMER:** "Probably the cost. I just can't see dropping $20,000 on this right now."

**YOU:** "Got it. So Jimmy, if I understand right, you haven't gone solar because you're worried about the roof, you're concerned about the cost, and you're not sure how it all works. And the cost is the biggest thing. Do I have that right?"

**CUSTOMER:** "Yeah, that's pretty much it."

**YOU:** "Perfect. And there's nothing else I haven't asked about?"

**CUSTOMER:** "No, I think that covers it."

## The Skills You're Developing

When you master the needs audit, you're developing:

1. **Listening** - Really hearing what they say (not waiting for your turn to talk)
2. **Curiosity** - Genuine interest in their situation
3. **Qualification** - Knowing who can actually buy from you
4. **Rapport** - Building relationship through conversation
5. **Problem discovery** - Understanding their real barriers
6. **Control** - Gently leading the conversation through questions

All of these skills are the foundation of an exceptional closer.

**Week 1:** Learn the questions. Practice saying them naturally.

**Week 2:** Go to 10 doors/appointments. Ask every question. Take notes on responses.

**Week 3:** Refine based on feedback. Which questions get the best responses? Which need work?

**Week 4:** Master it. Needs audit should be conversational, not robotic.

## Common Mistakes (Don't Make These)

### Mistake 1: Talking Too Much
You: "So you're concerned about cost. Let me explain..."

You should be: "OK, cost is the biggest thing. Anything else I haven't asked?"

### Mistake 2: Defending Instead of Discovering
Customer: "Solar is too expensive."

Wrong: "Actually, it's not that expensive..."
Right: "What does 'expensive' mean to you? What would feel comfortable?"

### Mistake 3: Not Confirming You Understood
You ask questions, then jump to presentation.

You should: Restate what you heard and confirm.

### Mistake 4: Insufficient Probing
They say "We've thought about it." You move on.

You should: "Tell me more about that. What have you thought about?"

### Mistake 5: Making Assumptions
You assume they have a concern without asking.

You should: Ask directly: "What would hold you back?"
