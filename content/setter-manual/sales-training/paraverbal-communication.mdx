# Paraverbal Communication

<Lead>Words are the least important part of what you communicate. Studies consistently show that **words carry roughly 7%** of the message. The rest is delivered through nonverbal cues (55%) and paraverbal signals — tone, pace, volume, and inflection (38%).</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Tone">

<ArticleVideo src="/paraverbal-tone.mp4" />

<KeyTerm term="Tone">The emotional color of your voice. It communicates your internal state more accurately than your words — people can hear whether you're nervous, rushed, bored, or genuinely interested.</KeyTerm>

**Aim for:** Warm, calm, and unhurried. Not overly cheerful (reads fake). Not flat (reads disinterested).

<Callout type="warning" title="WATCH FOR">
- **Rising intonation** at end of statements → sounds uncertain
- **Dropping your voice** at end of sentences → sounds defeated
- **Monotone delivery** → kills engagement in under 30 seconds
</Callout>

<Callout type="tip">
Record yourself doing a pitch. Don't watch — just **listen**. What does your tone tell someone about how you feel?
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Pace">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "TOO FAST", label: "Sounds nervous, pushy, desperate" },
  { value: "TOO SLOW", label: "Sounds uncertain, condescending" },
  { value: "RIGHT", label: "Slightly slower than conversational" }
]} />

Give people time to absorb. Pause at natural breaks.

<KeyTerm term="The Power Pause">After asking a question — especially the AFP — don't fill the silence. Let them answer. Rushing past silence signals you're afraid of it.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Volume">

**Too loud:** Aggressive, especially at a door. You're in their space.

**Too quiet:** Signals insecurity. If they strain to hear you, they disengage.

**Right:** Match the environment. Quiet street = quieter. Windy afternoon = project more.

<Callout type="insight">
**Volume variation** is your secret weapon. Naturally lower your voice when sharing something important — it creates intimacy and signals: *lean in, this matters.*
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Inflection and Emphasis">

Where you place emphasis changes the meaning entirely:

<PullQuote>"We don't charge you anything upfront."</PullQuote>

vs. *"We don't charge **you** anything upfront."* vs. *"We don't charge you **anything** upfront."*

<KeyTerm term="The Rule">Emphasize the benefit, not the feature. "Zero money down" should land with emphasis on ZERO, not money.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Paraverbal and Emotion">

<Callout type="important" title="YOU CANNOT FAKE IT">
Your voice is a direct window into your emotional state. You cannot fake a calm, confident tone if you don't feel calm and confident — the dissonance leaks.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>The best paraverbal adjustment you can make is to actually believe in what you're doing.</PullQuote>

This is why mindset work isn't optional. Walk up thinking *"I genuinely have something valuable to show them"* and your voice will carry that.

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Practical Drills">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Record Every Pitch">
Not to watch — to **audit your paraverbal patterns**. Listen for pace, tone variation, and where you rush.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Mirror Drill">
Deliver your pitch to a mirror, focusing only on your voice. Don't think about words. Think about **warmth**.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Read Aloud">
5 minutes a day from anything. Loosens your voice, builds pace awareness, removes self-consciousness.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Debrief After No-Shows">
Often a monotone or rushed pitch contributed. Identify the moment and fix the next door.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>
