# Path Progression

Mastery in solar sales isn't random. It follows a sequence. The rep who tries to learn everything at once learns nothing well. The rep who builds skills in the right order develops a foundation that compounds.

<Accordion title="Stage 1: The Fundamentals (Weeks 1-4)">
Before anything else, master these in order:

1. **Case Open** — the 60-word pitch
2. **Needs Audit** — the discovery sequence
3. **Paraverbal Communication** — tone, pace, volume
4. **Nonverbal Communication** — positioning, eye contact, reading cues

These four are the engine. Every other technique in this training assumes you have them. If your case open is shaky or your needs audit feels scripted, work here first.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Stage 2: Objection Handling (Weeks 3-6, overlapping)">
Once the foundation is solid, add:

5. **Smokescreens** — surface objections and how to move past them
6. **Solving on the Door** — finding the Black Swan
7. **Additional Ammo** — reframes, vocabulary, unexpected situations
8. **SDGE Story** — the hypothetical close

You won't encounter objections at the same rate until your volume is high enough. Push your door count during this stage so you get enough reps to actually practice.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Stage 3: Appointment Quality (Weeks 5-8)">
Now focus on stickiness:

9. **Solidifying the Appointment** — the option close, rapport building
10. **Stickier Appointments** — softball questions, re-entry audit
11. **Text Templates** — confirmation system

Your closing percentage means nothing if your appointments don't show. This stage is about converting booked leads into real sits.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Stage 4: Advanced Technique (Weeks 7-10)">
With a solid foundation and a real pipeline:

12. **Drill Down Questions** — going deeper in any conversation
13. **Types of Questions** — deploying the right question at the right moment
14. **Maintain Control in Needs Audit** — handling premature questions
15. **Powerful Position Statements** — differentiating KIN with confidence
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Stage 5: Expansion (Week 10+)">
Once you're consistently booking and converting:

16. **Knocking an Install** — turning completions into referral pipelines
17. **Lead Calling** — working the phone alongside the door
18. **Door Pitch HVAC** — adding the second product line
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What Accelerates Everything">
**Door count.** Every stage progresses faster with more reps. A rep who knocks 60 doors a day compresses a 12-week development arc into 6 weeks because they encounter more situations faster.

You cannot think your way to skill. You have to knock your way there.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Open Door Policy (First 3 Weeks)">
New reps are required to attend open office hours during the first three weeks after bootcamp. This is the time to ask questions, review recordings, and get direct coaching while the fundamentals are fresh.

Use it.
</Accordion>
