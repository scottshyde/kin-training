# Powerful Position Statements

<Lead>A position statement is a deliberate claim about what you do, who you are, or what makes you different — stated with enough confidence that the prospect accepts it as fact before you've offered any proof.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Why Position Statements Work">

Most homeowners have heard a dozen solar pitches. They've been told "we're the best," "lowest prices," "great reviews." Those claims are **noise**.

<KeyTerm term="What Cuts Through">A powerful position statement is specific, confident, and often counterintuitive — which is why it separates you from every other rep.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Examples">

**On customer experience:**

<PullQuote>"We are obsessed with providing the best customer experience on the planet. Not in solar — on the planet. That's a bold claim and we mean it."</PullQuote>

**On pricing:**

<PullQuote>"I've personally audited five solar companies in this area. We have better pricing than all of them. I can say that because I've seen their numbers."</PullQuote>

**On why people go solar:**

<PullQuote>"The reason most of my clients decide to go solar isn't the savings — it's that they realize they're already paying for the energy. They're just paying the wrong company."</PullQuote>

**On margins:**

<PullQuote>"Most solar companies are operating at 20-30% profit margins. We run at 10%. The difference shows up in your monthly payment."</PullQuote>

**On personal credibility:**

<PullQuote>"I've worked at four solar companies. KIN is the only one I've seen where I actually feel good about what we're showing homeowners. That's the reason I'm here."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Structure of a Position Statement">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="The Claim">
Something specific and bold.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="The Reason">
Why it's true — even briefly.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="The Implication">
What it means for the prospect.
</Step>
</Steps>

<PullQuote>"We have better pricing than anyone else in the area — because I've actually seen what they charge. Which means when we show you numbers, you're seeing the best available."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="When to Use Them">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "CASE OPEN", label: "Position KIN before the needs audit" },
  { value: "OBJECTIONS", label: "Respond without being defensive" }
]} />

**During objections** ("I've heard bad things about solar companies"):

<PullQuote>"I understand that — and honestly, some companies deserve that reputation. The reason I can say we're different isn't just because I work here — it's because I've seen the data."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Earning the Right">

<Callout type="important" title="CONVICTION IS REQUIRED">
A position statement said with hesitation isn't a position — it's a wish.
</Callout>

Know your product. Know your competitors. Know what makes KIN genuinely different. When you know that, the statements become **natural rather than rehearsed**.

</Accordion>
