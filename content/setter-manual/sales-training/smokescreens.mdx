# Smokescreens

<Lead>A smokescreen is not a real objection. It's a reflex. Prospects have been conditioned to get door-to-door salespeople off their doorstep as fast as possible. The comments they throw at you in the first 60 seconds are defense mechanisms — not real reflections of how they think and feel.</Lead>

<Callout type="important" title="THE TRUTH">
Most of what a customer tells you in the first minute is **not true**. It's a means to get you off their doorstep.
</Callout>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Rule">

<KeyTerm term="Resolve and Return">Don't give lengthy responses. Don't explain your company. Don't defend yourself. A quick, calm, confident 4-word answer followed by picking back up your Case Open is the entire move.</KeyTerm>

<PullQuote>Your goals are bigger than their 5 minutes.</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Word Tracks">

### "Not interested."

<PullQuote>"I understand. Most people that didn't do the old solar is because either they have a low bill, or they looked into a lease. Do you mind if I ask you just 1 question?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Use the same response for: *"We're good."*
</Callout>

### "I'm busy right now." *(light)*

<PullQuote>"Sure, I'll be brief."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Same for: *"It's dinnertime" / "We're headed out."*
</Callout>

### "Can I get a card?" / "Do you have a website?"

<PullQuote>"Of course, I'll make sure you have my info."</PullQuote>

### "Not interested!" *(before you've said what you do)*

<PullQuote>"Of course — just a heads up..." (point and continue)</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
This is before you've stated your position. They're rejecting something they haven't heard yet.
</Callout>

### Passionate "WE'RE NOT INTERESTED!" / "NO SOLAR!"

<PullQuote>"I get it. Sounds like you've been through the wringer. All I have is 1 question. Is that ok?"</PullQuote>

### "I'm busy right now." *(firmer)*

<PullQuote>"Sure, I won't waste your time."</PullQuote>

### "Can I get a card?" *(second time)*

<PullQuote>"We just went paperless."</PullQuote>

### "No, I'm not interested." *(at the Ask for Permission)*

<PullQuote>"Of course. If I was doing the same thing as the last 5 guys, I'd be wasting my time. All I have is 1 question — is that ok?"</PullQuote>

### "Do you have any information you can leave?"

<PullQuote>"Ya, I'm not the flyer guy..."</PullQuote>

### "Is this solar?"

<PullQuote>"How'd you guess?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"Do I just smell like sun?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"I could sell you traditional panels but that's not why I'm here..."</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"You've probably had a lot of guys coming by to sell you solar... those guys make my job difficult. Do you mind if I ask you just two questions?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
If they identify it as solar, switch to the **Adapted Case Open**.
</Callout>

### "What are you selling?"

<PullQuote>"Girl Scout cookies. I'm just kidding — I'm right here with your neighbor Sean..."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="What NOT to Do">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Don't Pause and Explain">
The more you explain at this stage, the more you validate the objection.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Don't Get Rattled">
A smokescreen means **nothing** about whether this is a good lead. It means they've answered the door before.
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Don't Abandon Your Position">
After the 4-word response, go right back to your Case Open like nothing happened.
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Don't Repeat Yourself Twice">
If you've handled a smokescreen twice and they're still pushing, that may be a **real objection**. Read the situation.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="insight">
The rep who stays calm, delivers a short confident response, and pivots immediately is the rep who gets in the door.
</Callout>

</Accordion>
