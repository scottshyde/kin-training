# Smokescreens

A smokescreen is not a real objection. It's a reflex. Prospects have been conditioned to get door-to-door salespeople off their doorstep as fast as possible. The comments they throw at you in the first 60 seconds are defense mechanisms, not real reflections of how they think and feel.

> **Most of what a customer tells you in the first minute is not true. It's a means to get you off their doorstep.**

<Accordion title="The Rule">
**Resolve and move right back to where you left off.**

Don't give lengthy responses. Don't explain your company. Don't defend yourself. A quick, calm, confident 4-word answer followed by picking back up your Case Open is the entire move.

Remember: your goals are bigger than their 5 minutes.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Word Tracks">
### "Not interested."
> "I understand. Most people that didn't do the old solar is because either they have a low bill, or they looked into a lease. Do you mind if I ask you just 1 question?"

*Use the same response for: "We're good."*

### "I'm busy right now." *(light)*
> "Sure, I'll be brief."

*Use the same response for: "It's dinnertime" / "We're headed out."*

### "Can I get a card?" / "Do you have a website?"
> "Of course, I'll make sure you have my info."

*Use the same response for: "I'll look you guys up."*

### "Not interested!" *(before you've said what you do)*
> "Of course — just a heads up..." *(point and continue)*

*Use for: "No soliciting!" / "We're not buying whatever you're selling!" — Note: this is before you've stated your position.*

### Passionate "WE'RE NOT INTERESTED!" / "NO SOLAR!"
> "I get it. Sounds like you've been through the wringer. All I have is 1 question. Is that ok?"

### "I'm busy right now." *(firmer)*
> "Sure, I won't waste your time."

### "Can I get a card?" *(second time)*
> "We just went paperless."

### "No, I'm not interested." *(at the Ask for Permission)*
> "Of course. If I was doing the same thing as the last 5 guys, I'd be wasting my time. All I have is 1 question — is that ok?"

### "Do you have any information you can leave?"
> "Ya, I'm not the flyer guy..."

### "Is this solar?"
> "How'd you guess?"
> "Do I just smell like sun?"
> "I could sell you traditional panels but that's not why I'm here..."
> "You've probably had a lot of guys coming by to sell you solar... those guys make my job difficult. Do you mind if I ask you just two questions?"

*Note: If they identify it as solar, switch to the Adapted Case Open.*

### "What are you selling?"
> "Girl Scout cookies. I'm just kidding — I'm right here with your neighbor Sean..."
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What NOT to Do">
- **Don't pause and explain.** The more you explain at this stage, the more you validate the objection.
- **Don't get rattled.** A smokescreen means nothing about whether this is a good lead. It means they've answered the door before.
- **Don't abandon your position.** After the 4-word response, go right back to your Case Open like nothing happened.
- **Don't repeat yourself twice.** If you've handled a smokescreen twice and they're still pushing, that may be a real objection. Read the situation.

The rep who stays calm, delivers a short confident response, and pivots immediately is the rep who gets in the door.
</Accordion>
