# Solving on the Door

<Lead>Your job at the door is to sell the appointment — not the deal. This is the most commonly violated principle in door-to-door solar, and it's the source of most blown leads.</Lead>

<Callout type="important" title="THE RULE">
Give bait, not the whole thing. Find the **Black Swan**, solve that, and book.
</Callout>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Black Swan">

<KeyTerm term="Black Swan">The one concern that — if solved — would remove the real barrier to an appointment. Most prospects have one dominant objection underneath several surface-level ones.</KeyTerm>

Your job isn't to solve every concern. Your job is to find the Black Swan and **solve that one**.

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "SURFACE", label: "Not interested / Heard bad things / Too busy" },
  { value: "BLACK SWAN", label: "Don't want debt / Planning to move / Bad past experience" }
]} />

<Callout type="insight">
The surface objection is what they say. The Black Swan is what they **mean**.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="How to Find It">

<PullQuote>"Can I ask what specifically has held you back from looking at this before?"</PullQuote>

Or, after a soft "not interested":

<PullQuote>"Totally fair. Most people feel that way. Is it more the debt side of things, or the 'I've heard bad things' side, or something else entirely?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Give them options. One of them will land — and when it does, their energy shifts slightly. **That's the Black Swan.**
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Solving the Black Swan">

<Callout type="important" title="THE METHOD">
Once you identify it, solve it **directly** — briefly — and then offer 1-2 secondary solutions. Don't over-explain.
</Callout>

### Black Swan: Moving / "We Might Sell"

<PullQuote>"No debt on your end, no leasing involved. If you sell, the system either transfers to the next owner and actually increases home value, or we handle the payoff at closing — it's designed for that exact situation. Would that change your thinking at all?"</PullQuote>

### Black Swan: Roof Concerns

<PullQuote>"Not every home qualifies — that's actually part of why I want to get my manager out here to look at the roof. But here's what I'll tell you: if the roof isn't in good shape, we work with a financing program where we can handle the roof as part of the same package."</PullQuote>

Then the SDGE hypothetical:

<PullQuote>"Hypothetically — if SDGE said they'd lock in your current bill for 25 years and then stop charging you, would you take that deal? Because that's essentially what solar does — and for homes with roof issues, we can include the roof in that."</PullQuote>

Close with:

<PullQuote>"The hardest part is not every home qualifies. That's exactly why I want to get someone out to look before I make any promises."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="What Not to Do">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Don't Solve Everything">
If you answer every objection at the door, you've given them all the information they need to say no without ever seeing the numbers. The appointment exists to show them something they **can't see at the door**.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Don't Go Complex">
When an objection gets detailed, the instinct is to match it with a detailed answer. **Resist it.** More complexity creates more hesitation.
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Don't Solve and Wait">
After you solve the Black Swan, move **immediately** toward the appointment:

<PullQuote>"That's something we can address when my manager comes by. What does your schedule look like this week?"</PullQuote>

The solve is a bridge to the booking. Not the end of the conversation.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Scarcity Close">

After the solve, if they're still neutral:

<PullQuote>"The hardest part is not all homes qualify. I'm not going to know whether yours does until we actually look. Let me make sure you don't miss out on finding out."</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
Scarcity that's true isn't manipulation — it's information. Not every home qualifies, and people respond to that reality.
</Callout>

</Accordion>
