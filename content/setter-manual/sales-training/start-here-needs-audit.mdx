# START HERE: The Needs Audit

<Lead>The needs audit is the keystone of the Griffin Hill system. This is what separates a good closer from an exceptional one — the difference between the rep who gets the business the first time around versus the one who has to go back multiple times.</Lead>

<Callout type="important" title="THE PRINCIPLE">
The best overcoming-objections playbook is a **great Needs Audit.** If the objection never surfaces because you did a quality NA, you win the first time around.
</Callout>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Right Mindset">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Discovery Mindset">
Engage the process with a mindset of **discovery** — not presentation.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Self-Solve">
Ask yourself: *How can I get the customer to solve their own problem?*
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Discover Everything">
Gather as much information as possible — don't filter, just listen.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Five Principles">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Active Listening">

<PullQuote>"It all starts with the universally applicable premise that people want to be understood and accepted. By listening intensely, a negotiator demonstrates empathy and shows a sincere desire to better understand what the other side is experiencing." — Never Split the Difference</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Not Passive">Listening is the most active thing you can do in a Needs Audit. Quiet the instinct to jump to solving. The prospect is not an obstacle — they are the path.</KeyTerm>

Ask questions. Use drill-down questions. Validate what they say. Use their name often. Reframe and mirror statements back to them.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Open-Ended Questions">

This is a **process of discovery**, not a presentation. Get the customer to self-solve. Ask questions that drive them toward their own conclusions.

<KeyTerm term="OPEC Framework">Opportunity, Problems, Effects, Consequences — the four categories that structure your discovery.</KeyTerm>

<PullQuote>"Negotiation is not an act of battle; it's a process of discovery. The goal is to discover as much information as possible." — Never Split the Difference</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Sharing Builds Trust">
Research shows the first objection is rarely the real objection. People buy from people they **like and trust** to get the job done. The more you learn about them, the more you can serve them.
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Purposeful Face-Time">
Quality conversations over quantity. More time with the prospect increases your chances of closing. Move them from the door to the panel. From the panel inside. From inside, show them the roof. **Every step matters.**
</Step>

<Step number={5} title="Position — Don't Sell">

<Callout type="warning">
This is **not** the time to discuss your solutions. Your job during the Needs Audit is to create anticipation, not to pitch. No more Whac-A-Mole — stop lobbing solutions at objections before you understand the real problem.
</Callout>
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Needs Audit Word Tracks">

**If You Don't Know What to Say:**

<PullQuote>"That's exactly why I'm here — other than that, what else has held you back from battery + solar?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"Thanks for bringing that up, that's something our energy engineers handle. Other than that, what else has held you back?"</PullQuote>

**Low Bill 1:**

<PullQuote>"I hear you. Are you concerned that if you went solar you'd be paying more than you currently pay? What else?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="tip">
Also covers: "We're not using much power" / "It's just the two of us" / "It's too expensive" / "The cost" / "Doesn't pencil" / "Not enough savings"
</Callout>

**Low Bill 2** *(Customer is comfortable or says no to Low Bill 1):*

<PullQuote>"I'm with you. Most of my clients don't go solar to save 10-20 bucks. They do it because we completely eliminate their bill. What else has held you back from going solar?"</PullQuote>

**I'm Moving:**

<PullQuote>"Awesome, where are you guys headed? Are you moving in the next few months or more like a year or two?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"I get it. Are you concerned that if you go solar you'll be investing money into something that isn't your forever home?"</PullQuote>

**We Need a New Roof:**

<PullQuote>"I'm with you. Do you have any leaks right now?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"Not a lot of people have 20-30k just laying around to pay cash for a roof. Other than being concerned about the roof, is there anything else that's held you back from going solar?"</PullQuote>

**Not a Priority / Putting Money Into Other Things:**

<PullQuote>"I get it. Are you worried you'll have to take money away from your current projects to pay for solar?"</PullQuote>

**So Many Projects Right Now:**

<PullQuote>"I'm with you. It sounds like solar is something you want to do but you feel like you'd be spreading yourself too thin. Right? Aside from that, is there anything else?"</PullQuote>

**I've Heard Bad Things About Solar:**

<PullQuote>"Of course. What are some of the bad things you've heard? What else? What else?"</PullQuote>

<PullQuote>"I totally get it. What are some good things you've heard?"</PullQuote>

**I'm Going to Be Dead in 10 Years:**

<PullQuote>"Yeah, I'm really glad you brought that up. Come on, you've got a lot longer than that. Let's play that hypothetical out — say you weren't here in 10 years and you already had the solar. What's the situation you want to avoid? How would you want that to play out?"</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Drill-Down Questions">

<Callout type="insight" title="TWO THINGS AT ONCE">
Every drill-down question accomplishes two things: learn the reality of the prospect's status quo, and stimulate the prospect to discover how much they actually want what you're offering.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Psychotherapy research shows that when individuals feel listened to, they tend to listen to themselves more carefully and openly evaluate and clarify their own thoughts and feelings. In addition, they tend to become less defensive and oppositional and more willing to listen to other points of view." — Never Split the Difference</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="Invitation, Not Interrogation">The drill-down is not prying — you're giving them space to arrive at their own conclusion.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Law of Occupied Space">

<KeyTerm term="The Principle">You cannot introduce something new into a space that is already full. If the customer's mind is occupied by doubt, confusion, or a prior narrative about solar, your solution has nowhere to land.</KeyTerm>

<Callout type="insight">
The Needs Audit **clears the space**. It surfaces the real concern, validates it, and creates room for something better. That's why closers who skip the Needs Audit work twice as hard for half the results.
</Callout>

</Accordion>
