The needs audit is the keystone of the Griffin Hill system. This is what separates a good closer from an exceptional one. The difference between the rep who gets the business the first time around versus the one who has to go back to the home multiple times.

> "A good negotiator prepares, going in, to be ready for possible surprises; a great negotiator aims to use their skills to reveal the surprises they're certain to find." — Chris Voss

**The best overcoming-objections playbook is a great Needs Audit.** If the objection never surfaces because you did a quality NA, you win the first time around.

---

## The Right Mindset

Before you ask the first question, get your head right:

- Engage the process with a mindset of **discovery**
- Ask yourself: *How can I get the customer to solve their own problem?*
- Discover as much information as possible — don't filter, just listen

---

## The Five Principles

### 1. Active Listening

- Ask questions
- Use drill-down / elaborate questions
- Validate what they say
- Use their name often
- Reframe and mirror statements back to them

> "It all starts with the universally applicable premise that people want to be understood and accepted. By listening intensely, a negotiator demonstrates empathy and shows a sincere desire to better understand what the other side is experiencing." — Never Split the Difference

Listening is not passive. It is the most active thing you can do in a Needs Audit. As reps, we often engage in *selective listening* — hearing only what we want to hear so we can jump to solving. Quiet that instinct. The prospect is not an obstacle to route around. They are the path.

### 2. Open-Ended Questions

- This is a **process of discovery**, not a presentation
- Get the customer to self-solve
- Ask questions that drive customers toward their own conclusions
- Use the OPEC framework: **Opportunity, Problems, Effects, Consequences**

> "Negotiation is not an act of battle; it's a process of discovery. The goal is to discover as much information as possible." — Never Split the Difference

### 3. Sharing Builds Trust

- Research shows the first objection is rarely the real objection
- People buy from people they like and trust to get the job done
- The more you learn about them, the more you can serve them

### 4. Purposeful Face-Time

- Quality conversations over quantity
- More time spent with the prospect increases your chances of closing
- Move them from the door to the panel. From the panel inside. From inside, show them the roof. Every step matters.

### 5. Position — Don't Sell

This is **not** the time to discuss your solutions. Your job during the Needs Audit is to create anticipation, not to pitch.

No more Whac-A-Mole. Stop lobbing solutions at objections before you understand the real problem.

## Needs Audit Word Tracks

### If You Don't Know What to Say
- *"That's exactly why I'm here — other than that, what else has held you back from battery + solar?"*
- *"Thanks for bringing that up, that's something our energy engineers handle. Other than that, what else has held you back?"*

---

### Low Bill 1
**"I hear you. Are you concerned that if you went solar you'd be paying more than you currently pay? What else?"**

> Tip: Other phrases in this category: "We're not using much power" / "It's just the two of us" / "It's too expensive" / "The cost" / "Doesn't pencil" / "Not enough savings"

### Low Bill 2 *(Customer is comfortable with their bill or says no to Low Bill 1)*
**"I'm with you. Most of my clients don't go solar to save 10–20 bucks. They do it because we completely eliminate their bill. What else has held you back from going solar?"**

---

### I'm Moving *(Real concern: not wanting to invest in a non-forever home)*
**"Awesome, where are you guys headed? Are you moving in the next few months or more like a year or two?"**

- *"I get it. Are you concerned that if you go solar you'll be investing money into something that isn't your forever home?"*
  - **Yes:** "I'm with you. Other than that, is there anything else that's held you back?"
  - **No:** "It sounds like your main concern is that if you move, it could be a hassle to sell the house. Right? Other than moving, is there anything else?"

> Tip: Also covers "We don't know how long we're going to be here."

---

### We Need a New Roof
**"I'm with you. Do you have any leaks right now?"**

- *"No, our roof is just old."* → "How long have you had it?… I totally get it. Have you received any roof quotes?… What's been holding you back from getting a new roof?… Not a lot of people have 20–30k just laying around to pay cash for a roof. Other than being concerned about the roof, is there anything else that's held you back from going solar?"

---

### Not a Priority / Putting Money Into Other Things
**"I get it. Are you worried you'll have to take money away from your current projects to pay for solar?"**

---

### So Many Projects Right Now *(The Hassle Objection)*
**"I'm with you. It sounds like solar is something you want to do but you feel like you'd be spreading yourself too thin. Right? Aside from that, is there anything else?"**

- Follow-up: *"If you don't mind me asking — what makes you think it'd be a hassle?"*

---

### I've Heard Bad Things About Solar
**"Of course. What are some of the bad things you've heard? What else? What else?"**

Then: *"I totally get it. What are some good things you've heard?"*

---

### I'm Going to Be Dead in 10 Years
**"Yeah, I'm really glad you brought that up. Come on, you've got a lot longer than that. Let's play that hypothetical out — say you weren't here in 10 years and you already had the solar. What's the situation you want to avoid? How would you want that to play out?"**

---

## Drill-Down Questions

Every drill-down question accomplishes two things:

1. Learn the reality of the prospect's status quo
2. Stimulate the prospect to discover how much they actually want what you're offering

> "Psychotherapy research shows that when individuals feel listened to, they tend to listen to themselves more carefully and openly evaluate and clarify their own thoughts and feelings. In addition, they tend to become less defensive and oppositional and more willing to listen to other points of view." — Never Split the Difference

The drill-down is not interrogation. It's invitation. You're not prying — you're giving them space to arrive at their own conclusion.

---

## The Law of Occupied Space

The Needs Audit operates on the principle that **you cannot introduce something new into a space that is already full.** If the customer's mind is occupied by doubt, confusion, or a prior narrative about solar, your solution has nowhere to land.

The Needs Audit clears the space. It surfaces the real concern, validates it, and creates room for something better.

That's why closers who skip the Needs Audit work twice as hard for half the results.
