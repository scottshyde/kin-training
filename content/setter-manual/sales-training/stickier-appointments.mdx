# Stickier Appointments

The difference between a 40% show rate and a 70% show rate isn't luck or territory. It's the quality of the door interaction that produced the booking. Stickier appointments come from doing four things deliberately: gathering schedule context, getting off the doorstep, building both kinds of rapport, and using the option close.

<Accordion title="Softball Questions">
Softball questions are how you gather the prospect's schedule before asking for a time. They feel like casual conversation — because they are — but they also give you the information you need to make the option close precise.

> *"Do you work during the week, or are you home more during the day?"*

> *"Is evenings usually better for you, or do you prefer mornings on weekends?"*

> *"Are you usually around in the evenings or do you have a pretty packed week?"*

These questions serve two purposes:
1. They give you intel for the option close
2. They make the prospect feel considered, not processed

When you know their schedule before you ask for time, the option close feels logical instead of pushy.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Quality Needs Audit = No Reason Not to Book">
A thorough needs audit does more than gather information — it creates investment. The more a prospect shares about their situation, the more they're emotionally committed to the conversation continuing.

> *After a complete needs audit, there's genuinely no good reason not to book. They've already invested the time. They've already told you their concerns. The appointment is just the natural next step.*

If the needs audit feels rushed or surface-level, the prospect hasn't invested enough to show. Go deeper. Ask more. Let them talk.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Option Close">
Once you have schedule context, the option close is how you book without asking a wide-open question.

> *"What works better — evenings this week, or this weekend?"*

*(Evenings.)*

> *"Tuesday or Wednesday?"*

*(Tuesday.)*

> *"Six or six-thirty?"*

*(Six-thirty.)*

> *"Perfect. Let me get your number so my manager can confirm."*

At each step, the choice narrows. By the time you're at "six or six-thirty" they're already mentally there — it's just the time that's in question.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Getting Off the Doorstep">
The doorstep is a liminal space — they're still in "decision mode" as long as you're both standing there. Getting off the doorstep moves the interaction from "should I do this?" to "okay, this is happening."

Tactics:
- Ask to do the panel check: *"Let me just take a quick look at the roofline while I'm here."*
- Pull out your phone to take down their number
- Ask if they want to step inside while you're taking notes
- Walk them to the property line to look at the roof angle together

Any small movement breaks the static energy of the doorstep interaction and signals forward momentum.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Close Again with the Calendar">
Before you leave, confirm the time with specificity — and get confirmation from them verbally:

> *"So Tuesday at six-thirty — that works for you?"*

Wait for the verbal yes. Don't assume. The verbal confirmation matters because it activates commitment. People follow through on things they've said out loud more reliably than things that are just understood.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Re-Entry Needs Audit">
If they've gone through everything and still won't commit to a time, re-enter the needs audit:

> *"Can I ask you something honestly? Everyone who's been excited about this has eventually moved forward. What's really holding you back from at least seeing the numbers?"*

This question does two things:
1. It normalizes moving forward (everyone does it)
2. It asks them to name the real barrier

Whatever they say, address it directly — and then ask for the booking again. Most of the time, the real barrier is smaller than the one they've been performing.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="After You Leave">
The appointment doesn't solidify at the door — it solidifies in the 24 hours after. Send the confirmation text within 30 minutes. Touch base the day before. If they haven't confirmed, call.

The reps with the highest show rates treat the confirmation process like it matters. Because it does.
</Accordion>
