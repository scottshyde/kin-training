# Text Templates — Self-Gen

Self-gen leads are your highest-value pipeline. You found them, you qualified them, you own the relationship. These templates maintain that warmth from the door to the sit.

**Your Popl card:** https://popl.co/card/xtN92upV/5/s
Sign off as: *David West — Your Energy Best*

<Accordion title="Booked — With Bill">
> *"Hey [Name]! It was great meeting you today. Confirming your appointment for [Day] at [Time]. Based on your bill, I think the numbers are going to look really solid for you. See you then! — David West, Your Energy Best"*
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Booked — No Bill">
> *"Hey [Name] — David here from KIN Home. Great chatting with you! Confirming [Day] at [Time]. One thing that would help: if you can log into myenergycenter.com and screenshot your usage page, I can make sure we show you the most accurate numbers. Looking forward to it! — David West, Your Energy Best"*
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Interested — No Booking">
When they were warm but didn't commit to a time:

> *"Hey [Name] — David from KIN. Really enjoyed talking with you today. I know you wanted a bit more time to think, but I didn't want to close out my schedule without giving you the chance to see the numbers. I've got [Day] at [Time] or [Day] at [Time] — either one work? — David West, Your Energy Best"*
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Follow-Up (No Response)">
> *"Hey [Name] — just following up from our conversation [day]. No pressure at all — just want to make sure you have the chance to see what we put together before the week closes out. Worth 20 minutes if you're curious. — David West, Your Energy Best"*
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Tone Notes for Self-Gen">
Self-gen texts should feel slightly more personal than company-assigned lead texts. You met this person. They know your name. The text should reflect that:

- Use their name
- Reference something specific from the conversation if you can
- Don't over-explain — the warmth was built at the door
- Keep it brief and human

The sign-off "Your Energy Best" is intentional — it's memorable and warm without being salesy.
</Accordion>
