# Verbal Jiujitsu

<Lead>Verbal jiujitsu is the art of redirecting resistance without meeting it head-on. In physical jiujitsu, you don't meet force with force. You redirect the opponent's energy. Same principle applies at the door.</Lead>

<Callout type="important" title="THE CORE PRINCIPLE">
When someone says "I'm not interested," you don't argue and convince. You **redirect** and **reframe**.
</Callout>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="The Core Principle: Never Argue">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "THE TRAP", label: "They say 'not interested' — you argue why they SHOULD be" },
  { value: "THE RESULT", label: "They become MORE defensive" },
  { value: "THE WAY", label: "Acknowledge, question, redirect" }
]} />

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Technique 1: The Acknowledgment + Reframe">

**Situation:** "I'm not interested in solar."

<Callout type="warning" title="WRONG">
"But it saves you money! You should at least hear..."
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"I hear you. Most people aren't interested until they see the actual numbers. That's exactly why I'm here — just to show you quickly if it makes sense. Sound fair?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="What's Happening">Acknowledge their position → Reframe lack of interest as normal → Reposition as educator → Ask permission softly.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Technique 2: The Curiosity Question">

**Situation:** "I already looked into solar and decided against it."

<Callout type="warning" title="WRONG">
"Well, things have changed. Technology is better now..."
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Oh interesting. Tell me about that. What did you learn, and what made you decide against it?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="What's Happening">Show genuine curiosity → Get them talking → Understand their real concern → Open the door to addressing it specifically.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Technique 3: The Assumption Redirect">

**Situation:** "Solar is too expensive."

<Callout type="warning" title="WRONG">
"Actually, you can finance it for just $X per month..."
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"I get it. What does 'expensive' mean to you? Like, what would feel comfortable?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="What's Happening">Acknowledge the concern → Get specific about what "expensive" means → Shift from arguing price to understanding budget.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Technique 4: The Third-Party Reference">

**Situation:** "My neighbor tried solar and wasn't happy."

<Callout type="warning" title="WRONG">
"Well, they probably went with a bad company..."
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Oh, that's helpful to know. What specifically happened with them? Because that's exactly the kind of situation I help people avoid."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="What's Happening">Learn from their reference → Separate their neighbor's experience from yours → Position yourself as someone who learns from mistakes.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Technique 5: The Reflection + Question">

**Situation:** "I want to think about it."

<Callout type="warning" title="WRONG">
"What's to think about? You're paying too much, solar saves you money, let's move forward..."
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"I totally understand wanting to think about it. What specifically do you want to think through? I might be able to help clarify something right now."</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="What's Happening">Validate their thinking process → Find out what they're uncertain about → Address the real concern — often education, not indecision.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Technique 6: The Agree + Reframe">

**Situation:** "Everyone says solar is a great investment, but I'm skeptical."

<Callout type="warning" title="WRONG">
"Well, it's not everyone, it's..." (defensive)
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Healthy skepticism is actually smart. You should be skeptical about big decisions. That's exactly why people like you want to see the actual numbers before committing. Right?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="What's Happening">Agree with their skepticism → Reframe it as a strength → Position yourself as satisfying their skepticism → Redirect naturally.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Technique 7: The Hypothetical Question">

**Situation:** "I don't think our roof can handle solar."

<Callout type="warning" title="WRONG">
"Most roofs can. Unless you have a really old roof..."
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Fair point. If we could confirm your roof is in good shape, would that change things for you?"</PullQuote>

<KeyTerm term="What's Happening">Acknowledge the concern → Test if it's the real barrier or an excuse → If real: solve it. If excuse: find the actual barrier.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Verbal Jiujitsu Framework">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Acknowledge">
*"I hear you"* — *"That makes sense"* — *"I understand"*
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Don't Argue">
Don't defend. Don't convince. Don't lecture.
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Ask a Question">
*"Tell me more about that"* — *"What specifically concerns you?"* — *"If we could solve that, would this work?"*
</Step>

<Step number={4} title="Redirect">
Reframe their objection. Find the real concern. Position yourself as helper.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Common Door Resistances">

### "Not interested"

<PullQuote>"I hear you. Most people aren't interested until they see how much they could save. That's all I'm asking — two minutes to show you the numbers. Sound fair?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="insight">
Reframe: Interest comes from information, not from initial interest.
</Callout>

### "Not now, I'm busy"

<PullQuote>"I completely understand — I'll keep it quick. Two minutes, and if it doesn't make sense, I'm out. Fair?"</PullQuote>

### "I'm renting"

<PullQuote>"Got it. So you don't own. That actually makes sense then — solar works for owners. Just out of curiosity, are you thinking about buying in the area anytime soon?"</PullQuote>

### "Already have a quote"

<PullQuote>"Oh cool, you've done your homework. Who'd you talk to? And what stood out to you about them?"</PullQuote>

### "I don't have time to talk"

<PullQuote>"Perfect, I don't need much. Just one quick question: how long have you been paying SDGE? You been in the home that whole time? OK cool. Would Thursday afternoon work better to sit down and talk details?"</PullQuote>

### "Let me talk to my spouse first"

<PullQuote>"That's smart — this is a family decision. When's your spouse home? Maybe I can talk to both of you together Thursday when they're here?"</PullQuote>

### "I don't like salespeople"

<PullQuote>"I get it. Most people don't like the hard sell. That's not my style. I'm here because I help people figure out if solar actually makes sense for them. Some people it does, some it doesn't. Let me ask you a quick question..."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Philosophy">

<PullQuote>Verbal jiujitsu isn't manipulation. It's the art of conversation — of respecting someone's objection while gently exploring whether it's the real barrier or just surface resistance.</PullQuote>

The best closers aren't the ones who convince the hardest. They're the ones who **ask the best questions**, listen the deepest, and redirect resistance without ever making it confrontational.

<Callout type="insight">
Master verbal jiujitsu and you'll set more appointments than you ever thought possible. Because you won't be fighting resistance. You'll be **dancing with it**.
</Callout>
