# Solar Basics for Setters: Enough to Sound Credible

You don't need to be a solar engineer. But you DO need to understand solar well enough to sound credible, answer basic questions, and qualify customers. You're not selling solar. You're setting appointments with people who might buy solar.

<Accordion title="Solar 101: The Basics">
### What is Solar?

Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity. That electricity powers your home. Excess electricity goes back to the grid (SDGE credits you).

That's it. That's solar. Everything else is details.

### How Much Does It Save?

Average San Diego homeowner:
- Current SDGE bill: $150/month
- With solar: $50-80/month
- **Savings: $70/month, or $840/year**

That's the number that matters at the door.

### How Long Does It Last?

- Solar panels: 25+ years (covered by 25-year warranty)
- Inverter: 25+ years (covered by 25-year warranty)
- System: Full warranty for 25 years
- Long-term: Systems from 30+ years ago still work

### How Does It Work When It's Cloudy?

- Solar works on cloudy days (produces ~25-50% of sunny day output)
- San Diego has 260+ sunny days/year, so annual production is great
- System is sized to handle average production
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="SDGE Rate Increases: Your Strongest Selling Point">
**This is critical. Every setter should know this cold.**

**Historical increases:**
- SDGE rates have increased 5-8% annually
- Not slowing down
- This year: 18% increase announced

**What this means:**
- A $150/month bill today = $177/month in one year (with 18% increase)
- Without solar, customer's bill keeps going up
- With solar, bill is locked in (or increases slower)

**Your word track at the door:**
> "SDGE is raising rates 18% this year. Most people don't realize how much their bill will go up over 25 years. That's exactly what solar solves."
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Financing Options: Your Framework">
### PPA (Power Purchase Agreement)

**What customer sees:**
- $0 down
- Monthly solar bill (~$115/month example)
- 25 years locked in
- KIN maintains system

**Your pitch:**
> "No money down. Pay $115/month for solar instead of $150/month for SDGE. That's $35 savings right now."

### Loan

**What customer sees:**
- Owns system immediately
- Gets 30% federal tax credit ($6,000)
- Monthly payment for 7-15 years
- Tax credit reduces cost significantly

**Your pitch:**
> "You own it. You get a $6,000 tax credit. Monthly payment is about $160, which is actually less than your current SDGE bill."

### Cash

**What customer sees:**
- Pay $20,000 upfront
- Own system forever
- Get $6,000 tax credit
- ~$1,800/year savings = 7-8 year payoff

**Your pitch:**
> "If you have the cash, it's the best financial decision. $20,000 investment, $6,000 tax credit, and $1,800/year in savings for 25 years."
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Equipment You're Installing">
### Solar Panels

**What setters should know:**
- Convert sunlight to electricity
- Made to last 25+ years
- KIN uses premium brands (high efficiency)
- Bigger = more power, more cost

**Don't say "panels are complicated"** - Keep it simple.

### Inverter

**What setters should know:**
- Converts DC power from panels to AC power for your home
- Usually lasts 25+ years
- Central hub of the system
- Included in 25-year warranty

**Don't try to explain AC/DC** - Not necessary for setters.

### Batteries (Optional Add-On)

**What setters should know:**
- Stores solar power for evening use
- Adds $8,000-15,000
- Not necessary for basic solar
- Better for maximum savings/backup

**Don't explain battery chemistry** - Keep it simple: stores power.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Common Door Questions (and Simple Answers)">
### Q: "Do solar panels work on cloudy days?"
A: "Yes, absolutely. They produce about 25-50% on cloudy days. San Diego has so many sunny days that annual production is excellent."

### Q: "What about when it's hot?"
A: "Panels work in heat, though slightly less efficiently than cool days. But our panels are designed for San Diego heat, and the math still works out great."

### Q: "How much does solar cost?"
A: "Depends on your home size. Typical system is $15,000-25,000. But the government gives a 30% tax credit, and there are financing options, so it's very affordable."

### Q: "How long does it last?"
A: "25+ years, covered by warranty. Systems from 20+ years ago are still producing great. Essentially, it lasts forever."

### Q: "What if my roof is bad?"
A: "We check your roof before installing. If it needs work, we handle that first. Either way, it gets addressed."

### Q: "Can I remove it if I move?"
A: "Yes, if you want to. But most people leave it on the home - it increases the home's value. The new owner usually takes over the contract."

### Q: "Is solar new technology?"
A: "No, it's been around for decades. Very proven. Millions of systems installed worldwide."

### Q: "What happens at night?"
A: "You draw from the grid, just like now. But during the day, you produced power. The grid credits you for excess. It all balances out."
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="SDGE-Specific Knowledge">
### Net Metering

**Simple explanation:**
- Day: Solar produces power for your home. Excess goes to grid. SDGE credits you.
- Night: You use grid power. Uses credits you earned.
- Month: Bill shows net consumption (usage minus production).

**Your word track:**
> "Think of SDGE as a battery. During sunny days, you produce more than you use, so you credit SDGE. At night, you draw from grid using those credits. Very simple."

### Time of Use Rates

**Simple explanation:**
- SDGE charges different rates at different times
- Peak (4-9 PM): Most expensive
- Mid-peak/off-peak: Cheaper
- Summer: Rates are higher

**Your word track:**
> "Solar production peaks at midday when rates are lower. That's actually perfect, because you're saving on lower-cost power, and it still offsets your peak evening usage."
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What NOT to Say">
### Don't Say: "Our equipment is better because of X technical specification"
**Why:** Customer doesn't understand/care about technical specs
**Say instead:** "We use the highest quality equipment, backed by a 25-year warranty. You're protected."

### Don't Say: "You need solar because of environmental reasons"
**Why:** Financial motivation is stronger than environmental
**Say instead:** "Solar saves you money, protects you from rate increases, and also helps the environment."

### Don't Say: "Solar produces 8,000 kWh per year" (too specific)
**Why:** Customer doesn't relate to kWh
**Say instead:** "Your system will produce about $1,800 worth of power annually."

### Don't Say: "Net metering allows for peak shaving through demand response aggregation"
**Why:** Way too complicated
**Say instead:** "You produce power during the day, use credits at night. Simple."
</Accordion>
