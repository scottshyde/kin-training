# Solar Basics for Setters: Enough to Sound Credible

<Lead>You don't need to be a solar engineer. But you DO need to understand solar well enough to sound credible, answer basic questions, and qualify customers. You're not selling solar. You're setting appointments with people who might buy solar.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Solar 101: The Basics">

<KeyTerm term="What Is Solar">Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity. That electricity powers your home. Excess goes back to the grid and SDGE credits you. That's it. Everything else is details.</KeyTerm>

**How Long Does It Last?**
- Solar panels: **25+ years** (covered by warranty)
- Inverter: **25+ years** (covered by warranty)
- Systems from 30+ years ago still work

**Cloudy Days?**
- Produces ~25-50% of sunny day output
- San Diego has **260+ sunny days/year**
- System is sized to handle average production

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Financing Options: Your Framework">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="PPA (Power Purchase Agreement)">

$0 down. Monthly solar bill (~$115/month). 25 years locked in. KIN maintains the system.

<PullQuote>"No money down. Pay $115/month for solar instead of $150/month for SDGE. That's $35 savings right now."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Loan">

Own the system immediately. Get 30% federal tax credit ($6,000). Monthly payment for 7-15 years.

<PullQuote>"You own it. You get a $6,000 tax credit. Monthly payment is about $160, which is actually less than your current SDGE bill."</PullQuote>
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Cash">

Pay $20,000 upfront. Own it forever. $6,000 tax credit. 7-8 year payoff.

<PullQuote>"If you have the cash, it's the best financial decision. $20,000 investment, $6,000 tax credit, and $1,800/year in savings for 25 years."</PullQuote>
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Common Door Questions (and Simple Answers)">

**"Do solar panels work on cloudy days?"**

<PullQuote>"Yes, absolutely. They produce about 25-50% on cloudy days. San Diego has so many sunny days that annual production is excellent."</PullQuote>

**"How much does solar cost?"**

<PullQuote>"Depends on your home size. Typical system is $15,000-25,000. But the government gives a 30% tax credit, and there are financing options, so it's very affordable."</PullQuote>

**"How long does it last?"**

<PullQuote>"25+ years, covered by warranty. Systems from 20+ years ago are still producing great. Essentially, it lasts forever."</PullQuote>

**"What if my roof is bad?"**

<PullQuote>"We check your roof before installing. If it needs work, we handle that first. Either way, it gets addressed."</PullQuote>

**"Can I remove it if I move?"**

<PullQuote>"Yes, if you want to. But most people leave it on the home — it increases the home's value. The new owner usually takes over the contract."</PullQuote>

**"What happens at night?"**

<PullQuote>"You draw from the grid, just like now. But during the day, you produced power. The grid credits you for excess. It all balances out."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="SDGE-Specific Knowledge">

**Net Metering:**

<KeyTerm term="Simple Explanation">Day: solar produces power, excess goes to grid, SDGE credits you. Night: you use grid power with those credits. Month: bill shows net consumption (usage minus production).</KeyTerm>

<PullQuote>"Think of SDGE as a battery. During sunny days, you produce more than you use, so you credit SDGE. At night, you draw from grid using those credits. Very simple."</PullQuote>

**Time of Use Rates:**

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "4-9 PM", label: "Peak — Most Expensive" },
  { value: "OFF-PEAK", label: "Rest of Day — Cheaper" },
  { value: "SUMMER", label: "Rates Are Higher" }
]} />

<PullQuote>"Solar production peaks at midday when rates are lower. That's actually perfect, because you're saving on lower-cost power, and it still offsets your peak evening usage."</PullQuote>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What NOT to Say">

<Callout type="warning" title="AVOID THESE AT THE DOOR">
Each mistake kills credibility or overwhelms the prospect.
</Callout>

| **Don't Say** | **Say Instead** |
|----------|------------|
| "Our equipment is better because of X technical spec" | "We use the highest quality equipment, backed by a 25-year warranty. You're protected." |
| "You need solar for environmental reasons" | "Solar saves you money, protects you from rate increases, and also helps the environment." |
| "Solar produces 8,000 kWh per year" | "Your system will produce about $1,800 worth of power annually." |
| "Net metering allows peak shaving through demand response aggregation" | "You produce power during the day, use credits at night. Simple." |

</Accordion>
