# Setter Team Norms: The Culture of Excellence

<Lead>A great setter team doesn't just set appointments — they set the foundation for everything that comes after. Setters are the frontline of KIN. The way you show up, the attitude you carry, and the quality of work you do directly impacts closers, customers, and the entire company.</Lead>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Norm 1: Show Up Every Day, On Time, Ready to Go">

<Callout type="important" title="PERFECT ATTENDANCE IS EXPECTED">
You're at your first door by the scheduled time. You don't call in sick to avoid tough weeks.
</Callout>

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Monday Morning 9:15 AM">
You're in the meeting room, not pulling in.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Review Script the Night Before">
Show up prepared, not scrambling.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Water, Phone, Materials Ready">
No excuses for missing tools.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Show Up for Your Teammate">
They're counting on you. Consistency builds momentum.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Norm 2: Execute the System with Precision">

<KeyTerm term="The Standard">Use the exact door approach script. Ask the exact needs audit questions. Follow the solidification process. Don't improvise until you understand why the system works.</KeyTerm>

<Callout type="insight">
The system is proven — other reps use it successfully. Consistency compounds results. Closers can coach you when your process is consistent.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Norm 3: Get Both Decision Makers and the Bill">

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "BOTH", label: "Decision Makers Present" },
  { value: "BILL", label: "SDGE Bill Secured" },
  { value: "0", label: "Exceptions" }
]} />

<PullQuote>"Will both of you be here?"</PullQuote>

<Callout type="warning">
This is **non-negotiable.** Single decision maker appointments waste closer time. Bills without information = inaccurate proposals. If spouse isn't home, reschedule.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Norm 4: Respect the Closer's Time">

<KeyTerm term="Quality Standard">Your appointment is real (customer will show). It has both decision makers. It has the SDGE bill. Your customer isn't just "curious" — they're qualified.</KeyTerm>

<StatBlock stats={[
  { value: "80%+", label: "Target Show Rate" }
]} />

<Callout type="tip">
If your show rate is lower than 80%, you're overbooking — not over-performing.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Norm 5: Take Feedback Without Defensiveness">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="'Good Catch'">
Instead of defending.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Try the Suggestion Next Door">
Implement immediately.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Follow Up with Results">
Report back on what changed.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Ask Clarifying Questions">
Not defensive questions.
</Step>
</Steps>

<KeyTerm term="The Truth">Your best growth comes from feedback. Defensive people don't improve. Coachable people become top performers.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Norm 6: Support Your Teammates">

<Callout type="insight">
Teams outperform individuals. Collaboration is faster than competition. Your teammate's success helps the whole team.
</Callout>

<PullQuote>"Nice job on that door."</PullQuote>

Celebrate when someone sets a great appointment. Help a struggling teammate. Share techniques that work. A team win is your win.

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Norm 7: Represent KIN with Professionalism">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Dress Professionally">
KIN branded gear or professional dress — no holes, stains, offensive graphics.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Polite to Every Door">
"Thanks for your time" whether they book or not.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Don't Trash Competitors">
"They're good people, just a different approach."
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Follow Through">
When you say you'll follow up, follow up.
</Step>
</Steps>

<KeyTerm term="You Are the Brand">Customers think "KIN is professional" based on you. Your standards set neighborhood standards.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Norm 8: Own Your Results">

<Callout type="warning" title="NO EXCUSES">
"Bad neighborhood" isn't an excuse. "Busy day" doesn't explain poor performance.
</Callout>

| Situation | Ownership Response |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Miss appointment goal | "I need to adjust X and Y" |
| Not getting bills | "Let me work on my bill request" |
| Low show rate | "I'm not qualifying well enough" |
| Great week | "Here's what I did differently" |

<KeyTerm term="The Principle">Accountability drives improvement. Excuses justify mediocrity. Ownership attracts opportunity.</KeyTerm>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />

<Accordion title="Norm 9: Never Stop Learning">

<Steps>
<Step number={1} title="Review Script Nightly">
Keep it fresh and natural.
</Step>
<Step number={2} title="Ask 'Why That Question?'">
Understand the system, not just the words.
</Step>
<Step number={3} title="Watch Top Performers">
Study their door technique.
</Step>
<Step number={4} title="Read Training Materials">
The playsets and word tracks exist for a reason.
</Step>
<Step number={5} title="Understand the Full Flow">
How door → appointment → deal works end to end.
</Step>
</Steps>

</Accordion>

<GoldRule />
