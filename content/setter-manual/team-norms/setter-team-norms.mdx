# Setter Team Norms: The Culture of Excellence

## What Makes a Great Setter Team

A great setter team doesn't just set appointments - they set the foundation for everything that comes after. Setters are the frontline of KOTA. The way you show up, the attitude you carry, and the quality of work you do directly impacts closers, customers, and the entire company.

These norms define what a high-performing setter team looks like.

---

## The Core Setter Team Norms

### Norm 1: Show Up Every Day, On Time, Ready to Go

**What it means:**
- You're at your first door by the scheduled time
- You're mentally ready to knock
- You've practiced your script
- You have your materials
- You don't call in sick to avoid tough weeks
- Perfect attendance is expected

**In practice:**
- Monday morning at 9:15 AM - you're in the meeting room, not pulling in
- You review your script the night before
- You have water, phone, and materials ready
- You don't text in 5 minutes before: "running late"
- You show up for your teammate (they're counting on you)

**Why it matters:**
- Consistency builds momentum
- Customers see the same professional rep
- Closers have scheduled appointments (not surprises)
- Momentum compounds

---

### Norm 2: Execute the System with Precision

**What it means:**
- You use the exact door approach script
- You ask the exact needs audit questions
- You follow the solidification process
- You don't improvise or freestyle
- You understand why the system works before you modify it

**In practice:**
- Script is memorized and delivered naturally
- You know the "why" behind each question
- You follow the booking process without shortcuts
- You confirm address, both decision makers, and SDGE bill
- You hand off quality appointments to the closer

**Why it matters:**
- The system is proven (other reps use it successfully)
- Consistency compounds results
- Setters who follow system set more appointments
- Closers can coach you when process is consistent

---

### Norm 3: Get Both Decision Makers and the Bill

**What it means:**
- You don't book appointments without both spouses/partners
- You get the SDGE bill before the appointment (or ensure customer will have it)
- You don't make exceptions
- You reschedule if you have to

**In practice:**
- "Will both of you be here?" is non-negotiable
- SDGE bill is requested and secured
- If spouse isn't home, you reschedule for when they're available
- You explain why both decision makers matter
- You follow up on bill requests

**Why it matters:**
- Single decision maker appointments waste closer time
- Bills without information = inaccurate proposals
- This quality metric directly affects closer's close rate
- Professional setters don't book bad appointments

---

### Norm 4: Respect the Closer's Time

**What it means:**
- Your appointment is real (customer will show)
- Your appointment has both decision makers
- Your appointment has the SDGE bill
- Your customer isn't just "curious" - they're qualified
- You don't waste the closer's time with bad appointments

**In practice:**
- 80%+ show rate (if it's lower, you're overbooking)
- Customer knows what to expect (you explained it)
- Customer is interested (you qualified them)
- Decision makers are aligned (you confirmed it)
- Closer can trust that your appointment is solid

**Why it matters:**
- Closer's time is precious (they close deals)
- Bad appointments lower closer's close rate
- Respect from closer = respect for you
- Team only works if everyone respects each other's time

---

### Norm 5: Take Feedback Without Defensiveness

**What it means:**
- Your manager gives feedback - you listen without arguing
- Mentor shows you a better way - you try it
- You acknowledge when you're wrong
- You implement feedback quickly
- You report back on improvements

**In practice:**
- "Good catch" instead of defending
- Try the suggestion next door
- Follow up with results
- Ask clarifying questions, not defensive questions
- Actually change based on feedback

**Why it matters:**
- Your best growth comes from feedback
- Defensive people don't improve
- Coachable people become top performers
- Teams that embrace feedback outperform

---

### Norm 6: Support Your Teammates

**What it means:**
- You celebrate when someone sets a great appointment
- You help a struggling teammate
- You share techniques that work
- You don't compete - you collaborate
- You're part of a team, not a solo operator

**In practice:**
- "Nice job on that door" to a teammate
- Struggling to set appointments? Ask a peer for help
- Found a great talking point? Share it with team
- Peer sets an appointment? Genuinely happy for them
- Team win is your win

**Why it matters:**
- Teams outperform individuals
- Collaboration is faster than competition
- Your teammate's success helps the whole team
- High-performing teams have genuine camaraderie

---

### Norm 7: Represent KOTA with Professionalism

**What it means:**
- You dress professionally
- You're polite to customers
- You don't trash talk competitors
- You follow company branding
- You're a representative of KOTA quality

**In practice:**
- KOTA branded gear or professional dress (no holes, stains, offensive graphics)
- "Thanks for your time" to every door
- "They're good people, just different approach" about competitors
- You follow up when you say you will
- Customers think "KOTA is professional" based on you

**Why it matters:**
- First impression is critical
- You're the face of KOTA
- Professionalism builds trust
- Professional brand = more customers saying yes
- Your standards set neighborhood standards

---

### Norm 8: Own Your Results

**What it means:**
- You don't make excuses
- Bad week? Analyze what happened and fix it
- Good week? Understand what worked
- "Bad neighborhood" isn't an excuse
- "Busy day" doesn't explain poor performance

**In practice:**
- Miss appointment goal? "I need to adjust X and Y"
- Not getting bills? "Let me work on my bill request"
- Low show rate? "I'm not qualifying well enough"
- Great week? "Here's what I did differently"

**Why it matters:**
- Accountability drives improvement
- Excuses justify mediocrity
- Ownership attracts opportunity
- Leaders respect people who own results

---

### Norm 9: Never Stop Learning

**What it means:**
- You study the system
- You ask questions constantly
- You watch how others do it
- You read training materials
- You understand the "why" behind everything

**In practice:**
- Review script the night before
- Ask mentor "why that question?"
- Watch top performer's door technique
- Read overcoming objections playsets
- Understand how door leads to appointment leads to deal

**Why it matters:**
- Solar sales is a skill (not just knocking)
- Continuous learning compounds
- Curious people become excellent performers
- Understanding the system makes you better

---

### Norm 10: Celebrate Progress and Wins

**What it means:**
- First appointment set? Team celebrates it
- 10 appointments in a week? That's recognized
- Customer gives testimonial? That gets shared
- Team hits monthly goal? That gets celebrated
- You acknowledge your own progress

**In practice:**
- "Nice! First appointment set!" to new rep
- Monday meeting mentions great weeks from previous week
- Testimonials get shared with team
- Monthly achievements get announced
- You recognize personal improvement

**Why it matters:**
- Celebration builds momentum
- Recognition reinforces behavior
- Teams that celebrate outperform
- Your wins are team wins

---

## The Setter Team Agreement

When you join a setter team, you're committing to these norms. This isn't a list - it's an agreement:

1. I will show up every day, on time, ready to work
2. I will execute the system with precision
3. I will get both decision makers and the SDGE bill
4. I will respect the closer's time with quality appointments
5. I will take feedback without defensiveness
6. I will support my teammates
7. I will represent KOTA professionally
8. I will own my results
9. I will continuously learn
10. I will celebrate progress and wins

---

## The Culture This Creates

When a setter team lives these norms, something magical happens:

- **Appointment quality skyrockets** - Closers have easy appointments
- **Culture becomes legendary** - People want to join the team
- **Retention improves** - People stay because they love the culture
- **Success compounds** - Better culture = better results = better culture
- **Growth accelerates** - High-performing teams attract top talent
- **Everyone wins** - Better team performance = better individual income

---

## Your Role in Building Culture

You don't have to be a manager to influence culture. Every day you:
- Model the norms for newer reps
- Celebrate teammates' wins
- Take feedback gracefully
- Support struggling teammates
- Show up ready to work

That's culture-building.

---

## The Challenge: 30 Days of Living the Norms

**Week 1:** Review the 10 norms. Which 3 are your strongest? Which 3 need work?

**Week 2-4:** Pick one norm each week to focus on. Master it. Then move to the next.

By week 4, you'll see the difference in your performance and relationships.

## Final Thought

The best setter teams aren't the ones with the most talented people. They're the ones with the strongest culture.

Culture is built on norms - agreements about how you treat each other, how you work, and what you expect.

These norms are your foundation.

Live them, and you'll be part of something exceptional.
